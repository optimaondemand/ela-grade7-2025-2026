### Module 1: The Odyssey — Overview

In this module you will read Homer's *The Odyssey* — one of the oldest and most influential poems in any language — and argue whether its hero, Odysseus, deserves to be called a hero at all. You will study the poem's episodes (the Cyclops, the Underworld, the Sirens, the homecoming) through close reading, VR spatial composition, discussion, and short analytical writing, all of it building toward a 400–600 word analytical essay with a VR recording component.

**Primary text:** Homer, *The Odyssey* (Fagles translation, selected books — provided in Canvas)

## Quiz Questions (survey format — no correct answers)

**Q1 — Essential Question: First Response** (Essay question)
*Before you begin reading, write 2–3 sentences answering this question in your own words:*

"What makes someone a hero? Is it what they do, who they are, or how their story is told?"

There is no right answer. Write your honest first response. You will return to this question at the end of the module.

---

**Q2 — Objectives Acknowledgment** (Multiple choice — all correct)
*This module will ask you to:*

Select all that apply:
A) Read and analyze episodes from Homer's The Odyssey
B) Write a formal analytical essay with a clear thesis and specific evidence
C) Use ENGAGE VR to build spatial arguments about the text
D) Practice grammar techniques including parallel structure, anaphora, and periodic sentences

(All four are correct — this is an acknowledgment item.)

---

**Q3 — Group Work Opt-In** (Multiple choice)
