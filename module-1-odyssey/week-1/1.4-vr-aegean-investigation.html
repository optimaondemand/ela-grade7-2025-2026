The Odyssey — Week 1 — VR Activity 1

VR 1.4 — Aegean Setting Investigation

Reading a world for what it costs to live inside it

What You’re Doing

You are entering an ancient Mediterranean Location in ENGAGE to do what literary readers call “reading the setting.” In The Odyssey, setting is not background decoration — the sea, the islands, the gods’ domain, the mortal world — all of these are active forces that shape what characters can do and what they want. Your task is to observe this world carefully, annotate what you see, and begin building a spatial record of what Odysseus is up against. This session directly feeds your Week 1 Quick Write and your module project composition.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (recommended) or desktop ENGAGE client
  • ENGAGE account: logged in and linked to your class
  • Your copy of Books 1 and 5 (you read these before this session)
  • Estimated time: 25 minutes
How to Join
Session type:Persistent (the Location stays open; you enter alone)
Session ID:[Teacher: insert Session ID]
Location:Ancient Greece — Coastal Landscape (or closest available Mediterranean environment)
On arrival:Look for the Whiteboard IFX near the spawn point — it has your first instruction.
What to Do — In Order
1

Orient yourself. Walk or turn slowly to take in the whole Location. Notice the scale of the sea, the sky, the land. Do not start annotating yet — spend the first two minutes just looking.

2

Place four sticky notes. Each sticky note should name one feature of the setting and answer the question: What does this feature tell a traveler about what it costs to cross this world? Examples of features to annotate: the open sea, a rocky island, the sky/weather, the lack of landmarks. Write in full sentences on each sticky note.

3

Add a Whiteboard IFX. Open your menu, select Whiteboard IFX from the IFX library, and place it somewhere visible. On the whiteboard, write a two-sentence answer to this question: Why would a man who has been away for twenty years still want to cross this sea to get home? Connect your answer to something specific from Books 1 or 5.

4

Take a snapshot. Use the in-headset snapshot function (or the desktop screenshot tool) to capture your annotated space: four sticky notes visible and the Whiteboard IFX in frame. This is your submission artifact.

5

Before you leave: read back what you wrote on the Whiteboard IFX. If you can make it more specific — one more concrete detail from the text — revise it now. Then exit the Session.

What to Submit

One snapshot image showing your four sticky notes and Whiteboard IFX together in the frame. Upload the image file to the Canvas assignment submission box for VR 1.4. No written reflection required — the annotated image is the submission.

Grading: completion (10 pts) — if you did the activity and submitted a snapshot showing annotation, you receive full credit.

Estimated Time

25 minutes in-headset — 2 min orientation, 12 min sticky notes and whiteboard, 5 min revision, 6 min snapshot and exit.

Facilitator Note

Students are doing observational annotation in a Mediterranean coastal Location. Common sticking points: (1) Students try to summarize the plot on sticky notes instead of observing the setting — redirect them to what they see in the environment. (2) The Whiteboard IFX question asks for a text connection — remind students this is one sentence from Books 1 or 5, not a general statement. The snapshot submission should show all four sticky notes and the whiteboard in the same frame; if students submit a partial view, ask them to re-enter and retake.
