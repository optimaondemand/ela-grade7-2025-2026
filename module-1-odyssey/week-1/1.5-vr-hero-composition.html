7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 1 — VR Activity 2

VR 1.5 — “Who Is Odysseus?” Composition

Building your first spatial argument about a character

What You’re Doing

You are building a spatial composition in ENGAGE that argues, visually, who Odysseus is at the start of the poem — before his great trials, as we meet him in Books 1 and 5. This is not a decoration exercise; it is a visual interpretation backed by textual evidence. Every IFX you place and every sticky note you write should be connected to something specific in the text. By the end of this session you will have both a visual claim about Odysseus’s character and a narrated recording explaining it — which is the seed of your module project essay.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (recommended) or desktop ENGAGE client
  • Books 1 and 5 nearby for reference
  • Estimated time: 30 minutes
How to Join
Session type:Persistent
Session ID:[Teacher: insert Session ID]
Location:Ancient Greece — Coastal Landscape (same Location as VR 1.4, different area)
On arrival:You will have a clear space to build. A sticky note near the spawn point says: “This is your composition area.”
What to Do — In Order
1

Decide on your central claim. Before you place anything, answer this in your head: What is the most important thing to understand about Odysseus based on Books 1 and 5? Your composition will argue this claim visually.

2

Place 3–5 IFX objects from the ENGAGE IFX library that represent Odysseus’s situation, character, and values as they appear in the early books. Choose IFX thoughtfully — the shape, color, and scale of each object should support your claim. Arrange them in a way that creates a visual composition with a clear center or focal point.

3

Annotate with 3 sticky notes. Each sticky note must be attached to or placed near a specific IFX and must contain: (a) what this IFX represents about Odysseus, and (b) the specific detail from Books 1 or 5 that justifies your choice.

4

Record a spatial recording. Open the recording tool in ENGAGE and record a 1–2 minute narration walking through your composition. Narrate: (a) your central claim about Odysseus, (b) what each IFX represents, and (c) what specific moment from the text supports your reading. Speak in full sentences.

5

Copy the spatial recording link from ENGAGE (usually available in your recording history or sharing settings). Paste it into the Canvas text entry box for VR 1.5. Also upload a snapshot of your composition as a backup.

What to Submit

Two items: (1) the spatial recording link, pasted into the Canvas text entry box; (2) a snapshot of your composed scene with sticky notes visible, uploaded as an image file. If you cannot generate a spatial recording link, upload a video recording instead.

Grading: completion (10 pts) — if you submitted both items and the recording includes a narrated claim about Odysseus with at least one textual reference, you receive full credit.

Estimated Time

30 minutes — 3 min planning, 12 min IFX placement and sticky notes, 10 min recording and revision, 5 min snapshot and submission.

Facilitator Note

This is the first full composition session of the module — many students will not know what IFX to choose. Encourage them to pick objects that feel right first, then justify the choice with evidence. Common issue: students narrate a plot summary instead of a character claim. Prompt them: “What does this tell you about WHO Odysseus is, not just what happened?” The spatial recording link can be obtained from the ENGAGE recording dashboard; demonstrate this before students begin if working in a live session.
