7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 2 — VR Activity 1

VR 2.4 — Cyclops Cave Staging

Analyzing Odysseus’s plan where it happened

What You’re Doing

You are staging the Cyclops episode inside an ENGAGE cave Location to analyze exactly how Odysseus’s plan works — and exactly where his hubris breaks it. This is a scene-staging exercise: you place IFX to represent the key elements of the space and the key moments of the encounter, then annotate with your analysis. This session directly feeds the module project essay.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset or desktop ENGAGE client
  • Book 9 nearby for reference
  • Estimated time: 25 minutes
How to Join
Session type:Persistent
Session ID:[Teacher: insert Session ID]
Location:Cave or cavern-type ENGAGE Location
On arrival:Find the Whiteboard IFX at the spawn point listing your three staging zones.
What to Do
1

Stage Zone 1: The Cave. Place IFX to represent the Cyclops’s cave — its scale, the sheep, the blocked entrance. Stick a sticky note on the entrance IFX: “Why does Odysseus enter knowing it’s dangerous?” Answer in one sentence.

2

Stage Zone 2: The Plan. Place IFX representing the stake, the wine, and the “Nobody” name. Annotate with a sticky note on each: (a) what this element does in Odysseus’s plan, (b) why it is clever.

3

Stage Zone 3: The Boast. Place a single IFX representing the moment Odysseus shouts his name. Annotate: (a) Why does Odysseus do this? (b) What does it cost him? Connect to something specific in Book 9.

4

Take a snapshot showing all three zones and annotations in the frame. Submit to VR 2.4 in Canvas.

What to Submit

One snapshot image showing all three zones annotated. Upload to Canvas VR 2.4. Completion-graded (10 pts).

Estimated Time

25 minutes in-headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are staging a three-zone scene analysis. The most valuable annotation is Zone 3 (the boast) — encourage students to connect directly to a line from Book 9 rather than paraphrasing. If students can’t identify why Odysseus shouts his name, prompt: “What would have been lost if he stayed silent?”
