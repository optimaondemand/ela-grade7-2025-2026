7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 2 — VR Activity 2

VR 2.5 — Cunning vs. Strength Composition

Building a visual argument about how Odysseus solves problems

What You’re Doing

You are building two contrasting IFX scenes side by side: one representing a problem solved by strength alone, one representing the same or equivalent problem solved by cunning. The goal is to make a visual argument about what mētis does that brute force cannot. This composition will inform your module project essay’s central claim.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset or desktop ENGAGE client
  • Books 9–10 for reference
  • Estimated time: 30 minutes
How to Join
Session type:Persistent
Session ID:[Teacher: insert Session ID]
Location:Ancient Greece — Coastal Landscape or Cave Location
On arrival:Two marked composition zones are labeled “Strength” and “Cunning” with sticky notes.
What to Do
1

Build Zone 1 (Strength). Arrange 2–3 IFX objects representing a problem from Books 9–10 that cannot be solved by force — or showing what would happen if someone tried brute force and failed. Annotate with one sticky note explaining why strength is insufficient here.

2

Build Zone 2 (Cunning). Arrange 2–3 IFX objects showing Odysseus’s actual solution to the same or equivalent problem. Annotate with one sticky note: what specific steps does the cunning plan require that brute force does not?

3

Add a Whiteboard IFX between the two zones. Write a two-sentence summary: “Cunning requires ___. Strength requires ___.” Then add one more sentence: “Homer prefers ___ because ___.”

4

Record a 1–2 minute spatial recording walking through both zones and reading your Whiteboard IFX claim aloud. Explain what the contrast reveals about your central question: Is Odysseus’s cunning a virtue?

5

Submit the spatial recording link + one snapshot showing both zones. Paste link into Canvas text entry, upload snapshot as image.

What to Submit / Time / Grading

Spatial recording link + snapshot. 30 minutes. Completion-graded (10 pts).

Facilitator Note

The key challenge is the Whiteboard IFX claim — students often write “cunning is smarter than strength,” which is too vague. Push them toward a specific claim about what cunning requires (patience, knowledge of the enemy, self-control) that strength does not. This is the seed of their module project thesis.
