Quick Write 2 — When Does Cleverness Become Cruelty?

Connecting Odysseus’s cunning to the module’s central question

The Prompt
Based on Books 9–10 of The Odyssey, write one paragraph (5–7 sentences) responding to this question: When does Odysseus’s cleverness become cruelty? Your paragraph should name a specific moment from Books 9–10 where his plan works but causes suffering — either to himself, to his men, or to someone else — and argue whether that suffering was necessary or avoidable.

This connects to the module’s central question: Is Odysseus’s cunning a virtue? You don’t have to answer that question completely yet — your job is to notice the tension and take a position on one specific moment.

What a Strong Paragraph Does
  • Names a specific moment from Books 9–10 (not a general description of Odysseus’s character)
  • Describes what Odysseus gains from his plan
  • Describes what it costs — someone suffers, something is lost
  • Takes a position: was that cost necessary or avoidable?
  • Ends with a sentence that connects to the question of whether cunning is a virtue
A Note on the Boast

If you’re writing about the Cyclops episode, the most interesting moment is not the blinding — it’s the boast. Odysseus blinded Polyphemus under the name “Nobody,” which protected him. Then he shouted his real name across the water. What does that moment tell you about the limits of mētis? Can cunning coexist with the need for recognition?

How to Submit
