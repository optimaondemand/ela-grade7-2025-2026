# Week 2 Quiz — The Odyssey, Books 9–10
## Canvas Quiz | 10 questions | Auto-graded | 50 pts (5 pts each)

---

**Q1.** When Odysseus and his men arrive at the Cyclops's island, Odysseus insists on entering the cave even after his men urge him to leave. What is his stated reason for staying?

A) He wants to steal the Cyclops's sheep  
B) He hopes the Cyclops will offer them gifts as a host should  
C) He is afraid of being seen by the Cyclops outside  
D) He wants to prove his courage to his men  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** Odysseus tells Polyphemus his name is "Nobody." What is the immediate tactical benefit of this lie?

A) It prevents Polyphemus from telling his father Poseidon who blinded him  
B) When Polyphemus cries out in pain, the other Cyclopes assume no one is hurting him  
C) It confuses Polyphemus so he drinks more wine  
D) It makes Polyphemus believe Odysseus is a god  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q3.** After escaping the Cyclops's cave, Odysseus shouts his real name across the water. What does this boast cost him?

A) His ship is sunk by a boulder Polyphemus throws  
B) His men mutiny and take control of the ship  
C) Poseidon, hearing his son's prayer, becomes a permanent enemy of Odysseus  
D) Athena withdraws her protection for the rest of the journey  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** The word *hubris* in ancient Greek refers to:

A) The wisdom that comes from suffering  
B) Excessive pride that leads to reckless or disrespectful behavior  
C) The loyalty a hero owes to his crew  
D) A trick or cunning plan  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q5.** In Book 9, Odysseus deliberately chooses a large olive stake to use against the Cyclops. Why does he sharpen it and hide it rather than use it immediately?

A) He must wait until Polyphemus is asleep so he can use it without being caught  
B) He is afraid the stake isn't sharp enough yet  
C) He plans to use it as a weapon to escape during the day  
D) He wants to leave it as evidence of who blinded the Cyclops  

**Answer: A**

---

**Q6.** *Xenia* was the ancient Greek principle of guest-friendship. By killing and eating Odysseus's men, Polyphemus violates this principle. Which detail from Book 9 most directly shows that Polyphemus operates outside Greek civilization's norms?

A) He lives alone in a cave rather than in a city  
B) Homer tells us the Cyclopes have no laws, no assemblies, and no council  
C) He speaks a different language than Odysseus  
D) He is the son of Poseidon  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** In Book 10, Circe turns Odysseus's scouting party into pigs. What does Odysseus use to resist her magic when he goes to confront her?

A) A prayer to Athena  
B) His sword alone  
C) The herb *moly*, given to him by Hermes  
D) A piece of olive wood from the Cyclops's cave  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** After Circe restores Odysseus's men, she keeps Odysseus on her island for a full year. What eventually prompts Odysseus to ask to leave?

A) Athena appears and commands him to go  
B) His men remind him that he has been away from home long enough  
C) Circe warns him that Poseidon is approaching  
D) A messenger from Ithaca arrives with news of Penelope  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q9.** Which phrase best describes the relationship between *mêtis* (cunning intelligence) and physical strength in Books 9–10?

A) Odysseus uses strength first and cunning only when strength fails  
B) Cunning replaces the need for strength entirely in both episodes  
C) Cunning designs the plan while strength executes key parts of it  
D) Physical strength is more important than cunning in the Circe episode  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** At the end of Book 10, Circe tells Odysseus he must travel to the Underworld before he can return home. Why does Odysseus need to visit the Underworld?

A) He must make a sacrifice to Poseidon to end the god's anger  
B) He must speak with the prophet Tiresias to learn how to reach Ithaca safely  
C) He must retrieve his crewman Elpenor, who fell from Circe's roof  
D) He must pay a debt to Persephone for using Circe's power  

**Answer: B**
