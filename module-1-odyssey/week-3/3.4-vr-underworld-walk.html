7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 3 — VR Activity 1

VR 3.4 — Underworld Annotation Walk

Meeting the shades — placing the encounters that matter

What You’re Doing

You are building an annotated map of Odysseus’s Nekuia — his journey to the Underworld in Book 11. Inside a dark or cave-type ENGAGE Location, you will place IFX to represent the three most important encounters Odysseus has with the dead, annotate each with a sticky note connecting the encounter to the module’s central question, and record a short spatial recording walking through your annotations. This session directly builds evidence for your module project essay.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset or desktop ENGAGE client
  • Book 11 nearby for reference
  • Estimated time: 25 minutes
How to Join
Session type:Persistent
Session ID:[Teacher: insert Session ID]
Location:Dark cave or underworld-type ENGAGE Location
On arrival:Find the Whiteboard IFX at the spawn point listing your three encounter zones.
What to Do
1

Place Encounter 1: Tiresias. Choose 1–2 IFX representing the prophet. Add a sticky note: What does Tiresias tell Odysseus, and what does it reveal about the cost of hubris? Quote or closely paraphrase one detail from Book 11.

2

Place Encounter 2: Anticleia. Choose 1 IFX representing Odysseus’s mother. Add a sticky note: What does her death tell us about the cost of Odysseus’s journey — not to himself, but to the people he left behind?

3

Place Encounter 3: Achilles. Choose 1 IFX. Add a sticky note: In one sentence, state what Achilles’s words imply about whether heroic glory is worth its price. Use the phrase “Although Achilles…” to practice your concessive clause.

4

Record a 1–2 minute spatial recording walking through all three encounters. At each stop, read your sticky note aloud and add one spoken sentence connecting that encounter to the module question: Is Odysseus’s cunning a virtue?

What to Submit / Time / Grading

Spatial recording link + one snapshot showing all three zones. 25 minutes. Completion-graded (10 pts).

Facilitator Note

The most analytically rich encounter is Achilles — encourage students who finish early to expand that sticky note with a specific line from Book 11. Students who struggle to connect the Underworld encounters to the module question can be prompted: “Does what Odysseus learns here change how he travels? If not, why not?” That gap — between knowledge and behavior — is the heart of the hubris argument.
