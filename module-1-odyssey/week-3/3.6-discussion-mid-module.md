# Mid-Module Discussion — The Odyssey
## Canvas Discussion | Week 3 | Tier 2 | 15 pts

---

## Purpose

This is the module's only graded discussion. It sits at the midpoint — after students have read Books 1–12 and completed six VR sessions — so they have enough textual evidence to argue, not just react.

---

## Prompt (post to Canvas Discussion exactly as written)

**Post your response first, then reply to one classmate.**

---

Homer gives Odysseus two qualities that seem to pull against each other: *mêtis* (cunning, practical intelligence) and a pride so powerful that he shouts his name to the Cyclops and invites ten more years of suffering.

**Take a position:** Is Odysseus's cunning a virtue, or does his pride make it impossible for cunning to be virtuous? Use **one specific scene from Books 1–12** as your primary evidence. Your post should:

1. State your position clearly in your first sentence.
2. Name the specific scene and explain what it shows.
3. Acknowledge what the opposing view would say — and answer it.

**Then reply to one classmate** whose position differs from yours. Do not just say you agree or disagree — identify the specific piece of evidence they used and explain why you read it differently.

---

## Rubric

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **15 pts** | Takes a clear position in sentence 1. Names a specific scene with accurate detail. Acknowledges and answers the opposing view. Reply engages the classmate's specific evidence. |
| **8 pts** | Position present but vague, OR scene is named without explanation, OR reply is general ("I agree/disagree") rather than evidence-specific. |
| **0 pts** | Not submitted, or post is fewer than 3 sentences with no engagement with the text. |

---

## Canvas Setup Notes (Teacher)

- Create as a **Canvas Discussion** — graded, 15 pts
- **Require a post before seeing replies** (prevents copying)
- Attach this rubric directly to the discussion via Canvas Rubric tool
- Add to **Module 1 → Week 3** in Canvas module order, after VR 3.5
- Due: end of Week 3 (teacher sets exact date)
- Allow responses through end of Week 4 so students in different time zones can engage

---

## Teacher Facilitation Note

Read posts in one batch. Drop 2–3 public replies in the thread: one modeling a stronger concessive move ("What would you say to someone who argued that the Polyphemus episode actually *proves* cunning is a virtue — that the boast is hubris, not cunning?"), one affirming a student who made a strong evidence choice, one pushing back gently on a reading that misrepresents the text. Total facilitator time: ~20 minutes.
