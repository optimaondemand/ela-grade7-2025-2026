# Week 3 Quiz — The Odyssey, Books 11–12
## Canvas Quiz | 10 questions | Auto-graded | 50 pts (5 pts each)

---

**Q1.** In Book 11, Odysseus visits the Underworld using a technique called the Nekuia. What does he do to allow the dead to speak to him?

A) He descends into Hades through a cave  
B) He pours blood into a trench, which the shades drink to temporarily regain speech  
C) He calls upon Hermes to carry his message to the dead  
D) He burns offerings on an altar and reads the smoke  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** Tiresias gives Odysseus specific instructions for reaching home safely. Which warning is most directly connected to events that happen later in the poem?

A) Avoid the island of the Phaeacians  
B) Do not harm the cattle of the sun god Helios  
C) Keep your men away from the land of the Lotus-Eaters  
D) Never speak your name aloud at sea  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q3.** When Odysseus tries to embrace his mother Anticleia, what happens?

A) She turns away from him in anger  
B) She is able to embrace him, but grows cold and fades  
C) His arms close on air — her shade cannot be held  
D) She does not recognize him  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** Achilles tells Odysseus in the Underworld that he would rather be the poorest living man than king among the dead. What does this reveal about Homer's view of heroic glory?

A) Glory in battle is the highest thing a man can achieve  
B) Even the greatest heroic achievement cannot compensate for the loss of life itself  
C) Achilles regrets choosing glory because he was tricked by the gods  
D) The Underworld is a punishment for heroes who die in battle  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q5.** The term *katabasis* refers to:

A) A ritual prayer to the dead  
B) A living hero's descent into the underworld  
C) A battle between a hero and a monster  
D) The return voyage of an epic hero  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q6.** In Book 12, the Sirens are dangerous because:

A) They attack ships with rocks thrown from the cliffs  
B) Their singing lures sailors to wreck their ships on the rocks trying to reach them  
C) They poison the water around their island  
D) They can take the form of people the sailors love  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** Odysseus wants to hear the Sirens' song but survive it. What does he do?

A) He stuffs his own ears with wax and ties his men to the mast  
B) He ties himself to the mast and puts wax in his men's ears  
C) He has his men row as fast as possible while he covers his ears  
D) He asks Circe for a charm that makes the song inaudible  

**Answer: B**

---

**Q8.** When navigating between Scylla and Charybdis, Odysseus follows Circe's advice and steers toward Scylla. What is his reasoning?

A) Scylla is afraid of fire, and he has a torch  
B) Charybdis is on Poseidon's side, so Scylla is the safer option  
C) Losing six men to Scylla is survivable; Charybdis could destroy the whole ship  
D) Scylla is slower than Charybdis, giving the crew time to escape  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** Which sentence best uses a concessive clause to describe Odysseus's decision at Scylla and Charybdis?

A) Odysseus chose Scylla because it was the safest option available.  
B) Odysseus had no choice; both monsters were equally dangerous.  
C) Although steering toward Scylla meant six men would certainly die, Odysseus judged it the only decision that preserved the ship and the rest of the crew.  
D) Odysseus should have found another way through because no good leader sacrifices his men.  

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** After leaving the Underworld, what does Odysseus do with the information he received from Tiresias?

A) He immediately changes course to avoid all the dangers the prophet named  
B) He shares Tiresias's full warning with his crew so they can vote on the route  
C) He keeps most of the information from his men but warns them about the cattle of Helios  
D) He writes down Tiresias's words on a piece of wood and keeps it on the ship  

**Answer: C**
