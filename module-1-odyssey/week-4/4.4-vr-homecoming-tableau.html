7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 4 — VR Activity 1

VR 4.4 — The Homecoming Tableau

Stage the recognition scenes — who knows Odysseus, and what does that knowledge cost them?

What You’re Doing

Homer structures the homecoming as a series of recognition scenes — each character who sees through Odysseus’s disguise reveals something about the nature of loyalty. In this session you will stage three of those scenes using IFX in the Persistent Session, creating a spatial record of who recognizes Odysseus and what the moment costs each of them. You will annotate each zone with a sticky note that names what kind of loyalty the scene depicts, then take a snapshot of your full tableau for submission.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required — IFX placement and spatial recording are headset-only features)
  • Session type: Persistent Session
  • Session ID: ELA7-M1-W4
  • Location: Ancient Greek Villa
  • Spawn point: Main courtyard — look for the welcome sticky note at arrival
  • Pre-work: Read the Lesson 4.1 notes on recognition scenes before you enter VR
How to Join
1. Open ENGAGE on your headset.
2. Select Join a Session and enter Session ID: ELA7-M1-W4.
3. Location: Ancient Greek Villa.
4. Spawn at the main courtyard. A sticky note there will orient you.
What to Do
1

Orient yourself at spawn

Read the sticky note at the courtyard spawn point. It marks three zones labeled A (near the well), B (near the threshold), and C (near the far wall). Each zone is where you will stage one recognition scene.

2

Stage Zone A — Argos

In Zone A (near the well), place a Dog IFX and a figure IFX representing the disguised Odysseus. Scale the dog small — old, neglected. Place a sticky note labeled Zone A that names what this recognition scene reveals about loyalty. Your note must be one complete sentence.

3

Stage Zone B — Eurycleia

In Zone B (at the threshold), place two figure IFX — one seated (Eurycleia washing feet) and one standing (the disguised Odysseus). Place a Torch IFX nearby to suggest the intimate, low-lit scene. Add a sticky note labeled Zone B with a sentence naming what this scene reveals about loyalty — specifically what Eurycleia’s recognition is based on and why that matters.

4

Stage Zone C — Penelope and the bow

In Zone C (far wall), place a Bow IFX or an archery-related object, a figure IFX for Penelope, and one for the disguised Odysseus nearby. Add a sticky note labeled Zone C with a sentence that addresses the ambiguity: does Penelope half-know who the beggar is before the official recognition? Your note should take a position and name the evidence you are drawing on.

5

Capture your tableau

Step back to a position where all three zones are visible in one view. Take a snapshot inside ENGAGE that captures all three zones and their sticky notes. This is your submission artifact.

What to Submit

Upload your snapshot image to this Canvas assignment. Your snapshot must show all three zones and all three sticky notes. If your headset does not support snapshot export, take a photo of your screen from outside the headset — what matters is that all three annotations are legible.

Points: 10 pts — completion credit (all three zones staged and annotated, snapshot submitted).

Estimated Time

25 minutes in-headset. The three zones are already marked at spawn so orientation is fast; most of your time goes to choosing the right IFX and drafting the sticky note sentences.

Facilitator Note

Students are staging three recognition scenes as a spatial argument about loyalty. The main creative challenge is Zone C (Penelope) — students who haven’t finished the reading may not feel confident taking a position on her awareness. Encourage them to pick a side and defend it with one piece of textual evidence in the sticky note; the point is not to be right but to make an interpretive claim. Watch for students who write vague sticky notes like “she knows” without explanation — prompt them to add a “because”. Good IFX choices for this Location: figure IFX, torch, dog, bow, amphora.
