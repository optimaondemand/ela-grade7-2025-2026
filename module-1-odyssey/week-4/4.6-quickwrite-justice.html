7th Grade ELA — Module 1
The Odyssey — Week 4 — Quick Write

Quick Write 4.6 — Justice or Revenge?

One concessive sentence — grant the best point against your own position

The Prompt
Is the slaughter of the suitors in Book 22 justice or revenge?

Take a clear position in your first sentence. Then write one concessive sentence that begins with “Although…” and grants the strongest point on the other side. Your concessive sentence must acknowledge something real — not a weak or obvious objection, but the best argument your opponent could make.

Model (do not copy — write your own): “The slaughter of the suitors is justice, not revenge, because Odysseus acted to restore an order the suitors themselves violated. Although 108 men is a staggering number to kill in a locked hall, and some of them were young men following the lead of older ones who made the real decisions, the Greek concept of dike does not require proportionality in the modern legal sense — it requires that the proper order of things be restored, and a half-measure would have left the violation unresolved.”

Grammar Reminder

A concessive sentence grants something real to the opposing view before pivoting back to your own. The structure is:

Although [the strongest true point on the other side], [your claim stands because…].

What makes a concession strong: the “although” clause should make the reader genuinely pause — it concedes something worth conceding, not something trivial. If the objection you grant is too easy to dismiss, the sentence has no weight.

Submission

Write directly in the Canvas text box below. You need at minimum two sentences: your position statement and your concessive sentence. You may write more if the concession needs further development, but keep the response focused — three to four sentences is plenty.

Points: 5 pts — completion credit. Submit something genuine and you have full credit. Your teacher will not write individual feedback on Quick Writes, but strong responses may be shared (anonymously) with the class as models.
