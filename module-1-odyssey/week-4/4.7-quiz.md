# Week 4 Quiz: The Homecoming (Books 13-24)

**Q1.** When Odysseus returns to Ithaca, Athena disguises him as which of the following?

A) A traveling merchant
B) An old beggar
C) A Phaeacian sailor
D) A shepherd from the hills

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** Which character recognizes Odysseus first when he returns home?

A) Penelope
B) Telemachus
C) Argos the dog
D) Eurycleia the nurse

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** Eurycleia recognizes Odysseus by:

A) His voice when he speaks to her alone
B) A scar on his thigh from a childhood boar hunt
C) The way he holds his bow
D) A birthmark on his shoulder

**Answer: B**

---

**Q4.** The Greek term *anagnorisis* means:

A) The restoration of proper order after a violation
B) A descent into the underworld
C) A moment of recognition or discovery, especially of a character's true identity
D) The repetition of a word at the beginning of successive clauses

**Answer: C**

---

**Q5.** Penelope devises the contest of the bow because:

A) She believes none of the suitors will succeed and wants to humiliate them
B) She has been instructed to do so by Athena in a dream
C) Only Odysseus could string and shoot the bow through twelve axe handles
D) Telemachus tells her it is time to choose a new husband

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** After Odysseus wins the contest of the bow, he turns his arrows on the suitors. Homer describes this as an act of *dike*, which means:

A) Vengeance taken out of personal anger
B) The proper restoration of order that the suitors violated
C) Divine punishment sent directly by the gods
D) A legal judgment passed by a community of elders

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** Which of the following best describes why some modern readers find the slaughter of the suitors in Book 22 troubling, even if Greek audiences did not?

A) Modern readers believe Penelope should have been consulted first
B) Modern readers think Telemachus should have done the killing instead
C) Modern values often emphasize proportionality and legal process, which the slaughter does not follow
D) Modern readers do not believe the suitors actually violated *xenia*

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** Which sentence uses anaphora correctly?

A) The suitors were greedy, arrogant, and showed no respect for the household.
B) He waited for justice. He waited for his wife. He waited for the moment the gods would allow.
C) Although the suitors were wrong, some of them were young and may not have understood the violation.
D) Penelope wove patiently, intelligently, and with great loyalty.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q9.** Which sentence uses parallel structure correctly?

A) Odysseus was cunning, he was brave, and showed great patience.
B) Penelope was intelligent, she was loyal, and had a talent for waiting.
C) The suitors were arrogant, greedy, and contemptuous of their host.
D) Odysseus wanted to return home, to see his son, and finding Penelope faithful.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** The recognition scenes in Books 13-22 sort the household before Odysseus acts by showing:

A) Which characters have been disloyal so they can be punished along with the suitors
B) Which characters have remained loyal across twenty years of absence without being told to
C) Which characters Athena has protected versus abandoned
D) Which characters Telemachus has already informed about Odysseus's return

**Answer: B**
