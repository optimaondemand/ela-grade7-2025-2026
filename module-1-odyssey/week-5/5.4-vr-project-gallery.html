The Odyssey — Week 5 — VR Activity 1

VR 5.4 — The Module Gallery Walk

Walk your classmates’ compositions and write a response to one

What You’re Doing

Your Evidence Composition from VR 4.5 is now part of a persistent gallery that all students in the course have contributed to. In this session you will walk the gallery, look at two other students’ compositions, and write a formal response to one of them on a Reply Whiteboard IFX adjacent to their work. The response must name what claim the other student is making, identify their strongest piece of evidence, and then agree, disagree, or complicate their claim — with one piece of your own evidence. This is the VR equivalent of a written peer response, and it will help you sharpen your own project argument.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (recommended) or desktop ENGAGE (gallery walk works on desktop)
  • Session type: Persistent Session (your Evidence Composition is already there)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M1-W4
  • Location: Ancient Greek Villa
  • Spawn point: Gallery entrance — look for the sticky note labeled “Gallery Walk Start” near the inner wall
How to Join
1. Open ENGAGE on your headset or desktop.
2. Select Join a Session and enter Session ID: ELA7-M1-W4.
3. Location: Ancient Greek Villa.
4. Spawn at the gallery entrance (near the inner wall). The gallery is the row of Evidence Compositions your class has built across VR 4.5.
What to Do
1

Walk the gallery and read two compositions

Move through the gallery and read the Whiteboard IFX claims and evidence sticky notes in at least two other students’ compositions. Read the claim first, then examine the evidence zones. What is each student arguing? What is their strongest piece of evidence?

2

Choose one composition to respond to

Select the composition you find most interesting — either because you agree strongly, disagree, or find their evidence does something unexpected. You do not need to respond to the one you agree with most. A composition that challenges your thinking is worth more to your project.

3

Write your response on the Reply Whiteboard IFX

Each composition has a blank Whiteboard IFX placed next to it labeled “Reply Here.” Write your response on that board. Your response must include three things: (1) a one-sentence summary of the other student’s claim, (2) a sentence naming their strongest evidence and why it is strong, and (3) your own response — either “This supports my claim because…” or “I read this evidence differently because…” and a counter-claim with one piece of evidence. Sign your reply with your first name.

What to Submit

Take a snapshot of the composition you responded to, showing both the original Whiteboard IFX and your Reply Whiteboard IFX. Submit the snapshot to this Canvas assignment. If desktop ENGAGE does not support snapshot export, type your response text (what you wrote on the Reply Whiteboard) into the Canvas text box instead.

Points: 10 pts — completion credit (gallery walked, reply written with all three components, snapshot or text submitted).

Estimated Time

20–25 minutes. Most of this time is reading and thinking — the actual writing on the Reply Whiteboard takes 5–10 minutes if you have read carefully first.

Facilitator Note

This is the module’s peer-review mechanism. Because the Session is persistent, students who enter at different times will find the gallery populated with varying numbers of compositions — students who enter early may find only a few; students who enter late will find a full gallery. That is fine: the gallery builds over the course of the week. The Reply Whiteboard IFX is pre-placed next to each Evidence Composition by the teacher before the week begins. Students who cannot find a Reply board should place their own Whiteboard IFX next to the composition they chose and label it with their name. The main sticking point is the third component (the student’s own counter-response) — students who agree with everything they read may need a prompt: “What would someone who disagreed with this argument say? Can you make that objection on their behalf?”
