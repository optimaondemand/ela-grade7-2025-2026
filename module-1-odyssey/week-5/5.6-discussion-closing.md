# Closing Discussion: Is Odysseus a Hero?

## Canvas Discussion Setup

**Title:** Closing Discussion: Is Odysseus a Hero?
**Points:** 15
**Type:** Threaded, require initial post before viewing replies
**Grading type:** Points
**Rubric:** 15 = clear position with parallel-structure thesis + two specific scenes + substantive reply that engages the other student's actual claim. 8 = vague claim or generic reply. 0 = not submitted.

---

## Discussion Prompt (paste into Canvas message field)

The module opened with a question: is Odysseus a hero? You have now read the full poem, argued in VR, responded to a classmate's composition, and written your own analysis. Answer the question now — not with the first thing that came to mind in Week 1, but with the argument you have built across five weeks of work.

**Your post must do three things:**

1. **State your final claim in one sentence.** Use parallel structure. Name at least two specific qualities — one that supports admiring Odysseus and one that complicates it (or one that explains how both qualities are related).

2. **Support your claim with two specific scenes** — one from the voyage (Books 9–12) and one from the homecoming (Books 13–24). For each scene, say what it shows and connect it explicitly to your claim.

3. **Acknowledge the strongest objection to your position** in a concessive sentence beginning with "Although..." — then answer it.

**Then reply to one classmate** whose claim differs from yours. Your reply must:
- Name their specific claim (not just "I agree/disagree")
- Identify which of their two scenes you find most persuasive and explain why
- Say where your reading differs, with a specific reason

---

## Rubric Detail

**15 points (full credit):**
- Parallel-structure thesis names two specific qualities
- Two scenes named with textual specificity and connection to claim
- Concessive sentence is genuine (grants something real, not trivial)
- Reply names the classmate's claim and engages a specific scene

**8 points (partial credit):**
- Claim is present but vague or lacks parallel structure
- Only one scene, or scenes are named without textual detail
- Reply is generic ("I agree with your point about Odysseus") without engaging the actual argument

**0 points:** Not submitted.

---

## Note for Canvas Setup

This is the module's second and final Tier 2 discussion. It is the only place students are asked to integrate the full arc of the module's reading and argument. Teacher review: batch-score in SpeedGrader (~30 seconds per post) using the 15/8/0 rubric. Leave 2–3 public comments on posts that model especially strong parallel-structure theses or strong concessive sentences — these serve as visible feedback for the cohort without requiring individual comments.
