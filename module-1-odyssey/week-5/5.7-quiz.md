# Week 5 Quiz: The Poem as a Whole

**Q1.** The term "narrative irony" refers to:

A) When a character says something sarcastic to another character
B) The gap between what a narrator claims and what the story's events actually suggest
C) A plot twist that surprises the reader at the end
D) When the same event is narrated twice from different points of view

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** In the context of Homeric poetry, an epithet is:

A) A moral lesson stated directly at the end of the poem
B) A fixed descriptive phrase attached to a name and repeated for metrical and characterizing purposes
C) A speech given by the hero to explain his own qualities
D) A comparison between two unlike things using "like" or "as"

**Answer: B**

---

**Q3.** Which of the following is the best example of a periodic sentence?

A) Odysseus is cunning, brave, and capable of surviving any crisis.
B) Odysseus survived the Cyclops, having blinded him and escaped beneath the sheep.
C) Although he outwitted every monster and god who stood in his way, Odysseus lost every single one of his men.
D) The suitors were arrogant, greedy, and contemptuous of their host.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** A cumulative sentence differs from a periodic sentence in that a cumulative sentence:

A) States the main clause first and adds elaboration after it
B) Withholds the main clause until the very end
C) Uses anaphora to repeat an opening phrase
D) Presents a claim and then immediately concedes the opposing view

**Answer: A**

---

**Q5.** Which of the following best explains why narrative irony is present in The Odyssey?

A) Homer writes with a sarcastic tone throughout the poem
B) Odysseus often lies to the other characters about who he is
C) The narrator calls Odysseus "great-hearted" and "wily" at moments when his behavior raises real questions about those descriptions
D) The poem ends before Odysseus can fully prove he is a hero

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** Which of the following passages uses sentence variety most effectively?

A) Odysseus is smart. He is also brave. He is also proud. He is also cunning.
B) Odysseus is smart. He is smart in the Cyclops cave. He is smart with Circe. He is smart everywhere.
C) Odysseus deploys his cunning in every crisis the poem sets before him, out-thinking the Cyclops, resisting Circe, and navigating the Underworld. But cunning costs him. Every time his pride overrides his intelligence, his men pay.
D) Odysseus is a cunning character who uses his intelligence to survive and also he is proud and this causes problems for him sometimes.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q7.** Which of the following sentences correctly uses anaphora?

A) Odysseus is admirable for his intelligence, troubling for his pride, and fascinating for how both qualities depend on each other.
B) He was cunning in the Cyclops cave. He was cunning with Circe. He was cunning even in his own house, playing the beggar who already knew he would win.
C) Although Odysseus lost all his men, he eventually returned home and reunited with Penelope.
D) Penelope waited patiently, weaving and unweaving the same shroud for three years.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q8.** The opening line of The Odyssey promises a hero who is "never at a loss." By the end of the poem, a careful reader might notice this is:

A) Fully accurate -- Odysseus solves every problem he encounters without difficulty
B) Partially accurate -- Odysseus solves most problems but loses his men because of bad luck
C) An example of narrative irony -- Odysseus is at a loss repeatedly, especially when pride defeats intelligence
D) Irrelevant -- the opening line is a formulaic convention with no characterizing meaning

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** Which of the following is the best parallel-structure thesis about Odysseus?

A) Odysseus is admirable because he is smart and also he is troubling because he is proud.
B) Odysseus is admirable for his intelligence, troubling for his pride, and most fascinating for the way neither quality can exist without the other.
C) Odysseus uses cunning to solve problems but his pride creates more problems so it is hard to say if he is a hero.
D) The Odyssey shows that Odysseus is both good and bad in different ways depending on the situation.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q10.** In the context of the module's central question, which of the following best describes what "virtue" means in the classical sense?

A) Following rules and never breaking them
B) A quality of excellence that enables a person to function well and fulfill their proper role
C) Being humble and refusing to boast about your accomplishments
D) Obeying the commands of the gods without question

**Answer: B**
