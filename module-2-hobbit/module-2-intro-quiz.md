The central question that drives this module: <strong>Is heroism something you are, or something you become?</strong> By the end of the module, you will have read the full novel, staged its key scenes in VR, practiced ten grammar structures from Tolkien's prose, and written a comparison essay arguing what distinguishes Bilbo's heroism from Odysseus's. The module project is a 400–600 word comparison essay with a parallel-structure thesis and a VR spatial recording component, due at the end of Week 5.</p>

## Questions (5 questions — survey mode, no correct answer)

**Question 1** (Essay)
This module asks: Is heroism something you are, or something you become? What is your first instinct? Write 2–3 sentences explaining your initial answer before you begin reading.

**Question 2** (Multiple Choice — ungraded)
How would you describe your experience with *The Hobbit* before this module?
- I've never read it or seen any adaptation of it
- I've seen the films but not read the book
- I've read the book at least once before
- I've read the book and know it well

**Question 3** (Essay)
Which grammar structure from the list below are you most confident using in your own writing right now? Which one do you want to understand better by the end of this module?
*(Compound sentences, semicolons, active/passive voice, em dash, introductory phrases, parallel structure, complex sentences, periodic sentences, cumulative sentences)*

**Question 4** (Multiple Choice — group work opt-in)
For the module project, will you be completing the essay independently or with a group partner?
- Independently
- With a partner (you and your partner will submit one shared essay)

**Question 5** (Essay)
If you have a partner: write their name here. If you are working independently: write "independent." Then write one sentence about what you already think is most interesting about *The Hobbit* or Tolkien, even if it's just a question.
