## Questions (5 questions — survey mode, no correct answer)

**Question 1** (Essay — the essential question revisited)
At the start of this module you answered: "Is heroism something you are, or something you become?" Now that you have read the full novel, staged Bilbo's key moments in VR, and written the comparison essay, answer again. Has your position changed? What specific moment in the novel or the module most changed or confirmed your thinking?

*Write 3–5 sentences. Compare your answer here to what you wrote at the start of the module.*

**Question 2** (Likert scale — confidence rating)
How confident are you that you understand the central argument of your Module 2 Project essay?
- 5 — Very confident: I know exactly what I argued and why
- 4 — Confident: I have a clear thesis and good evidence
- 3 — Somewhat confident: I have a position but I'm not sure I proved it
- 2 — Not very confident: I'm not sure my essay argument is strong
- 1 — Not confident: I don't feel like I understood the comparison well

**Question 3** (Essay — what remains unclear)
Name one thing from this module that you still don't fully understand — a concept, a grammar structure, a passage from the novel, or an idea from the comparison. Be specific. "I don't understand everything" is not useful; "I don't understand why the Arkenstone matters more than Thorin's other treasure" is.

*This is the most important question on this form. Your teacher reads every response.*

**Question 4** (Multiple Choice — grammar self-assessment)
Which grammar structure from Module 2 do you feel you have mastered well enough to use in your own writing without being reminded?
- Compound sentences (FANBOYS)
- Semicolons
- Active vs. passive voice
- The em dash
- Introductory phrases (prepositional, participial, adverbial)
- Parallel structure
- Complex sentences with subordinating conjunctions
- Periodic sentences
- Cumulative sentences
- I'm not confident in any of them yet

**Question 5** (Essay — virtue reflection)
The seven Optima virtues are: courage, courtesy, honesty, perseverance, self-government, service, and responsibility. Which one did this module ask the most from you, personally — not Bilbo, but you as a student? Name the virtue and write 2–3 sentences explaining why.
