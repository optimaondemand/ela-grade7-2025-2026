Module 2 Project: Two Heroes, Two Journeys

A comparison essay — Bilbo Baggins and Odysseus — 400–600 words + VR component

The Question
Bilbo Baggins and Odysseus both leave home, face danger, and return changed. Are they the same kind of hero — or is what each novel calls “heroism” fundamentally different? What does the comparison reveal about what Tolkien values that Homer does not (or vice versa)?
What You’re Arguing

You have been building this argument all module. Your VR sessions have staged Bilbo’s key moments; your 5.5 spatial recording is your spoken rough draft; your closing discussion post is your preliminary argument. Now you are writing it. A comparison essay does not say “these heroes are similar and different” — it uses the comparison to make a specific claim about what the two works value. Your argument should illuminate something about one or both texts that a reader who had not compared them would miss.

Requirements
1

A parallel-structure thesis in your opening paragraph

Your thesis must name the central difference between the two heroes and use parallel grammatical form. It should not say “Bilbo and Odysseus are similar but different.” It should say what the difference means.

Example form: “While Odysseus returns to claim the identity he already had, Bilbo returns having become someone the Shire cannot quite recognize — and Tolkien argues that this invisible transformation is the harder kind of heroism.”
2

Two body paragraphs — one per hero

Each paragraph must cite one specific scene from the relevant text, describe what happens in the scene in detail, and explain what the scene reveals about that novel’s definition of heroism. Do not summarize the plot — use the scenes as evidence for your thesis claim.

3

One concessive sentence beginning with “Although...”

Grant the strongest objection to your thesis, then answer it. This sentence can appear in either body paragraph or in the conclusion — wherever the objection is most relevant.

4

Vocabulary words from both modules (at least two)

Use at least two vocabulary words from across Modules 1 and 2 in context where they do real analytical work (not just inserted for the requirement). Underline them.

5

VR component: link your 5.5 spatial recording

At the end of your essay, include a link to your VR 5.5 recording ("Bilbo at the End") as your spatial analysis component. The essay and the recording are both part of the same project.
