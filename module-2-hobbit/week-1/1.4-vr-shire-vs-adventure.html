7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 1 — VR Activity 1

VR 1.4 — Shire vs. The Road

Staging Bilbo’s divided identity in space

What You’re Doing

Bilbo Baggins is divided — between the Baggins side (comfort, routine, a full pantry) and the Tookish side (curiosity, the unknown, adventure). In this session you will stage that division in space: two zones representing each pull on Bilbo, and a boundary between them where you will place yourself as the character at the end of Chapter 6. The spatial arrangement is the argument. Where you position your “Bilbo IFX” and what your sticky note says becomes your claim about which pull is winning — and why.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required for full IFX manipulation)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W1
  • Location: Fantasy Countryside
  • Spawn point: Default — look for the teacher-placed Welcome sticky note on arrival
  • Pre-reading: Chapters 1 and 6 of The Hobbit
How to Join
Session type: Persistent (your IFX remain between visits)
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W1
Location: Fantasy Countryside
On arrival: Find the Welcome sticky note at the spawn point. It marks the center boundary line between Zone A (Shire) and Zone B (The Road). Your work happens on both sides of that line.
What to Do, In Order
1

Build Zone A — The Shire (left of the boundary line)

Place 3–4 IFX that represent Bilbo’s Baggins side — the life he is trying to hold onto. Think: domestic objects, food, comfort, stillness. Arrange them so they feel settled, contained, and secure. You are not recreating Bag End exactly; you are building the feeling of it. Scale at least one IFX smaller than default to reinforce the sense of a cozy, bounded world.

2

Build Zone B — The Road (right of the boundary line)

Place 3–4 IFX that represent what lies beyond the Shire — the unknown, the wild, the adventure Bilbo has now half-entered. Think: open skies, weather, height, distance, movement. Arrange them so they feel expansive and slightly unsettling. At least one IFX should feel large or open — something that dwarfs the scale of Zone A.

3

Mark the Boundary

Place one IFX at the center boundary line between the two zones. This is the threshold — the moment between leaving and not yet having arrived. It should feel ambiguous: it could belong to either zone. A gate, an archway, a standing stone, a lantern — anything that reads as a crossing point. This IFX is the spatial equivalent of Bilbo running out of Bag End without his pocket-handkerchief.

4

Place a Sticky Note — “Bilbo’s Position, Chapter 6”

Place a sticky note at the boundary IFX. Title it Bilbo’s Position, Ch. 6. Write 2–3 sentences explaining: at the end of Chapter 6, which pull is stronger — Zone A or Zone B — and what specific evidence from the text supports your reading? Quote or closely paraphrase at least one moment from the novel.

5

Take Your Snapshot

Position yourself so both zones and the boundary IFX are visible in the frame. Your sticky note must be legible — zoom in if needed to make the text readable. Take a snapshot from this position. This is your submission.

What to Submit

Upload your snapshot to this Canvas assignment. The snapshot must show both zones, the boundary IFX, and a legible sticky note. No written reflection required — the image and the note are your argument.

File format: PNG or JPG snapshot taken from inside ENGAGE
Naming: LastName_VR1-4_Shire
Time

Approximately 25 minutes in-headset. Most of this is building and adjusting the two zones — the sticky note writing is short once your arrangement is complete.

Points

10 points — completion
Two zones built with appropriate IFX, boundary IFX placed, sticky note present with textual evidence, legible snapshot submitted.

Facilitator Note

Students are building a two-zone spatial composition representing Bilbo’s divided character. Common sticking point: students may treat Zone B as simply “scary things” rather than “the pull of the unknown.” If a student’s Zone B consists only of monsters or weapons, prompt them to think about what draws Bilbo, not just what frightens him — open skies, the dwarves’ song, possibility. The sticky note is the key graded component; check that it contains a specific textual reference, not just a general claim. The persistent Session allows you to walk through and view all students’ compositions after the session.
