VR 1.5 — The Troll Encounter

Annotating Bilbo’s first test — what he does, what saves him, and what it means

What You’re Doing

The troll encounter in Chapter 2 is Bilbo’s first real test — and he fails it spectacularly. He gets caught trying to pickpocket a troll. The company is captured. They are saved not by Bilbo but by Gandalf, who tricks the trolls into arguing until dawn turns them to stone. In this session you will stage the encounter, annotate three key moments, and record a short analysis of what this scene reveals about heroism in The Hobbit: Is Bilbo a hero here? Is Gandalf? And does it matter who saves whom?

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W1
  • Location: Fantasy Countryside — move to a darker, more wooded area of the Location for your staging
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 2 of The Hobbit (“Roast Mutton”)
How to Join
Session type: Persistent (same Session as VR 1.4 — your Shire/Road composition is still there)
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W1
Location: Fantasy Countryside
On arrival: Move away from the Zone A/Zone B composition you built in VR 1.4. Find an open area with darker or more wooded surroundings. Your troll staging happens here, separate from the Shire composition.
What to Do, In Order
1

Stage the Three Trolls

Place three large IFX to represent Bert, Tom, and William — the three trolls. Arrange them around a central fire or campfire IFX. They should feel bulky, stupid, and dominant in the space — scale them larger than default if needed. This is the environment Bilbo walks into when he attempts the burglary.

2

Annotate Three Moments — One Sticky Note Each

Place three sticky notes in or near the troll arrangement, one for each of the following moments. Each note should include a brief quote or paraphrase from Chapter 2 and one sentence of analysis:

Note A — The Attempt: What does Bilbo do when he tries to pick the troll’s pocket? Does his action show cunning, courage, desperation, or something else?

Note B — The Capture: The trolls catch him. How do they treat him, and what does the scene suggest about Bilbo’s position in the company — is he valued, underestimated, or something in between?

Note C — The Rescue: Gandalf saves the company by tricking the trolls into arguing until dawn. Does this rescue make Bilbo’s failure matter less, or does Bilbo’s failure matter in a different way than it first seems?
3

Record a 60–90 Second Spatial Recording

Start a spatial recording. Walk through your troll arrangement and narrate your three sticky notes in order — A, B, C. Then answer this question aloud as you finish: Is this scene a failure for Bilbo, or is it the beginning of something? Defend your answer in one or two sentences. Stop the recording when you are done. Your recording should be between 60 and 90 seconds.

What to Submit

Upload your spatial recording to this Canvas assignment. The recording must walk through all three sticky notes and include your final spoken answer about whether the scene is a failure or a beginning.

Facilitator Note

Students are analyzing the troll encounter through spatial annotation and spoken reflection. The key interpretive challenge is Note C: students tend to dismiss Bilbo’s failure because Gandalf rescues them, missing the point that the scene matters precisely because Bilbo fails — it establishes what he has to grow past. If a student’s Note C says only “Gandalf saved them so it’s fine,” prompt them: why does Tolkien include a scene where the hero fails? What would be lost if Gandalf had just scared the trolls directly? The spatial recording is the main evidence of analysis — check that it includes the final spoken judgment, not just a summary of the notes.
