7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 1 — Quick Write

Quick Write 1.6 — Bilbo’s Divided Identity

Using appositives and compound sentences to show who Bilbo is — and who he’s becoming

What You’re Doing

You have studied two grammar structures this week: the compound sentence (two independent clauses joined by a coordinating conjunction) and the semicolon (two independent clauses joined without a conjunction). Now you will use both to capture Bilbo Baggins at two different moments — Chapter 1 and Chapter 6 — and show, through the structure of your sentences, how he is the same character who is becoming a different one.

Your Three Sentences
1

Sentence 1 — Bilbo in Chapter 1 (appositive)

Write one sentence that introduces Bilbo using an appositive — a noun phrase set off by commas that renames or further describes the subject. The appositive should capture his Baggins side: comfort, routine, domestic contentment. The rest of the sentence should show something he does or values in Chapter 1.

Model: “Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit of perfectly conventional tastes and an admirably well-stocked pantry, refused Gandalf’s invitation without a second thought.”
2

Sentence 2 — Bilbo in Chapter 6 (compound sentence)

Write a compound sentence using yet or but that captures Bilbo at the end of Chapter 6. One clause should acknowledge what he was; the other should show what he has done or become. The conjunction you choose matters — yet for a stronger sense of surprise or contradiction, but for a straightforward contrast.

Model: “He had never fought anything more dangerous than a bad cold in his life, yet by the end of Chapter 6 he had survived trolls, goblins, Gollum, and a ride on an eagle.”
Label which conjunction you used and explain in one phrase why you chose it over the other option.
3

Sentence 3 — The Distance Between Them (semicolon)

Write one sentence using a semicolon that puts Chapter 1 Bilbo and Chapter 6 Bilbo side by side without naming the connection directly. The two clauses should create the contrast on their own — the semicolon implies the relationship; the reader feels it. No conjunction allowed.

Model: “In Chapter 1, Bilbo sent Gandalf away before the man had finished his pipe; by Chapter 6, he was helping carry unconscious dwarves out of a goblin tunnel.”
Submission

Type all three sentences directly into the text box above, then submit. You do not need to write a separate paragraph. The three sentences are the assignment.

Points & Rubric
Sentence 1: appositive correctly punctuated, captures Baggins side
Pass / Fail
Sentence 2: compound sentence with yet or but, contrast is real (not just addition), conjunction labeled with brief justification
Pass / Fail
Sentence 3: semicolon between two independent clauses, no coordinating conjunction, contrast implied not stated
Pass / Fail

5 points for completing all three sentences correctly. Partial credit (3 pts) if two of three meet the standard. This is a grammar-application exercise, not a writing assessment — the goal is correct use of the structures, not prose quality.
