7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 2 — VR Activity 1

VR 2.4 — The Riddle Game Chamber

Staging the darkness, the water, and the contest of minds

What You’re Doing

The riddle game in Chapter 5 takes place in near-total darkness, on a small island in an underground lake, with Gollum and Bilbo facing each other across the water. In this session you will reconstruct that setting, stage the two figures, and annotate the contest with a Whiteboard IFX that tracks the power dynamic — who has the advantage, and when does it shift? The argument you are making through your staging is about what kind of contest this really is: a riddle game, yes — but also a test of something harder to name.

What to Do, In Order
1

Build the Setting: Island and Lake

Use IFX to create a central island (flat, small, isolated) surrounded by a sense of water. You don’t need to recreate water literally — use dark, flat, low IFX around the island to suggest the underground lake. Scale the island IFX so it feels cramped: there is barely room for two figures to face each other. Add a rock IFX on the island for Gollum’s boat/seat, and an open space for Bilbo standing across from him.

2

Stage Gollum and Bilbo

Place one IFX for Gollum and one for Bilbo on the island, facing each other. Gollum should be low, crouched, and given slightly more visual weight than Bilbo — he controls the space. Bilbo should appear smaller and more exposed. You are not just decorating; your IFX choices and placement are making an argument about power in this scene.

3

Add a Whiteboard IFX — “Power Tracker”

Place a Whiteboard IFX near the island and connect it to your Drawpad. Title it Power Tracker. Using the Drawpad, draw or write a simple diagram tracking the power dynamic across the riddle game in three stages:

Stage 1 (early riddles): Who has the advantage and why?
Stage 2 (middle riddles): Does the advantage shift, or stay the same?
Stage 3 (“What have I got in my pocket?”): When and why does the balance tip to Bilbo?

Your tracker can be a simple left-right balance diagram, a timeline with arrows, or annotated text — whatever makes the shift visible on the whiteboard.

4

Take a Snapshot

Position yourself so the island staging and the Whiteboard IFX are both visible. The Power Tracker content must be legible. Take a snapshot from this position. This is your submission.

Facilitator Note

Students are doing spatial analysis of a power dynamic through staging and whiteboard annotation. The key interpretive question is Stage 3 of the Power Tracker: most students will correctly identify that Bilbo’s “What have I got in my pocket?” shifts the balance, but weaker students may only note that Bilbo “wins” without explaining the mechanism — that an unanswerable question gives him advantage precisely because Gollum cannot verify it. Prompt: How does Bilbo’s question break the rules of the game, and why does Gollum accept it anyway? The whiteboard is the evidence of analysis; encourage students to use visual layout (arrows, labels) rather than just written text if it helps them show the shift more clearly.
