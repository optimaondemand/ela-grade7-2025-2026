VR 2.5 — Mirkwood: Bilbo Names His Sword

Staging the spider encounter and the moment Bilbo claims an identity

What You’re Doing

Chapter 8 contains one of the most important moments in Bilbo’s transformation: he kills a Mirkwood spider alone, and then — without anyone watching, without prompting — he names his short sword “Sting.” In traditional epic literature, naming a weapon is an act of identity. Beowulf’s sword Hrunting, Siegfried’s Gram, Roland’s Durandal — a warrior who names a weapon is claiming the role of warrior. Bilbo does it alone in the dark, which makes it both more and less heroic than those traditional moments. In this session you will stage the spider encounter and record a short spatial analysis of what this naming means.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W2
  • Location: Underground Cave or Cavern (same Session as VR 2.4 — move to a different area of the cave)
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 8 of The Hobbit (“Flies and Spiders”)
How to Join
Session type: Persistent (same Session as VR 2.4)
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W2
On arrival: Move away from your riddle game island. Find an open area of the cave — this is your Mirkwood. The cave setting works as Mirkwood because Bilbo’s spider encounter happens in near-darkness and enclosed space.
What to Do, In Order
1

Stage the Encounter: Bilbo and the Spider

Place one IFX for the spider (large, angular, threatening) and one IFX for Bilbo (smaller, upright, not cowering). This is the moment just after Bilbo has stabbed the spider — he is alone, the spider is defeated, and he has just realized what he did. Your staging should suggest the aftermath: Bilbo standing in a space that is slightly more open now that one threat is gone, with the spider IFX lowered or tipped to suggest it has fallen.

2

Place a Sticky Note — “The Naming”

Place a sticky note near the Bilbo IFX. Title it The Naming. Write 2–3 sentences responding to this question: When Bilbo calls his sword “Sting,” is he claiming a heroic identity, or just making a practical observation? What does the fact that he does it alone, without witnesses, tell us about what kind of hero Tolkien is building?

3

Record a 60–90 Second Spatial Recording

Start a spatial recording. Walk through your staging and narrate: (1) what is happening in the scene, (2) what the naming of “Sting” means for Bilbo’s development, and (3) how this moment compares to Chapter 1 Bilbo — what specifically has changed. Stop when done. 60–90 seconds.

Facilitator Note

The key analytical move in this session is the "alone, without witnesses" observation. Students who make this point are noticing that Bilbo’s heroism at this stage is private — he has not been publicly recognized, and he doesn’t need to be for the naming to matter. Weaker responses will simply describe the naming without analyzing its significance. For the comparison to Chapter 1: look for specific details, not just “he was scared before and now he’s brave.” What did Chapter 1 Bilbo literally say and do? How does Chapter 8 Bilbo behave differently under comparable pressure? The spatial recording is the primary analysis artifact; check that it moves through all three required points in sequence.
