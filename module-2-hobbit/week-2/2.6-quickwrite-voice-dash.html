Quick Write 2.6 — Voice and Emphasis

Active voice, passive voice, and the dash — putting this week’s grammar to work

What You’re Doing

You have two grammar tools this week: the active/passive voice distinction and the em dash. This quick write asks you to use both in the same short paragraph, making deliberate choices about emphasis — who acts, who is acted upon, and where you want the reader to feel a beat.

Your Paragraph

Write a paragraph of 4–6 sentences describing one scene from Chapters 5–10 of The Hobbit. Your paragraph must include:

1

At least two sentences in active voice where the agent (the one doing the action) is a character you want the reader to focus on. Underline these sentences.

2

At least one sentence in passive voice, used deliberately — where the receiver of the action matters more than the doer. Put an asterisk (*) before this sentence.

3

At least one em dash — used for emphasis at the end of a sentence or for interruption inside a sentence. Put a [D] at the end of the sentence where you use the dash.

Annotation

Below your paragraph, write three short labels (one sentence each):
