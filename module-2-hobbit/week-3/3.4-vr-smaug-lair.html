7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 3 — VR Activity 1

VR 3.4 — Smaug’s Lair

Staging the hoard, the dragon, and the lone hobbit in the dark

What You’re Doing

Tolkien describes Smaug’s lair as a vast underground hall filled with the accumulated treasure of a mountain kingdom — cups, crowns, armor, coins, and jewels piled so deep the dragon sleeps on top of them. In this session you will build that lair in VR: a composition that captures the scale and danger of the space, the overwhelming presence of the dragon, and the smallness of Bilbo moving through it invisible. The composition is your interpretation of the power dynamic before Bilbo speaks his first riddle-name.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W3
  • Location: Underground Cave or Cavern
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 12 of The Hobbit (“Inside Information”)
How to Join
Session type: Persistent
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W3
Location: Underground Cave or Cavern
On arrival: Look for the Welcome sticky note. Find the largest open area of the cave for your lair staging.
What to Do, In Order
1

Build the Hoard

Place 5–8 IFX to represent Smaug’s treasure hoard. Use IFX that suggest wealth, accumulation, and age — not just coins, but armor, cups, crowns, ancient objects. Cluster them densely in the center of your space. The hoard should take up most of the floor area. Scale at least two IFX larger than default to give the hoard a sense of mass.

2

Place Smaug on the Hoard

Place one large IFX on top of or alongside the hoard to represent Smaug. This IFX should be the largest object in your composition — scale it significantly above default. Tolkien describes Smaug as encrusted with jewels from lying on the gold for centuries; try to choose or angle the IFX so it appears to be resting, occupying the space with weight and ease rather than aggression. The danger is in his stillness, not his movement.

3

Place Bilbo at the Tunnel Entrance

Place one small IFX at the edge of your composition — at the entrance to the lair, as far from Smaug as possible. This is Bilbo. Scale it significantly smaller than the Smaug IFX. The visual relationship between the two IFX (distance, scale difference, Bilbo at the margin) is your argument about the power imbalance in this moment.

4

Place a Sticky Note — “Before the First Word”

Place a sticky note near your Bilbo IFX. Title it Before the First Word. Write 2–3 sentences: At this moment, before Bilbo speaks, what is the most important thing the spatial relationship between Bilbo and Smaug is showing? Begin your note with an introductory phrase (prepositional, participial, or adverbial).

What to Submit

Take a snapshot showing the full lair composition — the hoard, Smaug, and Bilbo all visible in frame, with the sticky note legible. Upload to this Canvas assignment.

Time

Approximately 25 minutes in-headset: ~15 min building the lair, ~8 min sticky note, ~2 min snapshot.

Points

10 points — completion
Hoard (5+ IFX), Smaug IFX scaled large, Bilbo IFX scaled small at margin, sticky note with introductory phrase opening, legible snapshot.

Facilitator Note

The key grading question here is whether the scale relationship between the Smaug IFX and the Bilbo IFX is deliberate. Students who build the hoard and place both figures the same size have missed the spatial argument the assignment is asking for. Prompt: How big is Smaug compared to Bilbo in the text? Walk me through where Bilbo is standing relative to the dragon. The sticky note grammar check: does the student’s note begin with an introductory phrase (not a subject)? If it begins “Bilbo is...” or “Smaug is...” the grammar requirement is not met — redirect to their 3.2 lesson.
