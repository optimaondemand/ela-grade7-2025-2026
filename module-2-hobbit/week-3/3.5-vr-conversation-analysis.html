7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 3 — VR Activity 2

VR 3.5 — The Conversation with Smaug

Two Whiteboard IFX — what each character wants, what each reveals, and who gains from the exchange

What You’re Doing

The conversation between Bilbo and Smaug in Chapter 12 is a rhetorical contest — each speaker has a goal, each speaker is concealing information, and each speaker is trying to extract information from the other. In this session you will analyze that contest using two Whiteboard IFX: one for Bilbo’s side and one for Smaug’s side. Your analysis tracks what each character wants, what each reveals, and who comes away with more than they started with.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W3
  • Location: Underground Cave or Cavern (same Session as VR 3.4)
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 12 of The Hobbit — the dialogue section specifically
How to Join
Session type: Persistent (same Session as VR 3.4 — your lair is still there)
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W3
On arrival: Place your two Whiteboard IFX near your lair composition from VR 3.4. They should face each other across the space, like two sides of a debate.
What to Do, In Order
1

Place Two Whiteboard IFX

Place one Whiteboard IFX and connect it to your Drawpad. Title it BILBO’S SIDE. Place a second Whiteboard IFX and connect it. Title it SMAUG’S SIDE. Position them facing each other across a central space — the conversation happens in the gap between them.

2

Fill Bilbo’s Whiteboard

Using your Drawpad, write or draw three sections on Bilbo’s board:

What Bilbo wants from this conversation: (e.g., information about the bare patch; to get out alive)
What Bilbo reveals: (Note: he accidentally reveals that he came from Lake-town through the riddle-name “Barrel-rider”)
What Bilbo gains: (knowledge of Smaug’s weakness; survival)
3

Fill Smaug’s Whiteboard

Using your Drawpad, write or draw three sections on Smaug’s board:

What Smaug wants from this conversation: (to identify who sent the thief and where they came from)
What Smaug reveals: (the bare patch — an act of hubris; his location and plans)
What Smaug gains: (confirmation of Lake-town connection; but he is ultimately outmaneuvered by what the thrush overhears)
4

Add a Verdict Sticky Note

Place a sticky note in the space between the two whiteboards. Title it Who Won? Write 2–3 sentences: Who comes away from this conversation with more than they started with? Is the answer clear, or does it depend on how you define “winning”? Use the word hubris in your answer. Begin with an introductory phrase.

Facilitator Note

The key analytical challenge in this activity is the “What Bilbo reveals” section: many students will think Bilbo concealed everything successfully. He did not — “Barrel-rider” pointed Smaug directly at Lake-town. Prompt: What does Smaug say about the barrel-riders that shows he has inferred where Bilbo came from? The verdict sticky note should demonstrate that students can hold a complex answer: neither character fully wins or loses — Bilbo escapes and gains crucial information; Smaug gains confirmation of the Lake-town connection but reveals his weakness through hubris. Grammar check: does the sticky note begin with a prepositional, participial, or adverbial phrase?
