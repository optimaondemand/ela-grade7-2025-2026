The Hobbit — Week 3 — Quick Write

Quick Write 3.6 — Opening the Sentence Differently

Revision and original sentences using introductory elements

What You’re Doing

This quick write has two parts: first, revising three flat sentences to open with introductory elements; second, writing two original sentences about the Smaug encounter that begin with different types of introductory elements. Both parts practice the craft move from this week’s grammar lessons.

Part 1 — Revision (3 sentences)

Rewrite each sentence so it begins with the type of introductory element named in brackets. Keep the same content. Add a comma after the introductory element.

1

“Bilbo examined the bare patch on Smaug’s belly carefully.” → Rewrite with a prepositional phrase opening.

▶ Sample answer
“With careful attention, Bilbo examined the bare patch on Smaug’s belly.” or “In the red glow of the dragon’s scales, Bilbo examined the bare patch carefully.”
2

“Smaug showed Bilbo his armored belly and said he was invulnerable.” → Rewrite with a participial phrase opening.

▶ Sample answer
“Rolling over to display his armored belly, Smaug boasted that he was invulnerable.” The participial phrase “rolling over” captures the physicality of Smaug’s gesture first, then names what he says.
3

“Bilbo noticed the bare patch and decided to tell the dwarves.” → Rewrite with an adverbial clause opening.

▶ Sample answer
“Although he could barely see in the dark, Bilbo noticed the bare patch and decided it was worth reporting to the dwarves.”
Part 2 — Original Sentences (2 sentences)

Write two original sentences about any moment in Chapters 11–14. Each must begin with a different type of introductory element. Label the type in brackets at the end.
