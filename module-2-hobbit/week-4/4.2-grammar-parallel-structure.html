7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 4 — Grammar Lesson

Lesson 4.2 — Parallel Structure

When items in a series must match — and what happens when they don’t

Objectives
1

Identify faulty parallelism in a series or with paired conjunctions and correct it.

2

Use parallel structure deliberately to create emphasis, balance, and rhetorical force.

Warm-Up 1 min
Read these two versions: (A) “Bilbo was brave, clever, and showed loyalty to his friends.” (B) “Bilbo was brave, clever, and loyal.” Both say the same thing. Which feels more satisfying? Why?

Version B uses parallel structure: three adjectives in a row, all the same grammatical form. Version A breaks the pattern — “brave” and “clever” are adjectives, but “showed loyalty” is a verb phrase. The structural mismatch creates a small friction the reader feels even if they cannot name it. Parallel structure is what makes a series sound finished.

Lesson

Parallel structure requires that items joined in a series or by paired conjunctions have the same grammatical form. Two rules:

Rule 1: Items in a series must match in form.

Faulty: “Bilbo survived trolls, goblins, and facing a dragon.” (noun, noun, gerund phrase — mismatched)
Fixed: “Bilbo survived trolls, goblins, and a dragon.” (noun, noun, noun)
or: “Bilbo survived fighting trolls, escaping goblins, and outwitting a dragon.” (gerund phrase ×3)

The matching form can be nouns, verbs, adjectives, gerunds, infinitives, or clauses — just keep it consistent within the series.

Rule 2: Paired conjunctions require matching structure on both sides.

The three paired conjunctions are: both/and, either/or, not only/but also. Whatever grammatical form follows the first word must follow the second:

Faulty: “Bilbo was not only brave but also showed cleverness.” (adjective / verb phrase — mismatched)
Fixed: “Bilbo was not only brave but also clever.” (adjective / adjective)
Fixed: “Bilbo not only survived the encounter but also learned something from it.” (verb phrase / verb phrase)

The easiest way to check: after the second conjunction word, ask “does this match the form after the first?”

Practice
1

Identify the faulty parallelism and rewrite: “Thorin was obsessed with gold, controlling the gates, and wanted to drive away the men of Lake-town.”

▶ Answer
Problem: noun phrase / gerund phrase / verb phrase — three different forms.
Fix (all noun): “Thorin was obsessed with gold, control of the gates, and the expulsion of the Lake-town men.”
Fix (all gerund): “Thorin was obsessed with hoarding gold, controlling the gates, and driving away the men of Lake-town.”
2

Rewrite using “not only / but also” with correct parallel structure: “Bilbo’s decision was brave. His decision also showed wisdom.”

▶ Answer
“Bilbo’s decision was not only brave but also wise.” (adjective/adjective)
or: “Bilbo not only acted bravely but also showed genuine wisdom.” (verb phrase/verb phrase)
3

Write one sentence about the Battle of the Five Armies using a series of three parallel items. Write another using “both/and” or “either/or.” Label the parallel elements in brackets.
