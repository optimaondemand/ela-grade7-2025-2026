7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 4 — Grammar Lesson

Lesson 4.3 — Complex Sentences

Making relationships between ideas explicit — the grammar of cause, condition, and concession

Objectives
1

Distinguish complex sentences from compound sentences and understand what each structure does for meaning.

2

Use subordinating conjunctions precisely to name the relationship between a dependent and an independent clause.

Warm-Up 1 min
Compare: (A) “Thorin loved the treasure, and he refused to share it.” (B) “Because Thorin loved the treasure, he refused to share it.” Both sentences contain the same two facts. What is different about what each one says about the relationship between them?

Version A joins two equal clauses with and — it says “these two things happened.” Version B is a complex sentence: the subordinating conjunction because makes one clause the cause of the other. The relationship is no longer implied; it is named. A complex sentence is the grammar of explanation — it tells the reader how the two ideas are connected, not just that they are.

Lesson

A complex sentence contains one independent clause and at least one dependent (subordinate) clause. The dependent clause is introduced by a subordinating conjunction that names the relationship:

Cause/Reason
because, since, as — “Since Thorin refused to negotiate, the standoff continued.”
Condition
if, unless, provided that — “Unless Thorin gave up the Arkenstone, there would be war.”
Concession
although, even though, while, whereas — “Although Bilbo acted against Thorin’s wishes, Gandalf called it well done.”
Time
when, after, before, until, while, once — “After the battle ended, Bilbo visited the dying Thorin.”
Purpose
so that, in order that — “Bilbo brought the Arkenstone so that war could be averted.”

Punctuation rule: If the dependent clause comes first (before the independent clause), put a comma after it. If the dependent clause comes second, no comma:

Comma needed: “Although Bilbo felt guilty, he gave away the Arkenstone.” (dependent clause first)
No comma: “Bilbo gave away the Arkenstone although he felt guilty.” (independent clause first)
Practice
1

Join these two sentences using the most precise subordinating conjunction. Explain your choice. “Thorin was furious.” “Bilbo had given away the Arkenstone.”

▶ Sample answer
“Thorin was furious because Bilbo had given away the Arkenstone.” Because is the most precise choice: it names cause (Bilbo’s action caused Thorin’s fury). When would name only a time relationship; although would create a concession that isn’t in the original meaning.
2

Write three complex sentences about the events of Chapters 15–17 — one expressing cause/reason, one expressing concession (although/even though), and one expressing condition (if/unless). Punctuate correctly and label the subordinating conjunction type in brackets.
