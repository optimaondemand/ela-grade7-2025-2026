The Hobbit — Week 4 — VR Activity 1

VR 4.4 — The Night Bilbo Leaves

Staging the threshold moment — what Bilbo holds, what he risks, and what he chooses

What You’re Doing

In the middle of the night, Bilbo slips out of the dwarves’ camp carrying the Arkenstone. He crosses to the enemy camp — the men of Lake-town and the elves of Mirkwood — and gives them the stone. This moment is the moral climax of Bilbo’s journey. In this session you will stage the threshold: the space between the two camps, with Bilbo at the crossing point, and two zones showing what he is leaving behind and what he is walking toward. Your staging is an argument about what this choice costs and what it accomplishes.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W4
  • Location: Fantasy Countryside or Mountain Terrain
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 15 of The Hobbit (“The Gathering of the Clouds”)
How to Join
Session type: Persistent
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W4
Location: Fantasy Countryside or Mountain Terrain
On arrival: Find the Welcome sticky note. Choose a large open area for the two-zone layout.
What to Do, In Order
1

Build Zone A — What Bilbo Is Leaving (the dwarves’ camp)

Place 3–4 IFX representing what Bilbo has built with the dwarves: companionship, shared danger, the quest they completed together. These should feel warm and earned — but also enclosed and increasingly tense. Add a sticky note titled Zone A: What He Risks listing two or three things Bilbo stands to lose by walking away.

2

Build Zone B — What Bilbo Is Walking Toward (the enemy camp)

Place 2–3 IFX representing the men and elves Bilbo is bringing the Arkenstone to — people he barely knows, who are currently enemies of the dwarves. These should feel more open and uncertain. Add a sticky note titled Zone B: What He Hopes to Accomplish listing two or three things Bilbo is trying to achieve by crossing over.

3

Place Bilbo at the Threshold + Record Analysis

Place a small IFX for Bilbo at the midpoint between the two zones — in the crossing, not yet arrived. Then start a spatial recording (60–90 seconds). Walk through your composition and answer: Is Bilbo betraying the dwarves, or is he honoring something larger than the contract? Use the words moral courage and reconciliation in your recording. Begin at least one sentence with a subordinating conjunction.

Facilitator Note

This is the moral climax of the module. The key question in the recording is whether students can hold the complexity: Bilbo’s action is simultaneously a betrayal (by Thorin’s measure) and the right thing (by Gandalf’s, and ultimately Thorin’s). Students who give a simple answer (“Bilbo was right because Thorin was wrong”) are missing the tension. Prompt: What did Bilbo actually give up? What does he risk by doing this? The vocabulary check: listen for moral courage (the quality that makes this act heroic) and reconciliation (what Bilbo is hoping for — and what he eventually gets, at Thorin’s deathbed).
