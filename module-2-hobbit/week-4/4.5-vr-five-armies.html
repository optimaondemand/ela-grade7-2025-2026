7th Grade ELA — Module 2
The Hobbit — Week 4 — VR Activity 2

VR 4.5 — The Battle of Five Armies

A map of forces — who is fighting whom, and why it didn’t have to happen

What You’re Doing

The Battle of the Five Armies involves five factions with different motivations: dwarves (reclaiming their kingdom), men of Lake-town (seeking their share of the treasure and compensation), Mirkwood elves (ancient claim to some of the hoard), wargs and goblins (opportunistic attack), and eagles + Beorn (arriving late to rout the goblin forces). In this session you will build a spatial map of the battle — five zones, one per faction — and annotate each zone with what that faction wants and how it ended up in this war. Your analysis will then connect back to Bilbo: where is he during the battle, and what does his position reveal?

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W4
  • Location: Fantasy Countryside or Mountain Terrain (same Session as VR 4.4)
  • Pre-reading: Chapters 17–18 of The Hobbit (“The Clouds Burst” and “The Return Journey”)
How to Join
Session type: Persistent (same Session as VR 4.4)
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W4
On arrival: Move to a different area from your Arkenstone threshold composition. You need a large open space to lay out five faction zones.
What to Do, In Order
1

Build Five Faction Zones

Place 1–2 IFX per faction in distinct areas of your space, arranged so the zones face a central area (the battlefield). Use scale to show power: the goblins and wargs should be the largest mass; the dwarves should be relatively small; the eagles should be placed high or apart from the central mass to signal their late arrival. Five zones: Dwarves, Men of Lake-town, Mirkwood Elves, Goblins/Wargs, Eagles/Beorn.

2

Add a Sticky Note to Each Zone

Place one sticky note per faction zone. Each note must answer two questions in one or two sentences: (1) What does this faction want from the mountain? (2) Did this faction have to be here — was there a point where a decision could have prevented their involvement in the battle?

3

Place Bilbo and Take a Snapshot

Find where Bilbo is during the actual battle: knocked unconscious by a stone at the start, he misses most of it. Place a very small IFX at the edge of your battlefield map, apart from all five zones, to represent Bilbo’s position. Take a snapshot showing all five zones, the faction sticky notes (as legible as possible), and Bilbo’s small IFX at the margin. Upload this snapshot.

What to Submit

One snapshot showing the full five-faction map with sticky notes visible. Take a second closer snapshot of the sticky notes if needed for legibility. Upload both to this assignment.

File format: PNG or JPG snapshot(s)
Naming: LastName_VR4-5_Battle
Time

Approximately 30 minutes in-headset: ~15 min building zones, ~12 min sticky notes, ~3 min snapshot.

Points

10 points — completion
Five zones built, one sticky note per zone answering both questions, Bilbo IFX placed at margin, snapshot(s) submitted.

Facilitator Note

The key analytical move is the second sticky note question: “did this faction have to be here?” Students who engage with this question are engaging with Tolkien’s theme — that the battle is the result of a failure to negotiate, and that almost every faction had legitimate grievances that Thorin’s dragon-sickness prevented from being addressed. The goblins and wargs are the only faction who would have shown up regardless; everyone else is there because of Thorin’s refusal. Bilbo’s position at the margin is thematically important: the hero of the quest is unconscious during the battle he tried to prevent. That irony is worth discussing with students who notice it.
