VR 5.5 — Bilbo at the End

A final composition and spatial recording — who is he now, and what did it cost?

What You’re Doing

This is your final VR session for Module 2. You will build one composition showing Bilbo at the end of the novel — back in the Shire, changed but not easily legible to those around him — and record a 2-minute spatial analysis comparing who he was at the start (Chapter 1) with who he is now (Chapter 19). The recording is your spoken thesis for the module project: you are building your argument here in VR, then refining it into writing. This session feeds directly into your module project essay.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session ID: ELA7-M2-W5
  • Location: Cozy Indoor or Hobbit-style environment if available; Fantasy Cottage otherwise
  • Pre-reading: Chapter 19 of The Hobbit (“The Last Stage”)
  • Recommended: Reread your sticky note from VR 1.4 (Bilbo’s position, Chapter 1) before this session
How to Join
Session type: Persistent
Session ID: ELA7-M2-W5
Location: Fantasy Cottage or closest available indoor/cozy Location
On arrival: Look for the Welcome sticky note. You are building a homecoming scene.
What to Do, In Order
1

Build “Bilbo at Home”

Place 4–6 IFX that represent Bilbo’s life in the Shire at the end of Chapter 19 — the same domestic elements he valued in Chapter 1 (comfortable furniture, food, quiet), but now accompanied by at least one IFX that represents something he brought back: a piece of the adventure, a memory, a changed perspective. The tension between the comfortable and the changed should be visible in the arrangement.

2

Place a Whiteboard IFX — “Start vs. End”

Place a Whiteboard IFX and connect it to your Drawpad. Create two columns on the board: Chapter 1 Bilbo and Chapter 19 Bilbo. List 3 specific qualities, behaviors, or values in each column. The comparison should be precise — not just “scared vs. brave” but specific moments, choices, or self-understandings that changed.

3

Record a 2-Minute Spatial Recording

Start a spatial recording. Walk through your composition and whiteboard and narrate your argument about Bilbo’s transformation. Your recording must address all four of the following:

1. Who was Bilbo in Chapter 1? (Use a specific detail.)
2. What is the single most important thing that changed, and what moment in the novel caused or revealed that change?
3. Is Bilbo a hero? Use the words moral courage and transformation in your answer.
4. How does Bilbo’s return compare to Odysseus’s — what does each hero bring home, and what does that difference reveal about what each novel values?

This recording is the spoken rough draft of your module project essay. Think of it as talking through your thesis before you write it.

Facilitator Note

This session is the oral rough draft of the module project. The Bilbo/Odysseus comparison in point 4 is the hardest element — students may struggle to articulate the key difference: Odysseus reclaims an identity he already had; Bilbo discovers one he did not know existed. If a student’s recording treats the comparison as simply “both went on an adventure and came home,” prompt: What did Odysseus have before he left that Bilbo did not? What did Bilbo gain that Odysseus does not need to gain? The whiteboard Start vs. End column check: look for specific moments rather than traits (“In Chapter 1, he slammed the door on Gandalf” rather than “scared”). This feeds directly into essay evidence quality.
