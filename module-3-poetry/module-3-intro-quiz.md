## Quiz Description (HTML — paste into description field)

<p style="font-size:15px;line-height:1.7;">Module 3 moves from prose fiction into poetry — five centuries of it, from an anonymous Scottish ballad to Walt Whitman's democratic catalogues. The central question that drives this module: <strong>What can a poem do that prose cannot?</strong> By the end of the module, you will have read poems by Shakespeare, Emily Dickinson, Robert Frost, Langston Hughes, Walt Whitman, and Billy Collins; staged poetic images and structures in VR; practiced seven grammar structures that appear in the poetry itself; and written an original poem with a craft analysis explaining how you made it. The module project is an original poem (8–20 lines) paired with a 250–350 word written craft analysis and a VR spatial recording, due at the end of Week 5.</p>

## Questions (5 questions — survey mode, no correct answer)

**Question 1** (Essay)
This module asks: What can a poem do that prose cannot? What is your first instinct? Write 2–3 sentences with your initial answer before you begin reading any of the module's poems. You will return to this question at the end of the module.

**Question 2** (Multiple Choice — ungraded)
How would you describe your experience with poetry before this module?
- I don't really read poetry and find it confusing or boring
- I've read some poems in school but don't think much about them
- I enjoy reading poetry occasionally and have a few favorites
- I read poetry regularly and feel fairly comfortable analyzing it

**Question 3** (Essay)
Which grammar structure from the list below are you most confident using in your own writing right now? Which one would you most like to understand better by the end of this module?
*(Appositive, relative clause, colon, absolute phrase, noun clause, anaphora, line break as punctuation)*

**Question 4** (Multiple Choice — group work opt-in)
The Module 3 Project (original poem + craft analysis) is completed individually — there is no group option. Will you request any specific Location for your VR 5.5 session, or are the default Locations fine?
- Default Locations are fine
- I would like to request a specific Location (I will message my teacher)

**Question 5** (Essay)
Do you have a subject in mind for your original poem — something you might want to write about? It does not have to be a complete idea. Write one or two sentences, even if it is just a question or an image you keep returning to. If you have no idea yet, write "no idea yet" and explain why choosing a subject feels difficult.
