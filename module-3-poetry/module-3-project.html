Poetry — Module Project

Module 3 Project: Original Poem + Craft Analysis

A poem you wrote — and an explanation of how you made it

The Question
What can a poem do that prose cannot? Write one — and then explain how you made it do that thing.
What You’re Submitting

This project has two parts, submitted together as one document:

Part 1: Your Poem (8–20 lines)

An original poem of your own. You may choose any form — free verse, a formal structure (ballad stanza, sonnet, or another pattern), or a hybrid. The poem must have a central image and a clear purpose: you must be able to explain, in Part 2, what you were trying to do and how the poem’s form serves it. Do not write a poem to meet a requirement; write a poem because there is something you want this poem to say or do that prose would not let you say or do as well.

Part 2: Craft Analysis (250–350 words)

A prose analysis of your own poem explaining how you made the formal choices you made. You are not summarizing the poem — you are explaining how it works. The craft analysis must address: (1) the central image and what you want it to do; (2) at least one specific line break and why it falls where it does; (3) at least one other formal choice (sound, repetition, stanza structure, a specific word) and its purpose; (4) which Module 3 poem influenced your approach and how. At the end of Part 2, include a link to your VR 5.5 spatial recording.

Requirements
1

A poem with a central image

The poem must have one image at its center that does more than illustrate — it must carry meaning. The craft analysis will explain what it does.

2

Two Module 3 grammar structures in the craft analysis

The craft analysis must use at least two of the Module 3 grammar structures (appositive, relative clause, colon, absolute phrase, noun clause, anaphora, or line break analysis). Underline them.

3

Two vocabulary words from the module (underlined)

Use at least two vocabulary words from Module 3 in the craft analysis in context where they do real analytical work. Underline them. Acceptable words: alliteration, imagery, meter, refrain, volta, extended metaphor, anaphora, speaker, free verse, catalog, enjambment, end-stopped, stanza.

4

VR 5.5 recording link

At the end of Part 2, include a line that reads “VR Recording: [link]” linking to your VR 5.5 spatial recording of your poem reading and spoken craft commentary.
