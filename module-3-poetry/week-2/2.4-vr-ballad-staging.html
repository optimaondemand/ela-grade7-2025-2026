VR 2.4: Staging the Ballad

Build the scene a ballad implies but never shows

What You’re Doing

Ballads tell their stories through what they withhold. “Lord Randall” never shows the poisoning; “Ballad of the Landlord” never shows the arrest. Your job is to stage the key implied scene from one of the two ballads — the scene the poem is really about but never directly depicts — using IFX. Then place a sticky note that identifies what you are staging and explains what the poem withholds and why the withholding matters.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M3-W2 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Medieval Hall or Interior Room Location (for “Lord Randall”) or City Street Location (for “Ballad of the Landlord”)
What to Do
1

Choose your ballad and the scene you will stage

For “Lord Randall”: the implied scene is the poisoning — Lord Randall eating the meal prepared by his sweetheart, not yet knowing it is his last. For “Ballad of the Landlord”: the implied scene is the arrest — the moment the police come, which the poem jumps over with a newspaper headline. Place a title sticky note naming your ballad and your chosen scene.

2

Build the scene (4–7 IFX)

Arrange 4–7 IFX to represent the scene. Think about power dynamics: who is in control? Who is vulnerable? Reflect this in positioning, scale, and arrangement. For “Lord Randall”: table IFX, food/cup IFX, two figures (the sweetheart dominant, Lord Randall unaware). For “Ballad of the Landlord”: building IFX (crumbling), landlord IFX (distant, elevated), tenant IFX (at the door), police/authority IFX converging. Lock IFX when satisfied.

3

Write the analytical sticky note

Write a sticky note beside the composition with: (1) which scene you are staging and why it is the poem’s implied center, (2) one sentence using a colon to introduce an explanation of what the poem withholds and why (from Grammar 2.2), (3) one sentence using an absolute phrase to describe the state of one of the figures (from Grammar 2.3). Both grammar requirements must be visible in the sticky note.

4

Snapshot and submit

Take a snapshot with the sticky note visible. Name it YourLastName_M3_2.4 and submit to Canvas.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M3_2.4. Estimated time: 25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are staging the implied scene — the one the ballad never shows. The conceptual key is understanding that ballads derive their power from withholding, not from describing. Students who build elaborate scenes but can’t explain why the poem doesn’t show this are missing the point. The grammar check: look for colon + explanation and absolute phrase in the sticky note. Students who omit these should revise before their work is counted complete. The power-dynamics consideration in Step 2 is the analytical heart of the composition choice — watch for students who stage both figures at equal height/scale, which flattens the interpretive content.
