VR 2.5: Meter Map and Spoken Analysis

Scan the stress pattern of a ballad stanza — then explain it aloud

What You’re Doing

You will work in the same Session as VR 2.4, in a new area. Place a Whiteboard IFX and write out one complete stanza from one of the Week 2 ballads. Scan the stanza (mark stressed syllables with a slash, unstressed with a small u or dash), identify the metrical pattern, and record a 60–90 second spatial recording explaining how the meter contributes to the ballad’s emotional effect.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M3-W2 (same Persistent Session as VR 2.4 — move to a new area)
  • Your ballad: You may use the same ballad as VR 2.4 or switch to the other one
What to Do
1

Place your Whiteboard IFX and write the stanza

Place a Whiteboard IFX in a new area of the Session. Title it: METER MAP — [Ballad Title] — [Stanza Number] — [Your Name]. Write out the stanza word by word on the board, leaving enough space above each syllable to mark the stress pattern.

2

Scan the stanza

Mark each syllable above the text: use / for stressed, u (or —) for unstressed. Read the line aloud a few times before marking — your ear is more reliable than your eye for stress. At the bottom of the board, write: (1) the metrical foot name (iamb, trochee, etc.), (2) the number of stresses per line (ballad stanzas alternate 4 and 3), (3) the rhyme scheme (ABCB or similar).

3

Record your spoken analysis (60–90 seconds)

Standing in front of your meter map, record a spatial recording. Your analysis must cover: (1) what the metrical pattern is and where you found it in the stanza, (2) how the meter makes the poem feel different from prose — does it speed up? build inevitability? (3) what happens in the stanza you chose that is significant. Use at least one absolute phrase (from Grammar 2.3) in your spoken analysis.

4

Submit the recording

End the spatial recording. Paste the link into the Canvas submission text box. Name it YourLastName_M3_2.5.

Submission

Paste spatial recording link into Canvas submission text box. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are scanning a ballad stanza on a Whiteboard IFX and recording a spoken explanation of what the meter contributes emotionally. Meter scanning is the technical skill; what the meter does emotionally is the analytical skill. Students often get the scansion roughly right but struggle to connect the pattern to the feeling. Common sticking point: students may mark stresses differently than expected — this is fine, since stress is partly dialectical. What matters is consistency and the ability to identify the dominant pattern. The absolute phrase requirement in the spoken analysis is the grammar integration point for Week 2’s second grammar lesson.
