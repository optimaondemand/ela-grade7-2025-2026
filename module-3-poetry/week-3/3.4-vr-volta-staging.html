VR 3.4: Before and After the Volta

Build a two-part spatial composition that shows the sonnet’s argument before and after the turn

What You’re Doing

The volta is the sonnet’s argumentative hinge. In this session you will create a two-part spatial composition: Zone A (Before the Volta) and Zone B (After the Volta). Each zone uses IFX to represent the poem’s argument at that stage. A Whiteboard IFX in each zone names the argument and quotes the key line. The point is to make the shift in the poem visible as a spatial shift in your composition.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M3-W3 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Elizabethan Theater or Classical Library Location (to evoke the Renaissance context)
  • Sonnet: Sonnet 18 or Sonnet 73 (your choice)
What to Do
1

Set up your two zones

Establish two distinct areas in the Location, clearly separated. Place a sticky note label at the entrance to each: ZONE A — Before the Volta and ZONE B — After the Volta. The physical distance between the zones should represent the argumentative distance the sonnet travels.

2

Build Zone A: Before the Volta (3–4 IFX)

Place IFX that represent the poem’s argument before the turn. For Sonnet 18: this is the world of summer and its impermanence — beautiful but fading (summer landscape IFX, sun IFX dimming, cloud IFX overshadowing). For Sonnet 73: this is the declining world — autumn leaves, twilight sky, dying fire. Place a Whiteboard IFX and write the argument of the octave/quatrains in one sentence, plus the key line you are representing.

3

Build Zone B: After the Volta (2–3 IFX)

Place IFX that represent the poem’s resolution. For Sonnet 18: the poem/book itself as the surviving artifact — perhaps a book IFX or scroll IFX, placed prominently. For Sonnet 73: something that suggests intensified love in the face of loss — two figures close together, or a single warm light in darkness. Place a Whiteboard IFX with the couplet argument in one sentence and the quoted couplet lines.

4

Write the boundary sticky note

At the threshold between Zone A and Zone B, place a sticky note identifying: (1) where exactly the volta occurs (line number and key word), (2) one sentence using a noun clause that states what the volta changes (e.g., “What shifts at line 13 is…”). This is the analytical heart of the composition.

5

Take two snapshots and submit

Take one snapshot showing Zone A and one showing Zone B, each with the relevant Whiteboard IFX visible. Submit both snapshots as YourLastName_M3_3.4a and YourLastName_M3_3.4b.

Submission

Upload both snapshots. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are making the sonnet’s argumentative structure visible by staging “before” and “after” the volta as two spatial zones. The conceptual key: Zone B should look different from Zone A in a way that represents a genuine shift in the poem’s argument, not just a different arrangement of similar IFX. Students who treat the two zones as interchangeable or decorative have missed the volta’s function. The boundary sticky note (noun clause) is the grammar integration point — look for “What shifts is…” or “What the couplet changes is…” Students who omit the noun clause should revise before the composition counts as complete.
