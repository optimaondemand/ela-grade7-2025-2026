VR 4.5: The Escalating List

Stage Hughes’s anaphora as a spatial sequence that builds toward explosion

What You’re Doing

Hughes’s “A Dream Deferred” uses anaphora to build an escalating list of similes. In this session, working in a new area of the ELA7-M3-W4 Persistent Session, you will stage each simile in the list as a separate IFX, arranged in a line from smallest/most passive to largest/most violent. The spatial arrangement performs the escalation. A sticky note at the end of the line (at the position of the final question) explains what the explosion represents beyond its literal meaning.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M3-W4 (same Persistent Session as VR 4.4 — move to a new area)
What to Do
1

Set up the escalating line

Place a title sticky note at the start: “A Dream Deferred — The Escalating List — [Your Name]”. Then arrange IFX in a line representing each simile in the poem, moving from left to right (or front to back) in order of escalating violence/energy. The IFX should visually grow in scale, density, or intensity from first to last.

2

Label each IFX with a sticky note quoting the simile

At each IFX, place a small sticky note quoting the relevant simile from the poem (e.g., “like a raisin in the sun,” “like a sore that festers,” “like a syrupy sweet,” “like a heavy load,” “explode”). You do not need perfect IFX matches for each image — interpretive equivalents are fine (a small, dried object for the raisin; something bright/explosive for the final question).

3

Place the analytical sticky note at the end of the line

At the position of “Or does it explode?”, place a sticky note with: (1) one sentence using anaphora to describe what each simile in the list represents (e.g., “The raisin represents… the sore represents… the explosion represents…”), (2) one sentence stating what the poem’s argument is beyond the literal question about drying or festering.

4

Take a snapshot and submit

Take a snapshot showing the full line of IFX from first to last, ideally with the analytical sticky note visible at the end. Name it YourLastName_M3_4.5 and upload to Canvas.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M3_4.5. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are staging Hughes’s anaphoric list as a spatial escalation. The key is whether the IFX scale actually increases from first to last — students who arrange IFX at the same size throughout have made the composition static rather than dynamic, which flattens the poem’s structure. The analytical sticky note is the grammar integration point: look for anaphora (“X represents… Y represents… Z represents…”). The second sentence in the sticky note should name what the poem is really arguing beyond the literal images. Students who only describe the images (the raisin is small and dried) without interpreting them (the raisin represents what hope looks like after long neglect) need a follow-up question: “What does Hughes want the reader to understand about what happens to people whose dreams are repeatedly denied?”
