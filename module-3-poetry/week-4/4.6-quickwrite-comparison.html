Poetry — Week 4 — QW 4.6

QW 4.6: Two Poets, One Theme

5 points — pass/fail — submit as text entry

The Task

Write a paragraph of 5–7 sentences comparing how two of the three Week 4 poems handle the same theme (time, constraint, or deferred possibility). Your paragraph must include all three labeled elements below.

Required elements

[ANA] One sentence using anaphora — a repeated parallel structure that names what each of the two poets does (from Grammar 4.2). Example form: “Frost argues that… Hughes argues that…”

[LB] One sentence analyzing how a specific line break in one of the poems creates a particular effect — name whether it is enjambment or end-stopped, quote the lines in question, and explain what the break does (from Grammar 4.3).

[COMP] One sentence that states the key difference between the two poets’ treatments of the theme — not just that they are different, but what the difference reveals about each poet’s perspective or historical position.

Example Paragraph (Frost and Hughes on constraint)

Frost argues that constraint is a choice we make and then narrate to ourselves as destiny; Hughes argues that constraint is imposed from outside and can only end in explosion or erasure [ANA]. The difference is not just one of subject matter but of historical position: Frost’s speaker chooses between two nearly equal roads in a New England wood, while Hughes’s speaker describes a dream denied by a society, not a decision deferred by temperament [COMP]. Hughes’s enjambment on the line “Does it dry up” creates a suspended moment — the reader is stranded in mid-question, forced to move to the next line to complete the thought, which enacts the very deferral the poem describes [LB]. Both poets use formal choices that mirror their content: Frost’s end-stopped regularity enacts the deliberate pause of someone weighing a decision; Hughes’s accumulating questions enact the pressure of a dream that has run out of quiet ways to be denied. The comparison reveals that “constraint” is not a single experience but depends entirely on whether the one constrained has chosen the constraint or had it imposed.

Submission

Type your paragraph into the Canvas text entry box. Label each required element in bold brackets. Full credit (5 pts): All three elements present, correctly used, comparison makes a substantive claim about how the two poets differ.
