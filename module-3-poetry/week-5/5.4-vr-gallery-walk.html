VR 5.4: Module Gallery Walk

Visit two classmates’ compositions from across the module and leave a Reply Whiteboard IFX

What You’re Doing

Over five weeks, your class has built a persistent gallery across multiple Sessions. In this session, you will visit two classmates’ compositions from two different weeks (visit Sessions ELA7-M3-W1, W2, W3, or W4) and leave a Reply Whiteboard IFX beside each one. Each reply engages analytically with the composition you are responding to: what claim does it make? What do you agree with or find compelling? Where do you see something missing or differently?

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Sessions to visit: Any two of ELA7-M3-W1, ELA7-M3-W2, ELA7-M3-W3, ELA7-M3-W4 (Persistent Sessions from earlier weeks)
  • Goal: Find two different classmates’ compositions (not your own) and leave a Reply Whiteboard IFX beside each
What to Do
1

Choose your first composition

Join one of the Week 1–4 Persistent Sessions. Find a classmate’s composition that is not your own. Read the sticky notes and study the IFX arrangement. Take a moment to understand the analytical claim the composition makes before you respond.

2

Place a Reply Whiteboard IFX beside the composition

Place a Whiteboard IFX close enough to the composition that it is clearly a response to it. Title it: [YourName] REPLY. On the board, write: (1) one sentence stating what claim the composition makes about the poem (not just describing the IFX, but naming the interpretive argument), (2) one sentence using a noun clause starting with “What I find compelling about this composition is…” or “What this composition reveals that I hadn’t considered is…” (3) one sentence of genuine disagreement, extension, or gap: something you see differently or something the composition leaves out.

3

Take a snapshot of Reply 1

Take a snapshot showing both the original composition and your Reply Whiteboard IFX in the same frame. Name it YourLastName_M3_5.4a.

4

Repeat at a second composition in a different Session

Leave the first Session and join a different Week’s Session. Find a different classmate’s composition. Leave a second Reply Whiteboard IFX with the same three-part structure. Take a snapshot. Name it YourLastName_M3_5.4b.

5

Submit both snapshots

Upload YourLastName_M3_5.4a and YourLastName_M3_5.4b to this Canvas assignment.

Submission

Upload both snapshots. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students are visiting persistent compositions their classmates built in Weeks 1–4. The key assessment criterion is Part 3 of the Reply Whiteboard IFX: genuine disagreement, extension, or identification of a gap. Replies that only praise (“Great composition, I agree with everything”) are not doing analytical work. The noun clause requirement is the grammar integration point — look for “What I find compelling is…” or “What this composition reveals is…” in the snapshots. Facilitate peer engagement if students visit a Session and find it empty or sparsely populated — suggest they move to a different Session from a different week. Note: this is the culmination of the persistent composition work from the whole module. Remind students to treat others’ compositions with care and not to move or delete anyone else’s IFX.
