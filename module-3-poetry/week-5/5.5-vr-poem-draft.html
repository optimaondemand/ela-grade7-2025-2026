VR 5.5: Your Poem in Space

Stage your original poem as a composition and record yourself reading it aloud with brief craft commentary

What You’re Doing

You have a draft of your poem (or are working on one). In this session, you will stage its central image as a spatial composition, write the poem on a Whiteboard IFX, and record yourself reading it aloud followed by 60 seconds of spoken craft commentary. This recording is the spoken rough draft of the analysis section of the module project. The composed IFX stay in the Session as part of the class gallery.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M3-W5 — Persistent Session
  • Location: A location that feels appropriate to your poem’s subject (you may request a specific Location from your teacher in advance if none of the defaults fit)
  • Your poem: A working draft — it does not have to be finished, but you should have something to read
What to Do
1

Stage the poem’s central image (3–5 IFX)

Place 3–5 IFX that represent the central image of your poem. This is not illustration — it is interpretation. Ask: what is the poem about at its deepest level? Stage that, not the surface subject. Place your name sticky note: [Your Name] — “[Poem Title]” — Module 3 Project.

2

Write your poem on a Whiteboard IFX

Place a Whiteboard IFX beside the composition. Write your poem on it — with its actual line breaks. (If your poem is too long for the board, write the most important section.) The spatial arrangement of the poem on the board should preserve the white space you have chosen.

3

Record: poem reading + craft commentary (2–3 minutes total)

Start a spatial recording. Read your poem aloud at normal pace, standing in front of the Whiteboard IFX. Then — without stopping the recording — spend 60–90 seconds on craft commentary: (1) what the central image is and what you want it to do, (2) one formal choice you made deliberately (line break, stanza structure, a specific word choice) and why, (3) which Module 3 poem influenced your approach most, and how. This spoken commentary is the rough draft of your written craft analysis.

4

Submit the recording

Paste the spatial recording link into the Canvas submission text box. Name it YourLastName_M3_5.5. This recording will be linked in your module project submission.

Submission

Paste spatial recording link into Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students who have not yet drafted their poem should still enter VR and stage what they can — even a partial draft read aloud and reflected on is useful. The recording here serves as the spoken rough draft of the written craft analysis; students who are articulate in the recording about their formal choices will find the written analysis much easier. Watch for students who read the poem and then stop without offering craft commentary — prompt them: “Now tell me why you broke the line there” or “Which Module 3 poem were you thinking of when you wrote that line?” The IFX composition should stay in the Session as part of the gallery. Compositions that are left incomplete can be supplemented by the student later — the Session is persistent.
