## QUIZ DESCRIPTION (HTML — paste into Canvas quiz description field)

<p><strong>Module 4: <em>Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry</em></strong> by Mildred D. Taylor takes us into rural Mississippi in 1933 to follow the Logan family — Black landowners fighting to keep their 400 acres while the world around them works to take everything they have. Over five weeks you will read the entire novel, analyze Taylor's formal choices (point of view, setting, symbolism, characterization), build VR compositions tracking the novel's key scenes and characters, and write a literary analysis essay arguing how one of Taylor's craft decisions serves the novel's argument. The essential question driving this module: <em>How do people hold on to what is theirs when the world tries to take it from them?</em></p>

## SURVEY QUESTIONS

**Q1 — Essay Response — "Before you start"**
The essential question for this module is: *How do people hold on to what is theirs when the world tries to take it from them?*

Write 2–3 sentences giving your first answer to this question before you begin reading. There is no right answer yet — write what you actually think. You will answer this question again at the end of the module.

---

**Q2 — Multiple Choice — Reading history**
Which of the following is closest to what you know about the American South in the 1930s?

A) I have studied this period in depth and know the major events and social structures well.
B) I know some general things about this period — Jim Crow, segregation, the Depression — but not in detail.
C) I have heard of this period but don't know much about it.
D) This is unfamiliar to me.

*(No right answer — this helps your teacher know how much historical context to build into class discussion.)*

---

**Q3 — Multiple Choice — Grammar confidence**
Rate your current comfort with each of the following grammar concepts from Module 3:

Which of the following describes you best?

A) I feel solid on all Module 3 grammar (absolute phrases, relative clauses, noun clauses, anaphora) — ready to move on.
B) I'm solid on most Module 3 grammar but still shaky on one or two of them.
C) I'm still working through Module 3 grammar and would benefit from a review.
D) I struggled significantly with Module 3 grammar and need to review before building new structures.

---

**Q4 — Multiple Choice — VR experience**
In your Module 3 VR work, how would you rate your experience placing and arranging IFX to make an argument rather than just to decorate?

A) I feel very comfortable — I know how to use composition to argue something, not just to represent it.
B) I'm getting more comfortable but could use more practice connecting IFX choices to analytical claims.
C) I still find it challenging to think of VR arrangements as arguments rather than illustrations.
D) I had significant trouble with the VR work in Module 3.

---

**Q5 — Essay Response — Essay focus**
This module's project is a literary analysis essay about one of three formal choices Taylor made: the child narrator, the land as symbol, or setting as argument.

Which of these are you most curious about as of today — even if you haven't read anything yet? Write 1–2 sentences explaining why that option interests you. (If you've already read the novel, you may answer based on what you know.)
