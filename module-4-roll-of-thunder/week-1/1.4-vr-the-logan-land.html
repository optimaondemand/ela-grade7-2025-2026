Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 1 — VR 1.4

VR 1.4: The Logan Land

Stage what the land represents — not just what it looks like, but what it means

What You’re Doing

In this session you will build a spatial composition representing what the Logan land means in Chapters 1–3. This is not an illustration of a farm — it is an interpretation of a symbol. The IFX you place should represent what the land stands for: independence, dignity, the ability to refuse, the inheritance the Logans are determined to protect. Your analytical sticky note must use the grammar and vocabulary from this week.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M4-W1 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Rural Landscape
  • Reading: Chapters 1–3 of Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry (read before entering VR)
What to Do
1

Stage the Logan land (4–6 IFX)

Place 4–6 IFX that represent what the 400 acres mean in the context of the first three chapters. Do not just place a generic farm — ask yourself: What does owning this land allow the Logans to do that their neighbors cannot? What is threatened by the night riders? What does Big Ma mean when she talks about this land? Let your IFX answer those questions, not describe a field.

2

Place your name sticky note

Add a sticky note: [Your Name] — “The Logan Land” — Module 4 Week 1

3

Place an analytical sticky note

Write 2–3 sentences explaining your IFX choices. Your note must include: one participial phrase (underline it and label it [PART]) and the word POINT OF VIEW or DRAMATIC IRONY used analytically (underline and label [VOCAB]).

Example of what to aim for: “Knowing that the land can be lost, the Logans hold it with a determination [PART: Knowing that the land can be lost] invisible to Cassie but felt by every adult in the novel. From Cassie’s point of view [VOCAB: POINT OF VIEW], the land is simply home — the reader understands it is also the family’s protection against everything the world wants to take.”

4

Take a snapshot and submit

Take a snapshot showing your full composition and both sticky notes. Name it YourLastName_M4_1.4. Upload to Canvas.

Submission

Upload snapshot to Canvas. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Watch for IFX that only illustrate (a generic farm, some trees) without interpreting (what this land means to this family, in this world, in 1933 Mississippi). Prompt: “If someone who hadn’t read the novel walked through your composition, what would they understand about why this land matters?” The participial phrase requirement drives students toward action-oriented description rather than static inventory. In the sticky note, flag phrases that are too plot-based (“This IFX represents their house”) and redirect toward analysis (“This IFX represents what owning the house makes possible”).
