Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 1 — VR 1.5

VR 1.5: Before and After

Stage the contrast between the Logans’ world and the threat pressing in on it — and what does not change

What You’re Doing

Still in Session ELA7-M4-W1, you will create a two-part composition in a new area of the same Location. One side shows the Logans’ world before the immediate threat materializes — the family, the land, the ordinary life. The other side shows the threat the night riders represent. Then you will use a Whiteboard IFX to write what changes and — crucially — what does NOT change. Record yourself walking through both sides and explaining the contrast aloud.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M4-W1 — Persistent Session (same as VR 1.4)
  • Move to a new area of the Location so your 1.5 composition is separate from your 1.4 composition
What to Do
1

Create a two-part composition

Place IFX on two sides or in two zones: one side represents the Logans’ world and what they hold (family, land, dignity, ordinary life). The other represents the threat the night riders bring. The two sides should be visually distinct but clearly in conversation with each other — arranged so a viewer can see both at once.

2

Place a Whiteboard IFX

Write two sentences on the Whiteboard: (1) What changes when the night riders come? (2) What does NOT change — what do the Logans hold on to? The second sentence is the more important one. It must use a participial phrase (label it [PART]) and the word DRAMATIC IRONY in analytical context (label it [VOCAB]).

3

Record: composition walk-through (60–90 seconds)

Start a spatial recording. Walk through both sides of your composition. Explain what each side represents and then explain your Whiteboard answer: what does the threat change, and what does the Logan family still hold? Name one specific moment from Chapters 1–3 as evidence.

4

Submit the recording

Paste the spatial recording link into Canvas. Name it YourLastName_M4_1.5.

Submission

Paste spatial recording link into Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The “what does NOT change” question is the key one — it drives toward the novel’s argument rather than just the plot event. Students who only describe the threat (the burning, the night riders) miss the point; ask them: “What are the Logans still holding at the end of Chapter 3?” Strong answers will name something like dignity, land ownership, the refusal to accept the damaged textbooks, or family solidarity. The Whiteboard sentence forces that articulation in writing before the recording makes it oral. Students who struggle to articulate the “what does not change” answer in writing will also struggle to say it — the Whiteboard is the rehearsal space.
