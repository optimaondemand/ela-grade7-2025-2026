7th Grade ELA — Module 4
Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 1 — Quickwrite 1.6

QW 1.6: Land and Freedom

5–7 sentences of analytical writing about what the land means in Chapters 1–3

The Prompt

In 5–7 sentences, explain what the Logan land means in the first three chapters. This is not a summary of the plot — you are making an analytical claim about what the land represents and what Taylor’s opening chapters ask us to understand about it.

Required Tags

Your response must include the following, clearly labeled in brackets:

  • [PART] — One participial phrase used to open or embed a sentence (label it in the text)
  • [POV] — One specific observation about how Cassie’s point of view shapes or limits what we understand in the opening chapters
  • [SYM] — One statement about what the land symbolizes — not what it is, but what it stands for in the novel’s argument
Example (from a different novel — do not copy this)

“In Steinbeck’s The Grapes of Wrath, the Joad family’s land is not simply property — it is identity and continuity. [SYM: the land stands for who they are, not just where they live.] Forced off the land by banks and tractors [PART], the Joads lose not only their home but the organizing principle of their lives. From Tom Joad’s point of view [POV], the land is something that was taken by a system too large and impersonal to fight — but Steinbeck’s narration reveals the scale of what has been destroyed in ways Tom cannot fully see.”

Submission

Type your response directly into the Canvas text box. Label your tags in brackets as shown above. 5 points, graded on completion and required tags present.
