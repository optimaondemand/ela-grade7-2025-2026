**Q1.** At the beginning of Chapter 1, Little Man refuses to accept his textbook. What does this act reveal about his character?

A) He is spoiled and difficult to please in school.
B) He is afraid of getting in trouble with the teacher and wants a different excuse.
C) He refuses to accept being treated as second-rate — the book's stamped record of its depreciation tells him exactly what the county thinks of Black students, and he will not pretend otherwise.
D) He has already read that textbook and wants a more advanced one.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q2.** The Logan family owns 400 acres of land in a community where most Black families are sharecroppers. What does this distinction mean for the family's situation?

A) It means they are wealthier than their neighbors and live more comfortably.
B) Owning the land outright means they cannot be evicted at will, they are not dependent on a white landowner's decisions, and they have the economic basis to refuse demands that sharecroppers cannot. The land is the material foundation of their independence.
C) It means they must pay higher taxes and deal with more paperwork than their neighbors.
D) It means they are envied by their neighbors and have no close friendships in the community.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q3.** When Cassie describes the night riders' visit in the opening chapters, she reports what happened without fully understanding its significance. What is the literary term for this effect?

A) Foreshadowing — the night riders hint at what will happen later in the novel.
B) Flashback — Cassie is remembering the event from the future.
C) Allusion — Taylor is referencing real historical events.
D) Dramatic irony — the reader understands the danger and terror of the night riders more fully than Cassie does, because her age and experience limit what she can grasp.

**Answer: D**

---

**Q4.** Which of the following best explains what "point of view" means as a literary term, as used in this module?

A) Point of view means the author's personal opinion about the events in the novel.
B) Point of view is the physical position a character stands in when watching a scene unfold.
C) Point of view refers to who is telling the story, what they can observe, and what lies outside their knowledge or understanding — it shapes what information the reader receives and how.
D) Point of view means the moral perspective the novel teaches, such as "justice matters" or "family is important."

**Answer: C**

**Q5.** Identify the participial phrase in this sentence: "Carrying the burned textbook back to the teacher, Little Man kept his chin high."

A) Little Man kept his chin high
B) back to the teacher
C) Carrying the burned textbook back to the teacher
D) his chin high

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** What is the key difference between a participial phrase and an absolute phrase?

A) A participial phrase uses -ing verbs; an absolute phrase uses -ed or -en verbs.
B) A participial phrase modifies a specific noun in the sentence and has no subject of its own; an absolute phrase has its own noun subject and modifies the whole sentence.
C) A participial phrase always comes at the beginning of a sentence; an absolute phrase always comes at the end.
D) There is no real difference — they are two names for the same structure.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** Which sentence contains a dangling participle (an error)?

A) Frightened by the noise, Cassie pressed against the wall.
B) Knowing the danger, Papa kept his voice calm.
C) Running down the road, the trees blurred past Cassie.
D) Warned by her mother, Cassie kept her thoughts to herself.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** Taylor sets the novel in 1933 Mississippi, where Black families faced specific legal, economic, and social conditions. Why does this historical context matter for understanding the Logan land?

A) It shows that the 1930s were a simpler time when families were closer to the land.
B) It explains why the Logans are so prosperous — the Depression made land affordable for everyone.
C) It establishes that land ownership by Black families was both rare and contested — the system was designed to prevent it, which is why the Logans' 400 acres represents more than property. It represents defiance of what the system intended.
D) It shows that the conflict in the novel is unique to one family and one time period.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** How does Mildred Taylor use Cassie's age and limited experience to create tension in the opening chapters?

A) Cassie's limited experience makes her an unreliable narrator who gets the facts wrong.
B) Cassie's age means she cannot understand school, so her descriptions of school are inaccurate.
C) Cassie is so young that she does not care what happens to her family, which makes the reader anxious on her behalf.
D) Cassie accurately reports frightening events — the night riders, the burned books, the adults' tension — without fully understanding their significance, creating dramatic irony: the reader feels dread that Cassie herself does not yet feel.

**Answer: D**

---

**Q10.** Which sentence uses an opening participial phrase correctly?

A) Frightened by the men on horseback, the lantern was quickly put out by Papa.
B) Knowing that the mortgage was due, the railroad work kept Papa away most of the year.
C) Reaching for her mother's hand, Cassie felt the warmth of someone who was not going to explain everything but who was not going to leave her either.
D) Running through the fields, the cotton was wet with morning dew.

**Answer: C**
