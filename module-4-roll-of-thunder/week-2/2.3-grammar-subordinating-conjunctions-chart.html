Subordinating Conjunctions: A Precision Chart

Choosing the right conjunction changes the meaning of your sentence

Why It Matters

Not all subordinating conjunctions are interchangeable. The one you choose determines the logical relationship between the two clauses. In analytical writing, a wrong conjunction doesn’t just sound awkward — it makes a different claim than you intended. “The boycott succeeded because the community held together” is a causal claim. “The boycott succeeded although the community faced pressure” is a concessive claim. Both may be true, but they make different arguments.

The Precision Chart
Relationship Conjunctions Example (novel context)
Cause
(X happened, so Y happened)		 because • since • as “Because the night riders targeted families who stepped out of line, Papa had to calculate every risk.”
Contrast
(X, but surprisingly Y)		 although • even though • whereas • while “Although T.J. kept getting Stacey into trouble, Stacey did not stop calling him a friend.”
Time
(X happened at this point)		 when • while • after • before • until • as soon as “Before Cassie went to Strawberry, she had never been told directly where the town thought she belonged.”
Condition
(Y only if X)		 if • unless “Unless the community stood together, the boycott would collapse under pressure.”
Concession
(X is true, but still Y)		 even though • though • granted that “Even though Big Ma’s silence protected Cassie from worse harm, it still left Cassie feeling alone.”
Exercise 1: Choose the Right Conjunction

For each sentence pair, two conjunction options are given. Choose the one that correctly captures the logical relationship and explain in one sentence why the other doesn’t work.

  1. The Logans drove to Vicksburg to buy supplies. They lost the convenience of shopping locally. [because / although]
  2. Mama organized the boycott. The community faced real economic risk. [even though / when]
  3. Mr. Barnett ignored the Logan children. They had been waiting patiently. [since / although]
  4. Cassie did not fully understand why Big Ma stayed silent. She remembered it for a long time afterward. [although / because]
  5. The boycott had a chance to succeed. Everyone in the community participated. [unless / if]
