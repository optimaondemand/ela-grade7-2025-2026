VR 2.4: Power in Space

Stage the Strawberry scene — who has power, who yields, and how the setting enforces the order

What You’re Doing

In this session you will stage the power relationships in the Strawberry chapters as a spatial composition. Power is not just a feeling in this novel — it is enforced through physical space: who is served first, who must step aside, who occupies the center and who is pushed to the edge. Your IFX arrangement should make that hierarchy visible.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M4-W2 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Town or City Street
  • Reading: Chapters 4–7, especially the Strawberry scenes, completed before entering VR
What to Do
1

Stage the power hierarchy (4–6 IFX)

Use IFX to show the power relationships in Chapters 4–7 — who has power, who is forced to yield, and how the town’s social order is enforced through space. Think about: who is at the center of your composition and who is at the margins? Who is elevated and who is pushed down? The physical arrangement of your IFX should argue something about how power works in Strawberry.

Facilitator Note

The key insight is that power is spatial — it determines who occupies space freely and who must yield. Students who arrange their IFX in a flat row are missing the hierarchy; prompt them: “Which IFX is at the center? Which ones have to move out of the way? Show the social order in the physical arrangement.” Students who place Mr. Barnett or Mr. Simms at the center and the Logan children at the margins are on the right track. The analytical sticky note should name the setting specifically (Strawberry, the store, the sidewalk) rather than just the power dynamic in the abstract. Watch for sticky notes that use SETTING merely as a location word rather than as an analytical concept.
