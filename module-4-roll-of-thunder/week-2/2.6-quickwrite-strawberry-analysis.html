7th Grade ELA — Module 4
Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 2 — Quickwrite 2.6

QW 2.6: Strawberry as Setting

5–7 sentences arguing what the Strawberry chapters reveal about the novel’s central concern

The Prompt

In 5–7 sentences, argue what the Strawberry chapters reveal about the novel’s central concern: holding on to what is yours. Focus on setting — how does Taylor use the town itself to show what the Logan family is up against? This is not a summary. You are making an argument about what Strawberry’s setting does in the novel.

Required Tags
  • [ADV] — One adverbial clause (because, although, since, when, even though, or another subordinating conjunction). Label it in the text.
  • [SET] — One analytical use of the word SETTING. Not “the setting of the scene is Strawberry” — but a sentence where SETTING carries analytical weight: what the setting of Strawberry argues, reveals, or enforces.
  • [SIL] — One sentence specifically about Big Ma’s silence. What does it mean? What does Taylor want us to understand by writing the scene this way?
Submission

Type your response directly into the Canvas text box. Label your tags in brackets. 5 points, graded on completion and required tags present.
