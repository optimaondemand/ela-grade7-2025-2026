7th Grade ELA — Module 4
Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 3 — Lesson 3.1

Uncle Hammer and the Foil

Chapters 8–10: two brothers, one value, two very different ways of holding on to it

Warm-Up

Think of two people who want the same thing but pursue it in completely different ways — one patient and strategic, the other immediate and confrontational. What does comparing them reveal about what they value? Write 2–3 sentences before reading.

The Lesson

Uncle Hammer arrives for Christmas driving a Packard that matches Harlan Granger’s car, dressed sharply, carrying himself with the confidence of a man who answers to no one in the South because he left the South years ago. He is everything Papa is not on the surface — and exactly the same underneath. Both brothers refuse to accept the judgment that they are lesser. Both brothers would die before they accepted that verdict. What differs is strategy.

When Hammer hears what happened to Cassie in Strawberry, his instinct is immediate: go find the Simms boy, right now, tonight. Papa talks him down — not because Papa disagrees, but because Papa is calculating the cost. If Hammer hurts a white man in Mississippi in 1933, Hammer will be killed. The land will be under more pressure. The family will be in greater danger. Papa’s patience is not submission. It is a longer game.

But Taylor does not let Papa be simply right and Hammer simply wrong. Hammer’s fury is the honest response to what the world is doing — it is the human response to being told that Cassie deserved what happened to her. Papa’s patience requires suppressing that honest response, and Taylor shows us what that suppression costs. The novel holds both men with respect. It doesn’t resolve the question of which approach is right, because it isn’t that kind of novel.

T.J., meanwhile, drifts. He has been Stacey’s best friend but has spent Chapters 4–7 getting Stacey in trouble. Now he begins spending time with the Simms brothers — white boys his own age who find him useful. T.J. wants things he sees others have. The Logans have it (dignity, solidarity, a family that holds) and he doesn’t quite know how to value what they have. Instead he wants the visible things: a gun, a coat, status. This is the setup for everything that follows.

Reading

Read Chapters 8–10 of Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry.

Mark: (1) every scene where Hammer and Papa differ in their response to the same situation, (2) any moment where Taylor seems to show the cost of Papa’s patience, (3) T.J.’s choices and what he seems to be looking for.

Vocabulary

Foil

A character whose qualities contrast with another character’s, highlighting both by comparison. Foils are not opposites — they usually share a core value or desire. What differs is method, temperament, or circumstance. Uncle Hammer and Papa Logan are foils: they share the same refusal to accept degradation, but Hammer acts on impulse and Papa acts on calculation. Comparing them reveals more about each than either could show alone.

Copia Exercise

Example: “As Papa’s foil, Uncle Hammer shows what Papa’s strategy costs — the suppression of an honest anger that Hammer never has to suppress.”

Rewrite this sentence using the word foil, applying it to two characters of your choice from the novel — or to Hammer and Papa in a different way than the example.

Characterization

The techniques a writer uses to develop a character: what they say, what they do, what others say about them, and what the narrator reveals. Taylor characterizes Uncle Hammer primarily through action and dialogue — we learn who he is by watching what he does and hearing how he speaks, not by being told. She characterizes T.J. partly through contrast with the Logan children: what T.J. does with his freedom reveals what he values.

Copia Exercise

Example: “Taylor’s characterization of T.J. relies heavily on contrast: showing what he does with his choices beside what Stacey does with the same choices.”

Rewrite this sentence using the word characterization, applying it to Uncle Hammer or to a different character in Chapters 8–10.

Comprehension Check

1. Why does Papa stop Uncle Hammer from going after the Simms boy? What is Papa calculating?

Write 2–3 sentences. Be specific about what Papa is protecting and what it would cost if Hammer acted.

2. What does Uncle Hammer’s Packard car signify? Why does Taylor give him a car that matches Harlan Granger’s?

Write 2 sentences in your own words.

3. In Chapters 8–10, what does T.J. want that the Logans have, and what does he do to try to get it?

Write 2–3 sentences. Be specific.
