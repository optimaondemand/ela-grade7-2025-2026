VR 3.4: The Foil — Papa and Hammer

Two compositions, in dialogue — stage what the foil reveals about how to hold on

What You’re Doing

In this session you will create two compositions — one for Papa, one for Uncle Hammer — staged so they are clearly in dialogue with each other. You are not illustrating the characters. You are arguing, through spatial arrangement, what the contrast between them reveals about how Taylor thinks about resistance to injustice. Neither composition should look “better” than the other — Taylor does not resolve this foil, and neither should you.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M4-W3 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Forest or Rural Night
  • Reading: Chapters 8–10 completed before entering VR
What to Do
1

Create two compositions (3–4 IFX each), facing each other

Place your Papa composition and your Hammer composition so they are clearly in dialogue — facing each other, side by side, or otherwise arranged so a viewer sees both at once. Use IFX to represent each man’s approach to injustice: not their physical appearance, but their emotional register, their strategy, the cost they pay. Ask: What does Papa’s patience require him to suppress? What does Hammer’s directness cost him? Let the IFX answer those questions.

2

Place label sticky notes

One sticky note near each composition identifying whose it is and the key quality you are representing: [Your Name] — Papa: [key quality] and [Your Name] — Hammer: [key quality].

3

Place a central analytical sticky note

Between the two compositions, place a sticky note with one compound-complex sentence (label it [CC]) that explains what the two IFX arrangements together argue about Taylor’s view of resistance. The word FOIL must appear.

4

Take two snapshots and submit

One snapshot showing the Papa composition with its label, one showing the Hammer composition with its label (both snapshots should include the central analytical sticky note if possible). Name them YourLastName_M4_3.4a and YourLastName_M4_3.4b. Upload both to Canvas.

Submission

Upload both snapshots to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The key criterion is that neither arrangement should look obviously “better” than the other. Taylor doesn’t resolve the foil, and neither should the student. If one composition appears clearly superior — larger, more central, better lit — ask: “Does Taylor think Hammer is wrong? What evidence do you have? Does Taylor think Papa is right? What does his patience cost?” The analytical sticky note should make a compound-complex claim that holds both men without judging either as simply correct. Watch for sticky notes that use FOIL as a label (“Papa is a foil of Hammer”) rather than as an analytical tool (“As foils, Papa and Hammer together argue that resistance to injustice can take more than one form and that each form exacts a different kind of cost”).
