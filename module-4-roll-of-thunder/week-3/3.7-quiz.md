**Q1.** When Uncle Hammer arrives for Christmas driving a Packard car that matches Harlan Granger's, what does Taylor want readers to understand?

A) Hammer is reckless with money and doesn't understand the danger he's creating.
B) Hammer is showing off for the Logan children, who are impressed by expensive cars.
C) Hammer's Packard is Taylor's characterization of his refusal to accept a second-class position — he will not own a lesser car to make white men more comfortable, and the danger it creates for him in Mississippi is part of the point.
D) The Packard is a borrowed car that doesn't actually belong to Hammer.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q2.** Why does Papa stop Uncle Hammer from going after the Simms that night?

A) Papa thinks Cassie deserved what happened in Strawberry and doesn't want Hammer to fight over it.
B) Papa is afraid of conflict and prefers to avoid trouble whenever possible.
C) Papa stops Hammer because the calculation is brutally simple: in 1933 Mississippi, a Black man who assaults a white man will be killed, and the family will be left more vulnerable — Papa's patience is not acceptance, it is strategy and survival.
D) Papa doesn't know the full story of what happened to Cassie yet.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** In what sense are Papa and Uncle Hammer foils rather than simply opposites?

A) They are foils because they look alike physically but have different personalities.
B) They are foils because one is good and the other is bad — Papa is right, Hammer is wrong.
C) They share the same core refusal to accept that they are lesser, but their strategies for acting on that refusal differ completely — patience and calculation versus immediacy and confrontation. Comparing them shows what each approach costs.
D) They are foils because Hammer lives in the North and Papa lives in the South, so they have completely different lives.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** What does T.J.'s choice to pursue friendship with the Simms brothers reveal about his characterization?

A) T.J. is simply mischievous — all boys his age would make the same choices.
B) T.J.'s drift toward the Simms brothers reveals a pattern of choosing what is immediately appealing (status, material things, approval) over what has genuine value (loyalty, protection, integrity) — and Taylor shows this through his actions across multiple chapters, not just one mistake.
C) T.J. dislikes the Logan family and is deliberately trying to hurt them.
D) T.J. is forced by circumstances to befriend the Simms brothers because he has no other options.

**Answer: B**

**Q5.** Which of the following is a compound-complex sentence?

A) Papa held his ground, and Hammer wanted more.
B) Although Hammer wanted to confront the Wallaces, Papa held him back.
C) Although Hammer wanted to confront the Simms, Papa held him back, and the night ended without the violence that would have destroyed the family.
D) Papa held his ground quietly.

**Answer: C**

**Q6.** A student writes: "As Papa's foil, Hammer shows what Papa's strategy costs — the suppression of an honest anger that Hammer never has to suppress." What makes this a good use of the term FOIL?

A) It uses the word correctly as a verb: to foil means to defeat.
B) It uses FOIL as an analytical concept — it names a specific quality revealed by the contrast (the suppressed anger) that wouldn't be as visible without the comparison.
C) It correctly identifies that foils always show that one character is right and the other is wrong.
D) It uses FOIL as a label to say that Hammer is Papa's opposite in every way.

**Answer: B**

**Q7.** What is the difference between CHARACTERIZATION as a literary term and simply describing what a character does?

A) There is no difference — characterization just means describing a character's behavior.
B) Characterization is the set of techniques a writer uses to develop a character — what they say, do, what others say about them, what the narrator reveals — and analyzing it means identifying which technique is being used and what it reveals about the character's inner life or function in the novel's argument.
C) Characterization refers only to the character's physical description.
D) Characterization is the backstory a character has before the novel begins.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q8.** What does Stacey's coat episode (T.J. convincing Stacey the coat was too big, then wearing it himself) reveal about the dynamic between them?

A) Stacey is foolish for giving T.J. his coat and deserves what happens.
B) T.J. is simply a thief who will take anything that isn't nailed down.
C) The coat episode shows T.J.'s pattern — using Stacey's trust and loyalty against him — while also showing Stacey's genuine affection for T.J. that makes him vulnerable to that pattern. Both things are true at once.
D) The coat is a minor incident that has no significance for the rest of the novel.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** Identify the compound-complex sentence. Label each clause: S (subordinate) or I (independent).

"Although Big Ma understood the danger better than Cassie did, she could not explain it that night, and Cassie carried the silence home with her for a long time."

A) This is a complex sentence — it has one subordinate and one independent clause.
B) This is a compound sentence — it has two independent clauses joined by "and."
C) This is a compound-complex sentence: "Although Big Ma understood..." = S; "she could not explain it that night" = I; "Cassie carried the silence home with her for a long time" = I.
D) This is a simple sentence with a long subject.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** Taylor does not tell readers whether Papa's approach or Hammer's is correct. What does this narrative choice suggest about her argument?

A) Taylor is uncertain about her own position and hasn't thought it through.
B) Taylor is suggesting that resistance to injustice is not one-size-fits-all — both approaches have real costs and real value, and reducing the question to one right answer would be dishonest about how hard it actually is to live under these conditions.
C) Taylor thinks both approaches are wrong and that the Logans should simply accept their situation.
D) Taylor favors Hammer but doesn't want to be too obvious about it.

**Answer: B**
