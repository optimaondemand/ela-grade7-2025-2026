Concessive Sentences

Acknowledging the strongest objection before answering it — how to make your argument more honest and more powerful

What a Concessive Sentence Does

A concessive sentence acknowledges the strongest objection to your argument before answering it. Far from weakening your position, this makes it stronger — it shows you have actually thought about the other side, rather than pretending it doesn’t exist.

Pattern: [Concession + your claim]

Opening words that signal a concession:

Even though • Although • While it is true that • Despite the fact that • Granted that • Admittedly

Examples:

“Even though T.J. made his own choices and cannot be excused for them, Taylor makes clear that the world he grew up in set him up to fail.”
“Although Papa burns part of what he has fought to protect, the act is not defeat — it is the exercise of the very freedom the land represents.”
“Granted that Big Ma’s silence hurt Cassie, it may also have protected her from something worse.”

     = the concession (what you grant to the other side)

Exercise 1: Identify Concession and Claim

In each sentence, underline the concession and double-underline the main claim. Then write one sentence explaining what the concession grants and what the claim argues.

  1. “Even though the boycott created hardship for the Logans, it was the one form of resistance available to the community that didn’t require direct physical confrontation.”
  2. “Although Uncle Hammer’s anger is the more honest response to what happened to Cassie, Papa’s patience is the more survivable one.”
  3. “While T.J. made choices that hurt people who cared about him, Taylor’s characterization of him suggests that his circumstances made those choices almost inevitable.”
Exercise 2: Write a Concessive Sentence About the Ending

Write a concessive sentence about the novel’s ending. Your sentence should acknowledge what is lost in the final chapter, and then state what is preserved or demonstrated by the same events. Use one of the concession openers from the list above.
