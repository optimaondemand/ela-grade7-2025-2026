**Q1.** In Chapter 12, Papa sets his own cotton field on fire. What is his purpose, and what does this act cost?

A) Papa is burning the cotton accidentally while trying to signal for help.
B) Papa sets the fire to distract the white community from carrying out a lynching of T.J. — the fire demands everyone's attention to protect their own crops, which is more urgent in the moment than continuing to pursue T.J. What it costs is part of the land the family has worked and sacrificed to hold.
C) Papa burns the cotton as a protest against the injustice of the night riders burning other families' property.
D) Papa burns the cotton because it was a bad harvest and it was not worth anything anyway.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** T.J. is not saved from all consequences by the fire — he will almost certainly face jail. Why does Taylor end the novel this way rather than with a complete rescue?

A) Taylor forgot to write a rescue scene and the novel ends abruptly.
B) Taylor wants readers to feel hopeless about the possibility of justice.
C) The novel would be dishonest if it resolved T.J.'s situation cleanly — the injustice of T.J.'s fate is part of the novel's argument, and a tidy rescue would suggest that the system can be easily circumvented, which is not what Taylor wants to say.
D) T.J. is guilty of the crime and deserves to go to jail.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** The novel ends with Cassie crying without fully understanding why. What does this ending reveal about the novel's argument?

A) Cassie's tears show she is a sensitive child who cries easily.
B) The ending reveals that the novel's events were too sad to have a clear resolution.
C) Cassie's incomplete understanding at the end is Taylor's final use of the dramatic irony that has structured the whole novel: readers understand what Cassie is mourning (a world that should be different, a friend who is lost, a family that survived but at great cost) even as Cassie feels the weight without being able to name it fully.
D) The ending is left ambiguous because Taylor didn't know how to conclude the novel.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** Which of the following is a rhetorical question?

A) "What chapter does T.J. get arrested?"
B) "How many acres do the Logans own?"
C) "If Papa burned his own land to save T.J., what does that tell you about what holding the land really meant?"
D) "Who is the author of Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry?"

**Answer: C**

---

**Q5.** What makes a rhetorical question different from a genuine question in analytical writing?

A) Rhetorical questions are always shorter than genuine questions.
B) A rhetorical question is asked for effect — the answer is implied or deliberately left open as the argument's point — while a genuine question asks for factual information the writer doesn't have.
C) Rhetorical questions are only used in persuasive speeches, not in literary essays.
D) A rhetorical question has no question mark.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q6.** Which sentence is a correct concessive sentence?

A) Even though Papa burned the cotton. The family survived.
B) Papa burned the cotton, because the family survived.
C) Even though Papa burned part of the land that had defined the family's independence, the act demonstrated the very freedom that land was meant to protect — the ability to choose what to sacrifice and when.
D) Papa burned the cotton even though. He chose sacrifice.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q7.** What is the difference between a THEME and a TOPIC in a literary analysis essay?

A) A theme is the same as a topic — they are interchangeable terms.
B) A topic names a subject area (land, racial injustice, family); a theme is a specific claim or argument the novel makes about that subject (holding on to dignity sometimes requires spending what you have been protecting).
C) A theme is the author's biography; a topic is the novel's plot.
D) A topic is more important than a theme in literary analysis.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q8.** Throughout the novel, the Logan land appears at every crucial moment — when the family faces threats, when they choose resistance, and finally when Papa burns it. What literary term describes this recurring pattern?

A) Foreshadowing — the land hints at future danger.
B) Characterization — the land reveals character through action.
C) Motif — the land is a recurring image that gathers meaning each time it appears, reinforcing the novel's central theme.
D) Setting — the land establishes where the novel takes place.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** A student argues that Papa's fire is ultimately a defeat because he loses part of the land. Write the best concessive response to this argument.

A) The student is completely right — any loss is a defeat.
B) The student is completely wrong — there is no loss in the fire at all.
C) Even though Papa does lose part of the cotton crop — a real and painful cost — the fire is not a defeat because it demonstrates exactly what the land was meant to protect: the freedom to choose how and when to act with dignity.
D) Papa doesn't actually lose anything significant — the crop can be replanted.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** Looking at the novel as a whole: what is the most specific statement you can make about Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry's central theme?

A) The theme is that racism is bad and families should stay together.
B) The theme is about land ownership in Mississippi in the 1930s.
C) The theme is that holding on to what is yours — dignity, land, solidarity — does not mean hoarding it but knowing when spending it in the service of something larger is the right thing to do.
D) The theme is that children should listen to their parents because parents know best.

**Answer: C**
