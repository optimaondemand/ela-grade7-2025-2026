7th Grade ELA — Module 4 — VR Assignment
Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry — Week 5 — VR 5.4

VR 5.4: Gallery Walk — Classmates’ Compositions

Visit two classmates’ Module 4 compositions and respond analytically to what you find

What You’re Doing

You have spent four weeks building VR compositions about Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry. So have your classmates. In this session you will visit the persistent sessions from two of your classmates and look at one composition each — then leave a sticky note responding to what you see. Seeing how another reader staged the same novel gives you a new angle on your own essay argument.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session type: Persistent — join classmates’ sessions by Session ID
  • Session IDs: Your teacher will post two classmate Session IDs to Canvas. Find them in the Canvas announcement before joining. (If you are working asynchronously and no IDs are posted yet, ask your teacher via Canvas message for two Session IDs to visit.)
  • No reading required — this is observation work. Bring your own essay focus question with you.
What to Do
1

Join the first classmate’s session

Use the Session ID your teacher posted. Move through their compositions — they may have work from VR 1.4, 2.4, 3.4, 4.4, 4.5, or others. Pick one composition that you find interesting, surprising, or that you disagree with. Spend at least 3–4 minutes studying it closely.

2

Leave a response sticky note at Classmate 1’s composition

Place a sticky note near the composition. Write your name and two sentences: (1) one thing this arrangement argues that you hadn’t considered — stated as a specific analytical claim, not just “I liked this”; (2) one question this arrangement raises for your own essay. Label your sentences [OBS] (observation) and [Q] (question).

3

Repeat at the second classmate’s session

Join the second Session ID and repeat the process: find one composition, study it, leave a labeled sticky note ([OBS] and [Q]).

4

Take a screenshot and submit a written reflection

Take one screenshot in each classmate’s session showing your sticky note in context. Then, in the Canvas text box, write 3–4 sentences: What did you see across the two compositions that surprised you? Did either composition change how you’re thinking about your essay argument? Name the classmate and the composition.

Submission

Upload both screenshots to Canvas (YourLastName_M4_5.4a and YourLastName_M4_5.4b) plus your written reflection in the Canvas text box. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Post Session IDs for two students whose compositions represent meaningfully different interpretations — for instance, one who focused on the fire as sacrifice and one who focused on the land as motif, or one whose T.J. station treated him as tragedy and one whose treated him as foil. The goal is not agreement but productive encounter with a different reading. If you have a student working asynchronously, give them two Session IDs from earlier in the week when the session is still active. The written reflection is a Tier 1 completion grade; the analytical substance of the sticky notes is what the student carries into VR 5.5.
