## Discussion Prompt

**The Essential Question one last time:**

> How do people hold on to what is theirs when the world tries to take it from them?

You have spent five weeks reading, annotating, building, and arguing about *Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry*. Now answer the essential question directly — not as a plot summary and not as a theme statement, but as your own argument about what Taylor's novel taught you that you didn't fully understand before you read it.

**Your post must do three things:**

1. **State a specific claim** about what the novel argues — not "the Logans hold on to their land" but what *holding on* means and costs and requires. One to two sentences.

2. **Name one formal choice** Taylor made (child narrator, land as symbol, or setting as argument) and explain *in one sentence* how that choice enforces the claim you just made.

3. **End with a question** for your classmates — a genuine question about something in the novel or in the essential question that you still find unresolved or worth arguing about. Not a rhetorical question. A real question that you want someone to answer.

**Your reply to one classmate** must do two things: (1) respond directly to their question with your own position and at least one piece of evidence from the novel; (2) extend or complicate their original claim — add something they didn't say, or name one place where you see it differently.

---

## Grading Rubric

**15 points — Substantive:** Original post meets all three requirements with specific, analytical language. Reply engages directly with classmate's question and adds something genuine to the argument.

**8 points — Present but thin:** Post is present but does not meet one or more of the three requirements, or the reply is a general agreement without added analytical substance ("Great point, I agree!").

**0 points — Missing:** No post, or a post that does not address the essential question.
