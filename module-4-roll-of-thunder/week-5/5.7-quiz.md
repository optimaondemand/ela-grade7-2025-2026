**Q1.** Taylor chose a child narrator for *Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry* rather than an adult narrator. What specific effect does this choice create that an adult narrator could not?

A) A child narrator makes the novel more exciting by creating action-focused scenes.
B) A child narrator means the reader gets more historical information and context because children ask more questions.
C) A child narrator creates dramatic irony: Cassie's genuine, limited understanding of what she experiences leaves the reader holding both Cassie's authentic perspective and a larger understanding of what that experience actually means — a gap the novel uses as its central literary engine.
D) A child narrator makes the novel easier to read because the vocabulary is simpler.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q2.** What does the Logan family's 400 acres of land represent throughout the novel — not just literally, but symbolically?

A) The land represents the family's financial wealth and economic security.
B) The land is a reminder of slavery and functions as a symbol of historical trauma.
C) The land represents the physical, material proof that the Logans cannot be entirely erased — it is the record of what they have sacrificed, survived, and held, making it the novel's central symbol of dignity and the right to exist on one's own terms.
D) The land represents the American Dream and shows that hard work can overcome racism.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** In the novel's final chapter, Cassie cries without fully understanding why. How does this ending function analytically?

A) The ending shows that Cassie is overwhelmed by the evening's events and needs to rest.
B) The ending is Taylor's way of avoiding a difficult political statement about the justice system.
C) The ending demonstrates that the novel's resolution is meant to be ambiguous — the land is still there, T.J. is going to jail, the family is intact but diminished. Taylor refuses to resolve things cleanly because the history the novel depicts did not resolve cleanly.
D) The ending shows that Cassie finally understands everything that has happened, but her grief is too large for words.

**Answer: C**

**Q4.** Which of the following best identifies the literary term that describes the land's recurring appearance at every crucial moment in the novel?

A) Symbol — the land stands for something else throughout the novel.
B) Theme — the land states the novel's central argument.
C) Foil — the land contrasts with T.J.'s desire for material things.
D) Motif — the land is a recurring image that accumulates meaning each time it appears, reinforcing the theme by connecting all the major events to the same central idea.

**Answer: D**

**Q5.** Which of the following is the best thesis statement for an essay about Taylor's use of the child narrator?

A) Taylor uses a child narrator in *Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry*.
B) The child narrator in the novel is Cassie, who is nine years old.
C) By using a child narrator whose understanding is genuine but incomplete, Taylor builds a sustained dramatic irony that lets the reader feel both Cassie's authentic bewilderment and a fuller recognition of what Cassie is experiencing — turning limitation into the novel's most powerful rhetorical tool.
D) Taylor chose a child narrator because children's stories are more interesting than adult stories.

**Answer: C**

**Q6.** Which sentence contains a correctly formed compound-complex sentence?

A) Even though Papa burned the land. It was a sacrifice not a defeat.
B) Papa burned the cotton to stop the lynching, and the family survived, even though the cost was real.
C) Papa burned the cotton because it was a sacrifice.
D) Papa burned the cotton, and the family survived.

**Answer: B**

**Q7.** A student writes: "Taylor should have given the Logans a bigger victory at the end." What is the best concessive response to this position?

A) The student is completely right — a bigger victory would have made the novel more satisfying.
B) The student is missing the point entirely and hasn't read carefully.
C) Even though a larger victory might have made the ending feel more satisfying, Taylor's refusal to resolve things cleanly is the novel's most honest formal choice — the injustice the Logans face in 1933 did not resolve in a single season, and a clean victory would have made that argument dishonest.
D) Taylor didn't give the Logans a bigger victory because she ran out of pages.

**Answer: C**

**Q8.** Which sentence uses a participial phrase correctly?

A) Burning in the night, Papa set fire to the cotton.
B) Papa set fire to the cotton. Burning in the night.
C) Having carried the weight of the mortgage for years, Papa understood that the land's value was not what it earned but what it proved.
D) Papa having carried the weight, set fire to the cotton burning.

**Answer: C**

**Q9.** What is the difference between a THEME and a MOTIF?

A) A theme is a repeated image; a motif is the novel's central message.
B) A theme and a motif are interchangeable terms for the same concept.
C) A motif is a repeated image, symbol, or idea that accumulates meaning across a text; a theme is the specific argument or claim the text makes about its subject. The land in *Roll of Thunder* is both: it is a motif (it recurs) and it carries the theme (it argues that holding on sometimes means spending what you hold).
D) A motif is found only in poetry; a theme applies to novels and plays.

**Answer: C**

C) "If the land were only a financial asset, why would Papa be willing to burn part of it to save a boy who wasn't even his son?"
D) "How many chapters does the novel have?"
