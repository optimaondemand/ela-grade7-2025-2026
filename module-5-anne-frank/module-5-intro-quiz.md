## Quiz Questions

**Question 1** — Essay (required)
*The Essential Question, first answer*

Before you begin reading, answer this question in 2–3 sentences based on what you already know or believe:

**How do people preserve who they are when everything around them tries to erase them?**

There is no wrong answer here. Write what you actually think.

*(Essay response — ungraded, teacher reads for cohort patterns)*

---

**Question 2** — Short Answer
*What do you already know?*

What do you already know about Anne Frank, the Holocaust, or the Secret Annex? Write one to three sentences. If you've read the diary before, say so — and say what you remember most.

*(Short answer — ungraded, teacher reads to gauge prior knowledge)*

---

**Question 3** — Multiple Choice (Likert)
*Grammar readiness*

Which of the following best describes your comfort with the em dash right now?

A. I know how the em dash works and when to use it.
B. I've seen the em dash but I'm not sure when to use it versus a comma.
C. I'm not sure what an em dash is.
D. I know what it is but I tend to avoid it in my own writing.

*(Multiple choice — ungraded, teacher reads distribution to plan Grammar 1.2)*

---

**Question 4** — Multiple Choice (Likert)
*VR readiness*

How comfortable are you working in VR to build a composition — arranging IFX, writing sticky notes, recording — as part of an academic assignment?

A. Very comfortable — I've done this in previous modules and I know what I'm doing.
B. Somewhat comfortable — I've done it before but I still have questions about how to submit.
C. Not very comfortable — I need a refresher on how the Session and submission work.
D. I have not done a VR assignment before.

*(Multiple choice — ungraded, teacher reads to identify students needing check-in at W1 VR)*

---

**Question 5** — Essay (required)
*Essay focus curiosity*

Look at the four Module 5 essay focus options: (A) Anne's Voice, (B) The Dash, (C) Audience, (D) Identity Claims. Which one interests you most right now, and why — even if you haven't read the diary yet?

*(Essay response — ungraded, teacher reads to track student thinking before module begins)*
