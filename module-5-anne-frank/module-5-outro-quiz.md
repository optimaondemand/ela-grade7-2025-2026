# Module 5 Outro Quiz — The Diary of a Young Girl
# Canvas Quiz — Survey Mode (graded for completion, 5 pts)
# Appears as final Module 5 item, after the Module Project

---

## Quiz Questions

**Question 1** — Essay (required)
*The Essential Question, final answer*

You have spent five weeks reading, annotating, and writing about *The Diary of a Young Girl*. Now answer the essential question in 3–4 sentences — and somewhere in your answer, name one specific moment, entry, or quotation from the diary that changed how you would answer this question.

**How do people preserve who they are when everything around them tries to erase them?**

*(Essay response — teacher reads every response; compare to intro answer for growth)*

---

**Question 2** — Multiple Choice (Likert)
*Confidence check — early warning signal*

How confident are you that you could explain Anne Frank's use of one formal choice (voice, dash, audience, or identity claims) to someone who had never read the diary?

A. Very confident — I could write that explanation right now without looking back at the diary.
B. Mostly confident — I have a clear argument but I would want to check a few quotations.
C. Somewhat confident — I understand the idea but I am not sure I could explain it clearly in writing.
D. Not confident — I am still working out what my argument actually is.

*(Multiple choice — teacher flags C and D for follow-up before essay due date)*

---

**Question 3** — Essay (required)
*What remains unclear*

Name one thing about the diary, about Anne Frank, or about the essay assignment that you still find confusing or unresolved. Be specific — "I don't understand the essay" is too vague; "I'm not sure whether my concession sentence should come at the end of Body 1 or the beginning of Body 2" is specific.

*(Essay response — teacher reads every response; use to address common confusions in a Canvas announcement before essays are due)*

---

**Question 4** — Multiple Choice (checkbox/multiple-select)
*Grammar self-assessment*

Which of the four Module 5 grammar structures do you feel you could use correctly in your essay right now? Select all that apply.

A. The em dash (aside, pivot, emphasis break)
B. Parallel structure (balanced grammatical forms in a list or series)
C. Quotation integration with a signal verb (I-E-E pattern)
D. The periodic sentence (main clause arrives at the end, after building openers)

*(Multiple-select — teacher reads distribution; students who select none or only one need Grammar 5.2 or 5.3 revisited before essay)*

---

**Question 5** — Essay (required)
*Virtue reflection*

The Module 5 essential question is about preserving identity under pressure. Anne Frank's diary argues that this requires courage — not the courage of action, but the courage of continued self-definition when everything around you argues that your self does not matter.

Name one moment in your own life — large or small — where you had to decide whether to stay who you were or let the pressure around you change you. You do not have to have succeeded. What happened, and what does Anne Frank's diary make you think about it?

*(Essay response — ungraded; teacher reads but does not grade on content; this is a formation question)*
