The Diary of a Young Girl — Week 1 — VR 1.4

VR 1.4: Stage the Secret Annex

Eight people, twenty-five months, a space you can stage but not fully imagine

What You’re Doing

You will stage the Secret Annex — not as a floor plan, but as an argument about what it meant to live there. The Annex is not just a hiding place. It is where Anne Frank formed her identity as a writer, where eight people learned what confinement does to human beings, and where the question of what cannot be taken away became urgent. Your composition should argue what the space means, not just what it looks like.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M5-W1 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Domestic Interior or Home environment
  • Reading: Diary entries through the first weeks in hiding (June–July 1942)
What to Do
1

Stage what the space contains (4–6 IFX)

Choose IFX that represent what was present in the Annex — but not just furniture. What does Anne bring with her that she can’t leave behind? What does the space impose that no one chose? Let your composition argue something about the relationship between the space and the people who lived in it. Your IFX choices should answer: what can you bring into a hiding place, and what does confinement take away that you cannot pack?

Together, the two sentences should argue your interpretation of what the Secret Annex means. Use the word VOICE [VOCAB] in one of your sentences.

Facilitator Note

Students who stage the Annex as a living space (beds, books, food) are doing literal representation. The stronger move is to represent the tension between what the space provides (safety, community, time for writing) and what it takes away (freedom, normalcy, childhood). Prompt: "What IFX would you use if you could only show one thing that the space makes possible for Anne? What would you use to show one thing it takes away? Can the same IFX serve both meanings?" The analytical sticky note’s dash structure is the key assessment here — look for whether the dash is adding something that matters, or just adding length.
