VR 2.4: What Confinement Takes and Gives

A two-part composition about what prolonged confinement does to a person and a group

What You’re Doing

In Session ELA7-M5-W2, you will stage what two years of confinement did to the Annex residents — not as a story of suffering alone, but as an argument about what prolonged proximity and isolation do to human identity and relationships. What does the Annex amplify in the people who live there? What does it compress or distort?

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M5-W2 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Small interior space (apartment, study room, or similar)
What to Do
1

Stage what confinement amplifies (3 IFX)

Choose three IFX representing things that the confinement of the Annex makes more intense: not just the hardships, but any human quality that the Annex turns up to an extreme. Anne’s sharpness, the adults’ anxiety, the longing for the outside — what does two years of nowhere to go do to these things? Let your choices argue something, not just list things.

2

Place an analytical sticky note

Write two sentences using parallel structure [PARA]: one sentence listing three things the Annex amplifies in Anne (parallel adjectives or nouns); one sentence listing three things it takes away (same parallel form). Together, the two sentences should argue what confinement does that is not only destructive. Use VOICE [VOCAB] at least once.

Submission

Facilitator Note

The point of the assignment is the "amplifies" frame — not just "confinement is hard" but "confinement intensifies what was already there." Students who only stage suffering miss the complexity; students who notice that the Annex also amplified Anne’s humor, her intellectual rigor, her need for an audience, are reading more carefully. Prompt: "What does Anne do more of in the Annex than she would have done in her normal life? What does that tell you about the Annex as a space?" The parallel structure in the sticky note is the main grammar assessment — check that both sentences use the same form (noun list or adjective list) and that neither list is broken.
