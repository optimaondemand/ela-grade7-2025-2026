7th Grade ELA — Module 5 — VR Assignment
The Diary of a Young Girl — Week 2 — VR 2.5

VR 2.5: Anne and the People Around Her

Three relationships, three sticky notes, one Whiteboard argument

What You’re Doing

Still in Session ELA7-M5-W2, you will stage three of Anne’s most important relationships: with her mother, with her father, and with Peter van Pels. Each relationship represents something different about what Anne needs and what the Annex provides or denies. Your Whiteboard sentence will argue what the three relationships together reveal about the module question.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M5-W2 — Persistent Session (move to a new area)
What to Do
1

Place three stations (2 IFX each)

One station for Anne & her mother, one for Anne & her father, one for Anne & Peter. Each station uses 2 IFX to represent what that relationship gives Anne and what it costs or denies her. A sticky note at each station: the relationship name + one sentence using a DASH [DASH] that states what this relationship is doing for Anne in the Annex context.

2

Place a Whiteboard IFX

Write one sentence on the Whiteboard using parallel structure [PARA] that connects all three relationships and argues what they together show about what Anne is looking for — and what the Annex can or cannot provide her. Label [WB].

3

Make a 60–90 second recording

Walk the three stations and answer: which of these three relationships is most important to Anne’s ability to hold on to who she is? Why? What does she need from it that she cannot get from the other two?

4

Take a snapshot and submit

Name it YourLastName_M5_2.5. Upload snapshot + paste recording link to Canvas.

Submission

Upload snapshot + paste recording link to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The three relationships form a spectrum: the mother relationship (conflict, criticism, longing) / the father relationship (warmth, connection, being seen) / the Peter relationship (emerging intimacy, the first peer relationship Anne has in the Annex). Students who understand Anne most clearly will recognize that the father relationship is what sustains her sense of being valued, while the Peter relationship is what sustains her sense of being a young person with a future. The Whiteboard parallel structure sentence is the synthesis move — watch for sentences that actually connect all three to the module question rather than just summarizing each.
