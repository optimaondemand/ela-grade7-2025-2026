**Q1.** Anne writes critically about her mother throughout the diary. What does this honesty reveal about what the diary is doing for her?

A) It reveals that Anne was an ungrateful child who didn't appreciate her mother.
B) It reveals that Anne and her mother had an unusual relationship for the time period.
C) It reveals that the diary is the one space where Anne does not have to perform — she can be angry, unfair, and complicated without protecting anyone, which is precisely why the diary preserves her full selfhood rather than a curated version of it.
D) It reveals that the confined space of the Annex made everyone irritable and that Anne was simply recording the facts.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q2.** Which of the following sentences uses correct parallel structure?

A) "Anne wanted freedom, to write, and that people would listen to her."
B) "The Annex was small, cold, and people were crowded into it."
C) "Anne wanted freedom, solitude, and an audience."
D) "The Annex was small, the cold was constant, and crowded."

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** What is the analytical purpose of parallel structure in a literary essay thesis?

A) It makes the thesis look more formal and academic.
B) It allows the writer to list more points without worrying about which is most important.
C) It signals that the listed items share a relationship the reader should notice, and it gives the thesis a rhythmic inevitability — the parallel form argues that all three items belong together and carry equal analytical weight.
D) It prevents the thesis from being too short.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** Which sentence uses a dash as a pivot — a turn from one idea to its consequence or complication?

A) "She wrote to Kitty — her imaginary friend — every single day."
B) "She wrote every day — the writing, it turned out, was the only part of herself the Annex could not confiscate."
C) "She wrote to Kitty — and to herself — when no one was looking."
D) "She wrote in the morning and at night — carefully, so the pages wouldn't rustle."

**Answer: B**

---

**Q5.** Anne's relationship with her father is central to the diary. What does it provide for Anne that the other Annex relationships do not?

A) It provides entertainment and distraction from boredom.
B) It provides information about what is happening in the war outside.
C) It provides the experience of being fully seen and valued as a person — Otto Frank is the one Annex resident who consistently treats Anne as intelligent, worthy, and real, which sustains her sense of identity throughout the hiding period.
D) It provides a model of how adults should behave in difficult situations.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** Which of the following is a parallel list that builds — where the final item is the most charged or significant?

A) "She missed her bike, her school, and her cat."
B) "She missed sunlight, laughter, and the particular freedom of being nobody's prisoner."
C) "She was curious, funny, and she wrote a lot."
D) "The Annex had books, food, and people."

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** What does prolonged confinement reveal about human nature, according to Anne's diary?

A) It reveals that people become kinder when they face difficulty together.
B) It reveals that human beings cannot survive without food and warmth.
C) It reveals that proximity and fear amplify rather than level human differences — the qualities that made each person who they were before the Annex become more concentrated and sometimes more difficult in the shared space.
D) It reveals that children adapt to confinement better than adults.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** Fix the broken parallelism: "The diary preserved her humor, her observation of the world around her, and being angry at injustice."

A) "The diary preserved her humor, her observations, and being angry."
B) "The diary preserved her humor, her observations, and her anger."
C) "The diary preserved humor, observations of the world, and anger at injustice."
D) "The diary preserved her humor and observations and also her anger."

**Answer: B**

---

**Q9.** Anne's relationship with Peter van Pels changes over the course of the diary. What does the emerging friendship reveal about what she needs that the diary alone cannot provide?

A) It reveals that Anne is bored and needs entertainment.
B) It reveals that Anne is romantic and wants a boyfriend.
C) It reveals that Anne needs a peer — someone her own age who can encounter her as a person with a future, not just as a dependent child or as a personality to be managed. The diary gives her an interior audience; Peter gives her an exterior one.
D) It reveals that Peter is the only person in the Annex who understands her intellectually.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** Which sentence combines both a dash and parallel structure correctly?

A) "She preserved three things — humor, writing, and to hope — through two years in hiding."
B) "She preserved — humor, curiosity, and anger — the interior life no one could confiscate."
C) "She preserved her humor, her curiosity, and her anger — the interior life the Annex could contain but never extinguish."
D) "She preserved humor and curiosity and anger — through writing in her diary."

**Answer: C**
