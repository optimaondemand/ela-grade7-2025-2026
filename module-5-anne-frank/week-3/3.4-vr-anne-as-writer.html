VR 3.4: Anne Frank, Author

Stage what it means to be a writer when the world has taken everything else

What You’re Doing

In Session ELA7-M5-W3, you will stage what it means to be a writer: not writing as a hobby or a school assignment, but writing as the primary way you hold on to who you are. Anne Frank is not just a diarist. She is a writer who took her work seriously enough to revise it for a future audience. Your composition should argue what writing does for a person who has lost most other ways of being in the world.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M5-W3 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Library or study space
What to Do
1

Stage Anne as a writer (4–6 IFX)

Not Anne as a person in hiding, but Anne as a writer with ambition. What does her writerly identity look like as a spatial composition? What does she carry with her that is specifically about her relationship to language, to audience, to the future? Your IFX choices should argue something about what writing makes possible for her — not just what she uses to write with.

2

Place an analytical sticky note with an I-E-E structure

Write three sentences: (1) introduction sentence using a dash [DASH] about what writing does for Anne; (2) a quotation from the diary integrated using one of the three patterns (label [Q] and [SIGNAL]); (3) explanation sentence using parallel structure [PARA] stating what the quotation reveals. This is your I-E-E sticky note.

Facilitator Note

The key move here is from "Anne writes" to "Anne is a writer" — the distinction between writing as a coping mechanism and writing as an identity claim. Students who see only the coping mechanism miss the way Anne's ambition (revising the diary, writing fiction, choosing a pseudonym) argues for something beyond survival: an insistence on being recognized as a maker of language. The I-E-E sticky note is the main grammar assessment: check that the quotation is actually integrated correctly (not just copied with quotation marks), that the signal verb is interpretive, and that the explanation sentence does more than restate the quotation.
