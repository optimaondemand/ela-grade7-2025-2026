The Diary of a Young Girl — Week 3 — VR 3.5

VR 3.5: Anne’s Identity Claims

What Anne insists is true about herself, and why insisting matters

What You’re Doing

Still in Session ELA7-M5-W3, you will stage two or three specific claims Anne makes about herself in the diary — not descriptions of her situation, but statements where she actively insists on who she is. Anne makes these claims throughout the diary, and they intensify as the hiding period goes on. Your composition should argue that these claims are not just self-description but acts of resistance.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M5-W3 — Persistent Session (move to a new area)
What to Do
1

Choose 2–3 identity claims from the diary

An identity claim is a moment where Anne says (or implies) something like "I am X" or "I know what I want" or "I am not the person you think I am." Find 2–3 of these moments in your reading. Stage each one with 1–2 IFX. Place a sticky note at each with the claim quoted directly (using correct quotation format [Q]) and a signal verb sentence [SIGNAL] naming what the claim is doing.

2

Place a Whiteboard IFX

Write one sentence on the Whiteboard that argues what the identity claims together reveal about the module question. Use AUDIENCE [VOCAB] and parallel structure [PARA]. Label [WB].

3

Make a 60–90 second recording

Walk the compositions and answer: why does Anne make these claims in writing rather than just feeling them privately? What does putting the claim in writing do that thinking it to yourself does not?

4

Take a snapshot and submit

Name it YourLastName_M5_3.5. Upload snapshot + paste recording link to Canvas.

Submission

Upload snapshot + paste recording link to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The recording question — "why does Anne make these claims in writing rather than just feeling them privately?" — is the heart of this assignment. Students who understand this will recognize that writing an identity claim is not just expression; it is a form of witness. The diary is Anne's evidence that she existed as a full person, not just as a victim of circumstance. The Whiteboard sentence should connect all three claims to the module question: preserving who you are when everything tries to erase you sometimes means writing yourself into existence on the page. Watch that the quotation on each sticky note is properly formatted.
