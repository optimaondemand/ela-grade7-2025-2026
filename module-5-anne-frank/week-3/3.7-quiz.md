# Week 3 Quiz — Anne as Writer, Quotation Integration, Audience

# Format: A) / B) / C) / D) with **Answer: X**

**Q1.** In the spring of 1944, Anne heard a radio broadcast and began revising her diary. What is the most accurate analytical explanation for what this decision reveals?

A) It reveals that Anne was unhappy with her original writing and wanted to correct her mistakes.
B) It reveals that Anne had nothing else to do in the Annex and needed a project.
C) It reveals that Anne recognized herself as a writer with a future audience — not just a diarist recording events, but an author shaping a text she wanted to survive and be read. The revision is an act of writerly ambition, not just a personal record.
D) It reveals that Anne was worried the original diary would be found and wanted to make it less personal.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q2.** What is the literary term for the intended or implied reader of a piece of writing — the person or group the writer imagines reading their work?

A) Narrator
B) Reader-response
C) Audience
D) Perspective

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** Which sentence correctly integrates a quotation using a signal verb?

A) "Anne Frank says that, "I want to go on living even after my death.""
B) "Anne Frank insists, "I want to go on living even after my death.""
C) "Anne Frank insists "I want to go on living even after my death.""
D) "Anne Frank: "I want to go on living even after my death.""

**Answer: B**

---

**Q4.** What is wrong with a "dropped quotation" in an essay paragraph?

A) A dropped quotation contains a grammatical error.
B) A dropped quotation uses the wrong punctuation.
C) A dropped quotation has no introduction or explanation — it is placed in the paragraph without context or analysis, so the reader does not know why it is there or what it demonstrates.
D) A dropped quotation is too long.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q5.** Which signal verb best captures what the following quotation is doing: "I know what I want, I have a goal, I have opinions, I have a religion and love. Let me be myself and then I am satisfied."

A) notes
B) wonders
C) insists
D) admits

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** What does the shift from writing to "Kitty" to revising the diary for a future public audience reveal about how Anne's relationship to her own writing changed?

A) It reveals that Anne grew bored of writing to an imaginary friend and needed a real reader.
B) It reveals that the diary changed from a private emotional outlet to a public document — from something Anne used to survive the present to something she shaped to bear witness beyond it.
C) It reveals that Anne stopped thinking of the diary as private and began sharing it with the other Annex residents.
D) It reveals that Anne lost faith in her ability to survive and began thinking about her legacy.

**Answer: B**

---

**Q7.** Which of the following is the best explanation sentence for the quotation "I want to go on living even after my death"?

A) "This shows that Anne didn't want to die."
B) "In other words, Anne is saying she wants to live after she dies by writing things down."
C) "This reveals that Anne understands writing as a form of survival distinct from physical survival — she is not arguing against her own mortality but insisting that the self she has constructed in language can persist beyond whatever happens to her body."
D) "This is interesting because Anne was only fifteen when she wrote this."

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** An identity claim in the diary is a moment when Anne:

A) describes what is happening in the war outside.
B) criticizes the other Annex residents.
C) actively asserts something true about herself — insists on who she is, what she values, or what she will not accept being reduced to.
D) writes about her feelings for Peter van Pels.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q9.** Which sentence integrates a quotation using Pattern 2 (integrated quotation, no comma, no opening capital)?

A) "Anne argues, 'Writing is the only thing that connects me to the world outside.'"
B) "Anne's goal was clear: 'I want to be a journalist or a writer, and later on a famous one.'"
C) "Anne insists that she wants 'to go on living even after my death' — a statement that redefines survival as something a writer achieves through language rather than through luck."
D) "Anne Frank said 'I want to go on living even after my death' in her diary."

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** Why is it analytically significant that Anne wrote identity claims — statements about who she is — in her diary rather than just thinking or feeling them privately?

A) It means other people could read the claims and agree with them.
B) It made the claims more permanent and therefore more comforting.
C) Writing the claims transforms them from private feelings into witnessed declarations — the diary becomes a kind of testimony, a record of Anne's selfhood that exists independently of whether anyone in the Annex acknowledges it. Putting identity in writing is an act of insisting on being known.
D) It shows that Anne was a skilled writer even at a young age.

**Answer: C**
