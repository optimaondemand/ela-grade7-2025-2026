Grammar 4.3: Sentence Architecture Review

All Module 5 structures working together — and how to choose

Module 5 Structures at a Glance
Structure What it does Best for Tag
Em Dash Interrupts, pivots, emphasizes, or adds an aside Voice-forward writing; showing that the added thought matters as much or more than the main clause [DASH]
Parallel Structure Creates rhythm and signals that list items share equal weight or relationship Thesis statements; explanation sentences with multiple effects; building lists that feel complete [PARA]
Quotation Integration (I-E-E) Anchors analysis in the text; demonstrates rather than asserts Any body paragraph that needs textual evidence; anywhere you want the author's own words to do work [Q] [SIGNAL]
Periodic Sentence Delays the main clause to create suspense, weight, and inevitability Thesis statements; conclusion sentences; anywhere the claim should land with maximum force [PERIODIC]
Model Analytical Paragraph: All Four Structures

[PERIODIC] Even after two years of confinement, even after hearing what was happening to Jews across Europe, even after losing almost every form of ordinary freedom, Anne Frank did not stop insisting — in writing, on the page, for a future reader she imagined but could not know — that she was a person with an identity worth preserving.

[Q][SIGNAL] She insists, "I know what I want, I have a goal, I have opinions, I have a religion and love. Let me be myself and then I am satisfied."

[PARA] This declaration reveals that Anne's sense of identity is built not from what she has — freedom, space, safety — but from what she is: her opinions, her beliefs, her ambition, and her refusal to be defined by what has been taken from her.

[DASH] The diary, in this sense, is not a record of erasure — it is evidence that erasure failed.
