# Week 4 Quiz — Final Entries, Periodic Sentences, Hope and Fear

# Format: A) / B) / C) / D) with **Answer: X**

**Q1.** The last diary entry is dated August 1, 1944. Anne does not know it is her last entry. What is the literary term for the effect this creates for a reader who knows what happened three days later?

A) Foreshadowing
B) Dramatic irony
C) Flashback
D) Symbolism

**Answer: B**

---

**Q2.** Anne writes in July 1944: "It's a wonder I haven't abandoned all my ideals, they seem so absurd and impractical. Yet I cling to them because I still believe, in spite of everything, that people are truly good at heart." What does this statement demonstrate about her character?

A) It demonstrates that Anne was naive and didn't understand how dangerous her situation was.
B) It demonstrates that Anne was in denial about the Holocaust.
C) It demonstrates that Anne's hope was not naive but deliberate — she acknowledges that her ideals seem irrational and holds them anyway, which is a form of courage rather than ignorance.
D) It demonstrates that Anne was optimistic by nature and couldn't help feeling hopeful.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q3.** What is a periodic sentence?

A) A sentence that repeats the same word or phrase at the beginning of each clause.
B) A very long sentence with many subordinate clauses.
C) A sentence in which the main clause is delayed to the end, after a series of subordinate elements that build context and anticipation.
D) A sentence used to mark the beginning of a new paragraph or section.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q4.** Which of the following is a periodic sentence?

A) "Anne wrote every day, even when afraid, even when exhausted, even when the news from outside was terrible."
B) "Even when afraid, even when exhausted, even when the news from outside was terrible, Anne wrote every day."
C) "Anne was afraid, exhausted, and surrounded by terrible news — but she wrote every day."
D) "Despite being afraid and exhausted, Anne wrote every day while the news from outside grew worse."

**Answer: B**

---

**Q5.** In a periodic sentence, the opening elements should build. What does "build" mean in this context?

A) Each opening element should be longer than the last.
B) Each opening element should introduce a new topic.
C) Each opening element should be more charged, specific, or difficult to dismiss than the last, so that by the time the main clause arrives, the reader has been fully prepared for it.
D) The opening elements should repeat the same idea in different words.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q6.** The diary's final silence — the absence of any entry after August 1, 1944 — is imposed from outside the text rather than chosen by Anne. What does this tell you about the diary as a literary artifact?

A) It tells you that the diary is incomplete and therefore cannot be analyzed as a finished work.
B) It tells you that Anne planned to stop writing and chose a natural ending point.
C) It tells you that the diary's ending is not an ending Anne made — which means the text itself is still in motion at the moment of interruption, and the silence that follows is part of what the diary, as a historical document, witnesses.
D) It tells you that the diary's final entries are less important than the earlier ones because Anne was running out of things to say.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q7.** Which of the following best describes what the diary argues about the module question — how people preserve who they are when everything tries to erase them?

A) The diary argues that survival requires luck and that nothing the individual does can ultimately protect them.
B) The diary argues that human goodness is inherent and cannot be destroyed by external circumstances.
C) The diary argues that writing — specifically the act of articulating the self on the page for an imagined future reader — is one of the most powerful forms of resistance available to a person whose other freedoms have been taken.
D) The diary argues that family relationships are the most important source of identity during a crisis.

**Answer: C**

---

**Q8.** Convert this cumulative sentence to a periodic sentence: "Anne survived, having written herself into existence, having refused to let confinement define her, having kept her voice intact for two years."

A) "Anne survived, and she wrote herself into existence and refused to let confinement define her and kept her voice intact."
B) "Having written herself into existence, having refused to let confinement define her, having kept her voice intact for two years, Anne survived."
C) "Anne refused to be defined by confinement, wrote herself into existence, and survived."
D) "Anne survived because she wrote, refused to be defined, and kept her voice."

**Answer: B**

---

**Q9.** A student writes: "The ending of the diary is sad because Anne Frank died." Write the best analytical version of this observation as a periodic sentence.

A) "Anne Frank died, which is why the diary's ending is sad."
B) "The diary is sad because Anne Frank was arrested three days after the last entry."
C) "Even knowing that the diary would survive, even knowing that future readers would find it, even knowing that her words would outlast everything that tried to erase her, the abrupt silence after the last entry remains — for every reader who reaches it — the most powerful argument in the text."
D) "After two years of writing, after believing she would survive, after revising the diary for a future reader, Anne Frank was arrested and did not survive the war."

**Answer: C**

---

**Q10.** What is the most accurate statement about the relationship between hope and fear in the diary's final entries?

A) Fear dominates the final entries, and hope is mostly absent.
B) Hope dominates the final entries because Anne refused to dwell on the danger.
C) The final entries are marked by both hope and fear held simultaneously — not resolved, not suppressed, but present together in a way that represents Anne's most mature and honest engagement with her situation.
D) The final entries show that Anne had accepted what was likely to happen and was writing to prepare herself.

**Answer: C**
