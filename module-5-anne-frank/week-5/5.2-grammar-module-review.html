Grammar 5.2: Module 5 Structures Review

All four Module 5 structures in one analytical paragraph

Quick Reference
Structure Tag Signal Best Use
Em Dash [DASH] — (longer than a hyphen) Voice, emphasis, aside, pivot
Parallel Structure [PARA] All items same grammatical form Lists, thesis statements, explanation sentences
Quotation Integration (I-E-E) [Q][SIGNAL] Signal verb + quotation marks + explanation Any time text evidence appears
Periodic Sentence [PERIODIC] Main clause at end, after building openers Thesis, conclusion, any high-stakes claim
Model Paragraph: All Four Labeled

[PERIODIC] Even in a space that denied her sunlight, movement, and the noise of ordinary life, even as she watched her family and community diminish under the weight of fear and proximity, even as the news from outside the Annex grew harder to bear, Anne Frank continued to insist — on the page, in the voice that was entirely her own — that she was a person who could not be fully erased.

[Q][SIGNAL] She declares, "I know what I want, I have a goal, I have opinions, I have a religion and love. Let me be myself and then I am satisfied."

[PARA] This declaration argues that identity is not defined by what one has — space, safety, freedom — but by what one is: the opinions held, the ambitions nurtured, and the refusal to allow external erasure to become internal surrender.

[DASH] The diary, in this reading, is not a record of what was taken — it is evidence that the taking was incomplete.

Exercise 1: Identify All Four

Read the paragraph below. Label each Module 5 structure in brackets: [DASH], [PARA], [Q][SIGNAL], [PERIODIC]. Write one sentence explaining what each structure contributes to the paragraph’s argument.

Although she had no way of knowing her diary would survive, although she could not know whether a future reader would ever find it, although her survival itself was uncertain, Anne wrote with the confidence of someone who believed she was worth reading. She insists, "I want to go on living even after my death." This statement reveals that Anne understood writing as a form of persistence — a way of existing in the world beyond physical presence. The diary preserves her humor, her anger, her ambition, and her unguarded self in a way that no other medium could have managed.

Exercise 2: Write Your Own Review Paragraph

Write a four-sentence analytical paragraph about the diary using all four Module 5 structures, labeled in brackets. The paragraph should be about your essay thesis — use the evidence and claim you have developed. This is a draft, not a finished product. If it doesn’t work perfectly, write a fifth sentence labeled [FIX] explaining what you would change.

The [FIX] sentence is the most useful one. Writers who know what they need to fix are writers who can fix it.
