## Discussion Prompt

**The Essential Question, one last time:**

> How do people preserve who they are when everything around them tries to erase them?

You have spent five weeks reading, annotating, building, and arguing about *The Diary of a Young Girl*. Now answer the essential question directly — not as a summary of the diary and not as a biography of Anne Frank, but as your own argument about what her diary taught you that you didn't fully understand before you read it.

**Your post must do three things:**

1. **State a specific claim** about what the diary argues — not "Anne Frank preserved her identity" but what *preserving identity* means and requires and costs, according to what the diary actually does. One to two sentences.

2. **Name one formal or rhetorical choice** Anne made (voice, use of the dash, audience, identity claims) and explain in one sentence how that choice enforces your claim.

3. **End with a question** for your classmates — a genuine, open question about something in the diary or in the essential question that you still find unresolved or worth arguing about.

**Your reply to one classmate** must: (1) respond directly to their question with your own position and at least one reference to the diary; (2) extend or challenge their original claim — add something they didn't say, or name one place where you see it differently.

---

## Grading Rubric

**15 points — Substantive:** Original post meets all three requirements with specific, analytical language. Reply engages directly with classmate's question and adds genuine analytical substance.

**8 points — Present but thin:** Post is present but doesn't meet one or more requirements, or the reply is general agreement without analytical addition.

**0 points — Missing:** No post, or a post that doesn't address the essential question.
