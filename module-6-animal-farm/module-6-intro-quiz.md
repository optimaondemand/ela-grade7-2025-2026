## Quiz Questions

**Question 1** — Essay (required)
*The Essential Question, first answer*

Before you begin reading, answer in 2–3 sentences based on what you already know or believe:

**How does language shape who holds power, and who loses it?**

*(Essay response — ungraded, teacher reads for cohort patterns)*

---

**Question 2** — Short Answer
*Prior knowledge check*

What do you already know about Animal Farm, George Orwell, the Russian Revolution, or Stalin? Write one to three sentences. If you have read Animal Farm before, say so — and say what you remember most.

*(Short answer — ungraded)*

---

**Question 3** — Multiple Choice (Likert)
*Grammar readiness — passive voice*

Which best describes your understanding of active vs. passive voice?

A. I know the difference and can identify and write both correctly.
B. I have heard of active and passive voice but I am not always sure which is which.
C. I am not sure what passive voice is.
D. I know the terms but I am not sure why the difference matters for writing.

*(Multiple choice — ungraded, teacher reads distribution to plan Grammar 2.2)*

---

**Question 4** — Multiple Choice (Likert)
*VR readiness*

How comfortable are you entering VR and completing an academic composition — placing IFX, writing sticky notes, recording — without step-by-step help?

A. Very comfortable — I've done this across multiple modules.
B. Mostly comfortable — I can do it but I sometimes have questions about submission.
C. Somewhat comfortable — I need a brief refresher on how things work.
D. I have not done VR composition work before.

*(Multiple choice — ungraded)*

---

**Question 5** — Essay (required)
*Essay focus curiosity*

Look at the four Module 6 essay focus options: (A) Passive Voice, (B) Changing Commandments, (C) Anaphora, (D) Final Commandment as Irony. Which one interests you most right now, and why — even before you have started reading?

*(Essay response — ungraded, teacher reads to track student thinking before module begins)*
