Animal Farm — Week 1 — VR 1.4

VR 1.4: Stage the Rebellion

What does a revolution look like before it is betrayed?

What You’re Doing

Represent the state of Animal Farm at the end of Chapter 3 — after the rebellion, when the animals have driven Jones out and the Seven Commandments are posted on the barn wall. You are staging the moment of maximum idealism, before the corruption has visibly begun. Your composition should argue something about what this moment already contains that makes what comes next possible.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M6-W1 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Outdoor Farm or Rural Landscape
  • Bring: Your notes on Chapters 1–3, especially Old Major's speech and the Seven Commandments
What to Do
1

Stage the moment of maximum idealism (4–6 IFX)

Choose IFX that represent what Animal Farm looks like in Chapter 3: the animals are free, the commandments are posted, and the work is being done collectively. Do not represent Jones or the humans — represent the farm without them. Ask yourself: what IFX captures the idea of a dream that is not yet broken?

2

Place one sticky note identifying what is already wrong

Your sticky note must name one thing in Chapters 1–3 that already contains the seeds of what goes wrong later — before the reader can obviously see it. Use the label [APP] to name a character or element in appositive form. Example format: "[Character], [appositive identifying their role in the allegory], already [specific action or behavior] in Chapter [X]."

3

Take a snapshot and submit

Name it YourLastName_M6_1.4. In the Canvas text box: one sentence describing what your composition argues, using an appositive labeled [APP] to identify the key element.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M6_1.4 + one-sentence Canvas text description. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The key question for this session is whether students can identify structural problems in the rebellion before they become obvious. A strong composition will stage the farm looking hopeful while the sticky note identifies something the reader might have missed (the pigs taking the milk, the fact that only pigs can read well, Snowball's and Napoleon's competition already visible in Chapter 3). A developing composition will stage the post-rebellion farm without making any analytical claim about what is already wrong.
