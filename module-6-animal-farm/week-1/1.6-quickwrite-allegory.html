7th Grade ELA — Module 6 — Week 1
Animal Farm — Quickwrite 1.6

Quickwrite 1.6: What the Allegory Argues

5 points — completion

The Prompt

Write 5–7 sentences arguing what the allegory in Chapters 1–3 says about power — not what the story says, but what the choice to tell this story as an allegory allows Orwell to say that he couldn't say in a straightforward political essay.

Think about it this way: why does Orwell write about pigs and horses instead of naming Stalin and Soviet workers directly? What does the animal frame let him do?

Required Tags

Label each required element in brackets within your writing:

[ALLEGORY] — one sentence using the word "allegory" to make a claim (not just define the word)
[APP] — one sentence using an appositive phrase to identify a character in the allegory
[EQ] — one sentence connecting your argument to the essential question: How does language shape who holds power, and who loses it?

Type your response directly into the Canvas text box. This is a first-draft response — write quickly and argue directly. You do not need to write a formal paragraph; you need to make a claim and support it with one specific reference to the text.
