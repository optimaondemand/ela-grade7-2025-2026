**Question 1** (5 pts)
Squealer is Napoleon's chief propagandist. Which of the following best describes his primary technique in Chapters 4–6?

A. He uses physical intimidation to prevent the animals from questioning Napoleon's decisions.
B. He uses statistics, rewritten history, and the threat of Jones's return to justify any decision Napoleon makes.
C. He bribes the animals with extra food rations in exchange for their loyalty.
D. He challenges Snowball's ideas publicly and defeats him in open debate.

**Correct: B**

**Question 2** (5 pts)
What is the passive voice?

A. A sentence in which the subject performs an action directly and energetically.
B. A sentence construction in which the subject receives the action and the agent (who did it) is omitted or moved to a secondary position.
C. A type of sentence used only in academic writing to sound more formal and objective.
D. A sentence in which the main verb is in the present tense, making the action feel immediate.

**Correct: B**

**Question 9** (5 pts)

How could these two sentences be most effectively revised using the structures from Weeks 1 and 2?

A. Combine them with a semicolon: "The pigs were taking advantage; the animals did not notice."
B. Reverse the order and add "however": "The other animals did not notice what was happening; however, the pigs were taking advantage."
