VR 3.4: The Changing Commandments

Stage the transformation of Animal Farm’s founding law — before and after

What You’re Doing

Stage the Seven Commandments in their original form and in their revised form as two separate spatial compositions. The contrast between the two is the argument. Use Whiteboard IFX for the text of the commandments, and use surrounding IFX to represent what each version represents about the farm's values.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M6-W3 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Library or Archive Location
  • Bring: The original Seven Commandments and at least two of their revised versions from Chapters 6–8
What to Do
1

Place two Whiteboard IFX: before and after

On the first Whiteboard: write at least two commandments in their original form, labeled [ORIGINAL]. On the second Whiteboard: write the same commandments in their revised form, labeled [REVISED]. Place the Whiteboards in visible opposition to each other — the spatial distance or arrangement should suggest something about what was lost.

2

Place 2–3 IFX near each Whiteboard

Near the [ORIGINAL] Whiteboard: IFX representing what the commandments were meant to protect (equality, collective ownership, freedom from human exploitation). Near the [REVISED] Whiteboard: IFX representing what the revisions serve (pigs' comfort, Napoleon's authority, Squealer's justifications).

3

Place a sticky note and submit

Sticky note: one sentence labeled [WHEREAS] naming what the original commandment meant and what the revision does. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M6_3.4 + one-sentence Canvas text using a contrast clause.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M6_3.4 + one-sentence Canvas text. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The strongest compositions will put the before and after Whiteboards in spatial tension — not side by side but in opposition (one elevated, one diminished; one large, one small; one surrounded by hopeful IFX, one surrounded by authority IFX). The [WHEREAS] sticky note is the key analytical artifact: check that it names what the revision does rather than just repeating what the two versions say.
