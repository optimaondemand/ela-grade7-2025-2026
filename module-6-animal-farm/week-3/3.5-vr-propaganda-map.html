VR 3.5: Map the Propaganda System

How does Squealer’s system work? Stage its mechanisms in space.

What You’re Doing

Remain in the same Session from VR 3.4, in a new area. Squealer uses several distinct techniques to control what the animals believe. Stage those techniques as a spatial map — each technique occupying its own area, with IFX and a labeled sticky note. The map should argue which technique is most essential to Napoleon's power and why.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M6-W3 — same Persistent Session as VR 3.4
  • Bring: Your notes on Squealer's techniques from Chapters 4–8
What to Do
1

Stage three propaganda techniques (2 IFX each)

Choose three of Squealer's techniques: (1) passive voice to hide agency, (2) statistics to distort reality, (3) the threat of Jones's return as a trump card, (4) rewritten history to erase the past, (5) public confessions to implicate victims in their own punishment. Each technique gets its own 2-IFX area in the Location. The IFX should represent the technique, not just the scene.

2

Place a sticky note at each technique area

Each sticky note: name the technique + one sentence labeled [WHILE] or [WHEREAS] that names the gap between what Squealer claims and what is actually happening + one passive-voice quotation from the text labeled [PASSIVE].

3

Record a 60–90 second argument and submit

Walk through the map. Argue which technique is most essential to Napoleon's power and why. Your recording should use at least one contrast clause. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M6_3.5 + recording link.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M6_3.5 + paste recording link to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The recording is the primary assessment artifact here. Listen for whether the student argues which technique is most essential or simply describes all three equally. The analytical move is the argument for priority — a student who can say "the rewriting of history is more powerful than the statistics because it eliminates the very language animals would need to challenge the statistics" is doing genuine literary argument. Prompt students who describe without prioritizing: "If Napoleon could only use one of Squealer's techniques, which should he keep and why?"
