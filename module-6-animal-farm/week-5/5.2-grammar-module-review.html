Grammar 5.2: Module 6 Structures Review

All four Module 6 structures in one analytical paragraph

Quick Reference
Structure Tag Signal Best Use
Appositive Phrase [APP] Noun phrase beside another noun, set off by commas Identifying allegorical roles; embedding analytical claims
Active / Passive Voice [ACTIVE] / [PASSIVE] To be + past participle (passive); subject performs action (active) Analyzing propaganda; naming agency that was hidden
Contrast Clause [WHEREAS] / [WHILE] / [ALTHOUGH] Contrast conjunction at start of clause Naming the gap between appearance and reality; building theses
Anaphora [ANAPHORA] Same word/phrase repeated at beginning of successive clauses Accumulative analytical force; analyzing propaganda's rhetoric
Model Paragraph: All Four Labeled

[APP] Squealer, Napoleon's propagandist and the novel's embodiment of state media, turns the passive voice into a governing technology — [PASSIVE] "It was announced," "it has been decided," "certain readjustments were found necessary" — each construction removes Napoleon's name from the sentence that describes Napoleon's decision.

[WHEREAS] Whereas the original commandments named what the animals had a right to expect, the revised versions insert qualifiers that technically satisfy the law while substantively violating everything the law was meant to protect.

[ANAPHORA] The language changed. The history changed. The law changed. And because the animals had no language of their own — no words not yet owned by those who had rewritten them — they could not name what had been taken.

Exercise 1: Identify All Four

Read the paragraph below. Label each Module 6 structure in brackets: [APP], [PASSIVE], [WHEREAS] or [ALTHOUGH], [ANAPHORA]. Write one sentence explaining what each contributes to the paragraph's argument.

Although Old Major's speech described the animals' oppression with genuine accuracy, the language of the rebellion was captured — long before Jones was driven out — by the pigs who could read well enough to revise it. Boxer, the farm's most loyal and least questioning worker, was sent to his death in a van whose side bore the words "Horse Slaughterer and Glue Boiler." The knacker had been announced as a veterinary service. The history was rewritten. The murderers were renamed. And Boxer, who had worked harder than anyone and questioned nothing, became the proof of exactly what the rebellion promised it would never do to its own.

Exercise 2: Write Your Review Paragraph

Write a four-sentence analytical paragraph about Animal Farm using all four Module 6 structures, labeled in brackets. The paragraph should be about your essay thesis — use the evidence and claim you are developing. Add a fifth sentence labeled [FIX] identifying what you would change.

The [FIX] sentence is the most useful sentence you will write this week. A writer who can name what needs to change is a writer who can change it.
