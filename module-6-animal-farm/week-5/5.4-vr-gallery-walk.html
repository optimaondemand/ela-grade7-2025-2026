7th Grade ELA — Module 6 — VR Assignment
Animal Farm — Week 5 — VR 5.4

VR 5.4: Gallery Walk — Classmates’ Compositions

Visit two classmates’ Module 6 compositions and respond analytically

What You’re Doing

You have spent four weeks building VR compositions about Animal Farm. In this session you will visit two classmates' sessions and respond to one composition each. Different students have read the same novella and arrived at different arguments about how it works. Encounter one of those arguments.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session type: Persistent — join classmates' sessions by Session ID
  • Session IDs: Posted by your teacher in Canvas before this session. If working asynchronously, request two IDs via Canvas message.
What to Do
1

Visit and study one composition at each classmate’s session

Find a composition from any week (1.4, 1.5, 2.4, 2.5, 3.4, 3.5, 4.4, or 4.5). Spend 3–4 minutes with it. What is it arguing? What does it do that you didn't do in your own compositions?

2

Leave a response sticky note at each composition

Your name + two sentences: [OBS] one analytical observation about what the composition argues; [Q] one genuine question it raises for your own essay argument. Use a contrast clause ([WHEREAS] or [ALTHOUGH]) in at least one sentence.

3

Screenshot and written reflection

Take one screenshot in each session showing your sticky note in context. In the Canvas text box, write 3–4 sentences: Did either composition change how you're thinking about your essay? Name the classmate and the composition.

Submission

Upload screenshots YourLastName_M6_5.4a and YourLastName_M6_5.4b + written reflection to Canvas. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Post sessions from students whose compositions represent meaningfully different interpretations — one student who focused on propaganda mechanisms (passive voice, commandment revision), one who focused on the allegory map or the final image. The [Q] sticky note is the key artifact: a student who can name what another composition raises for their own argument is synthesizing across perspectives. Check that the contrast clause is used structurally, not decoratively.
