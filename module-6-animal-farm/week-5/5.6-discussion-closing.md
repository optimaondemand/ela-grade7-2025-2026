## Discussion Prompt

**The Essential Question, one last time:**

> How does language shape who holds power, and who loses it?

You have spent five weeks reading, annotating, staging, and arguing about *Animal Farm*. Now answer the essential question directly — not as a plot summary and not as a biography of Orwell, but as your own argument about what the novella taught you about language and power that you didn't fully understand before you read it.

**Your post must do three things:**

1. **State a specific claim** about what *Animal Farm* argues — not "language is used as a tool of power" but what specifically language does and what specifically it takes from those who lose it. One to two sentences.

2. **Name one formal or rhetorical choice** Orwell made (passive voice, allegory, the changing commandments, anaphora, irony) and explain in one sentence how that choice performs the claim rather than just illustrating it.

3. **End with a question** for your classmates — a genuine, open question about *Animal Farm* or the essential question that you still find unresolved or worth arguing about.

**Your reply to one classmate** must: (1) respond directly to their question with your own position and at least one reference to the text; (2) extend or challenge their original claim — add something they didn't say, or name one place where you see the novella differently.

---

## Grading Rubric

**15 points — Substantive:** Original post meets all three requirements with specific, analytical language. Reply engages directly with classmate's question and adds genuine analytical substance.

**8 points — Present but thin:** Post is present but doesn't meet one or more requirements, or the reply is general agreement without analytical addition.

**0 points — Missing:** No post, or a post that doesn't address the essential question.
