**Question 1** (5 pts)
Which of the following best describes the allegorical function of Animal Farm as a whole?

A. A children's story about farm animals that accidentally became a political allegory after publication.
B. A narrative whose literal level (animals on a farm) and symbolic level (Soviet history and political corruption) operate simultaneously, so that the formal choices of the story — passive voice, the changing commandments, anaphora — perform the political argument rather than just illustrating it.
C. A straightforward account of the Russian Revolution, translated into animal characters to make it easier for young readers to understand.
D. A pessimistic argument that revolution is always doomed to fail, regardless of the intentions of those who begin it.

**Correct: B**
