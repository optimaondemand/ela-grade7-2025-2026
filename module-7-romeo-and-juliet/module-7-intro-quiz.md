<h2 style="color:#1B2A4A;">Module 7: Romeo and Juliet</h2>
<p><strong>Essential Question:</strong> <em>How does Shakespeare use the structure of language — meter, metaphor, and dramatic irony — to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?</em></p>
<p>In Module 7 you will read Shakespeare's <em>Romeo and Juliet</em> in full, studying the formal choices — iambic pentameter, the light/dark extended metaphor, dramatic irony, and the foil structure — that make the play an argument rather than simply a story. Over five weeks you will practice writing with these techniques, build spatial arguments about them in VR, and write a four-paragraph analytical essay arguing what one formal choice does that the plot alone cannot do. The module project offers four essay focus options; you will select and develop one across the five weeks.</p>
