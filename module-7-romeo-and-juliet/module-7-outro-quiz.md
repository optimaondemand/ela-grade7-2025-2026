# Module 7 Outro Quiz — Romeo and Juliet
# Canvas Survey (graded_survey) — 5 questions — completion credit
# Teacher: review confidence ratings and "what remains unclear" responses to identify students needing pre-essay support

---

**Question 1** (essay)
Return to your answer from the Module 7 intro survey: *How does Shakespeare use the structure of language — meter, metaphor, and dramatic irony — to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?*

Write three to four sentences with your revised answer. Name at least one specific formal choice from the play and one moment in the text where it performs the argument you are describing. Your answer does not need to be perfect — it should be more specific than your first attempt.

---

**Question 2** (Likert — 1 to 5)
How confident are you in your Module 7 essay argument right now?

1 — I do not have a thesis yet and I am not sure where to start
2 — I have a thesis but I am not sure my evidence supports it
3 — I have a thesis and evidence but I am not confident the argument holds together
4 — I feel good about my argument; I just need to write it clearly
5 — My argument is strong and I am confident in the essay

*(Teacher: flag students at 1 or 2 for individual check-in before essay due date)*

---

**Question 3** (short answer)
What is one specific thing about *Romeo and Juliet* or about the formal choices we studied in Module 7 that you still find confusing or unresolved? Be specific — name the scene, the technique, or the question, not just "I don't understand the metaphor."

*(Teacher: triage these responses for patterns — common confusions become the focus of any pre-essay live session or announcement)*

---

**Question 4** (multiple select — check all that apply)
Which of the following Module 7 grammar structures do you feel you can use correctly in your essay? Check all that apply.

- [METER] and [METER-SHARED] — iambic pentameter and shared lines
- [METAPHOR] — extended metaphor analysis
- [IRONY-D], [IRONY-V], [IRONY-S] — dramatic, verbal, and situational irony
- [FOIL] — foil structure and contrast
- [SOLO] — soliloquy analysis
- [APP] — appositive phrase
- [WHEREAS] / [WHILE] / [ALTHOUGH] — contrast clauses

*(Teacher: students who check none or only one are at high risk of missing required tags on the essay)*

**Question 5** (essay)
The Optima Portrait of a Graduate describes a student who is fueled by wonder and leaves school equipped for meaningful work. Think about the virtue this module asked most of you — whether that is patience, courage to sit with complexity, honesty about what you don't understand, or something else. In two or three sentences, name the virtue and describe one moment in this module — a reading, a VR session, a writing exercise — when you had to practice it.
