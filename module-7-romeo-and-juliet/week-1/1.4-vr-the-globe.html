VR 1.4: Stage the Prologue

Before the play begins, the Chorus tells you everything — stage what that means

What You’re Doing

The prologue tells us two things will collide: the feud and the love. Before you read the full play, stage this collision spatially. What does it look like for these two forces to occupy the same world? Your composition should argue something about how Shakespeare sets up the play’s central tension before a single scene has played out.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M7-W1 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Elizabethan Theatre / Globe Theatre reconstruction
  • Bring: Your copy of the prologue and Act 1; your copia sentence from Grammar 1.2
What to Do
1

Divide the stage space: feud vs. love (4–6 IFX)

Place IFX that represent the two forces the prologue introduces: the ancient grudge and the new love. They should not look the same. Choose IFX that show the contrast — what is old, inherited, and violent vs. what is young and new. Use the stage space itself: where you place things relative to each other is part of the argument.

2

Place a Whiteboard IFX with the prologue’s argument

On the Whiteboard IFX, write: [METER] — one sentence about what iambic pentameter in the prologue does (not just what it is); and [WHEREAS] — a contrast clause naming what the feud makes impossible vs. what love creates anyway.

3

Submit: snapshot + Canvas text

Take a snapshot named YourLastName_M7_1.4. In the Canvas text box, type your two Whiteboard IFX sentences and add one more sentence: what does your spatial arrangement argue that the Whiteboard IFX sentences don’t?

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M7_1.4 + Canvas text (three sentences). Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students may be tempted to recreate a scene rather than stage an argument. The key question to ask: “What does your arrangement argue about how these two forces relate?” If they can’t answer that, they need to rethink the layout. Strong compositions will show asymmetry — the feud is heavier, older, larger; love is new and small but placed at the center. The Whiteboard IFX sentences are the primary assessment item for this activity.
