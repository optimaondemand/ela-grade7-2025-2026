VR 1.5: Map the Shared Sonnet

Romeo and Juliet’s first conversation is a complete sonnet — stage what that means

What You’re Doing

In Act 1 Scene 5, Romeo and Juliet’s first exchange of dialogue is a complete Shakespearean sonnet — fourteen lines, spoken in alternating turns, ending with a shared rhyming couplet. This is one of the most sophisticated formal choices in the play. You will map the structure spatially and record a short argument about what the shared form argues about the nature of their connection.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M7-W1 — Persistent Session (same as VR 1.4)
  • Location: Elizabethan Theatre / Globe Theatre reconstruction
  • Bring: The text of Act 1 Scene 5, Romeo and Juliet’s first exchange (lines 93–106)
What to Do
1

Map the sonnet structure (3–4 IFX)

Place IFX representing Romeo’s three quatrains, then Juliet’s, then the shared couplet. The couplet IFX should be placed so it is clearly between the Romeo IFX and the Juliet IFX — belonging to neither alone. Label each with a sticky note naming whose lines it represents and one word describing the emotional register of those lines (Romeo’s first quatrain: reverent; Juliet’s response: playful; etc.).

2

Whiteboard IFX: thesis + appositive

On the Whiteboard IFX, write: [APP] — an appositive phrase identifying what the shared sonnet is, analytically (not “a poem” but what it does or represents in the play’s argument); and [METER-SHARED] — one sentence arguing what the shared verse does that two separate sonnets could not.

3

Record your argument (60–90 seconds) and submit

Walk through your composition and explain: what does the spatial arrangement of the sonnet’s three sections argue about how Romeo and Juliet relate to each other? Start with your [APP] sentence, walk through the quatrains, end at the shared couplet. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M7_1.5 + recording link.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M7_1.5 + paste recording link to Canvas. Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students need to know the specific lines before entering VR. The shared sonnet is Act 1 Scene 5, lines 93–106 in most editions. Romeo speaks lines 93–96 (first quatrain), Juliet responds 97–100, Romeo 101–104, Juliet 105–106, with a second exchange forming the couplet. Students who can’t identify the sonnet structure in the text will struggle with the composition — check comprehension before they enter the headset. Strong [METER-SHARED] sentence: “By having Romeo and Juliet complete each other’s lines of iambic pentameter, Shakespeare argues that their love is not a choice but a structural fact — they fit together before they know each other’s names.”
