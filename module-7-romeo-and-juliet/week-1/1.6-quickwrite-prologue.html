Romeo and Juliet — Quickwrite 1.6

Quickwrite 1.6: What the Prologue Argues

5 points — Canvas text box submission

Write 5–7 sentences responding to this prompt. This is not a formal essay — it should sound like clear, intelligent thinking on the page, not a rough draft and not a polished essay. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

Shakespeare’s prologue tells the audience that the lovers will die before the play begins. In 5–7 sentences, explain: what does this formal choice — announcing the ending before the story starts — argue about the relationship between language and fate? Is the prologue a warning? A sentence? A lament? What does it do to an audience that knows the ending from the first fourteen lines?

Required Tags

[METER] — use when analyzing iambic pentameter in the prologue
[APP] — use an appositive to identify what the prologue is or does
[EQ] — end your quickwrite by connecting to the essential question: How does Shakespeare use the structure of language to argue that some experiences exceed what ordinary words can carry?

Grading

5 points for a complete response that uses all three tags and answers the prompt with specific reference to the text. 0 points for a missing or off-topic response. This is graded for completion and genuine engagement, not for polish.
