Romeo and Juliet — Quickwrite 2.6

Quickwrite 2.6: What the Light Metaphor Argues

5 points — Canvas text box submission

Write 5–7 sentences responding to this prompt. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

In 5–7 sentences, explain: what does the light/dark extended metaphor in Act 2 argue about the nature of Romeo and Juliet’s love that the story itself — the events, the plot — cannot argue alone? Your answer should include at least one specific quotation from Act 2 and at least one statement about what the vehicle (light, sun, lightning) adds to the tenor (love) that changes how we understand it.

Required Tags

[METAPHOR] — before your analysis of the extended metaphor
[APP] — use an appositive to identify Romeo’s or Juliet’s dramatic function
[EQ] — connect your answer to the essential question: How does Shakespeare use the structure of language to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?

Grading

5 points for a complete response that uses all three tags and includes at least one specific quotation from Act 2. 0 points for a missing or off-topic response.
