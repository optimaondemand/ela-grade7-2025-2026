Romeo and Juliet — Week 3 — VR 3.4

VR 3.4: Stage Mercutio’s Death

Three characters, three different understandings of the same street — stage what each one knows going in

What You’re Doing

The fight in Act 3 Scene 1 happens because three people walk into the same space carrying completely different knowledge. Romeo knows he is Tybalt’s kinsman by marriage. Mercutio knows nothing of this. Tybalt knows only the logic of honor and the name Montague. Stage this gap. Your composition should make visible what each character carries into the scene — not what they do, but what they know, and what they cannot know — so that the fight reads not as accident but as tragedy engineered by irony.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M7-W3 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Marketplace / Public Square setting
  • Bring: Your [IRONY] sentences from Grammar 3.2 Exercise 2 and your Act 3 text
What to Do
1

Two-zone composition: knowledge vs. knowledge (4–6 IFX)

Divide the Public Square Location into two zones. In one zone, place IFX representing what Romeo knows going into the scene: the marriage, the kinship, his reasons for wanting peace. In the other zone, place IFX representing what Tybalt and Mercutio know: honor, the feud, the logic of the Capulet name. For each IFX, attach a sticky note with a phrase that names the knowledge or the blindness it represents. Where do the two zones overlap? That space of overlap is where Mercutio dies.

2

Whiteboard IFX: [IRONY] + [WHEREAS] sentence

On the Whiteboard IFX, write one sentence using both tags: [IRONY] to name the gap between what Romeo knows and what Tybalt and Mercutio know; [WHEREAS] to name the contrast in one clause. For example: “[IRONY][WHEREAS] Whereas Tybalt and Mercutio enter Act 3 Scene 1 armed only with the logic of the feud, Romeo enters carrying the secret of the marriage, and Shakespeare uses this gap to argue that the tragedy is not caused by hatred alone but by the impossibility of communicating what Romeo knows to the people who most need to hear it.” Write your own — do not copy this model.

3

Submit: snapshot + Canvas text

Take a snapshot named YourLastName_M7_3.4. In the Canvas text box, type your Whiteboard IFX sentence and add one sentence explaining what your spatial arrangement argues that the Whiteboard sentence doesn’t capture: what does the geography of the two zones say about the gap in knowledge?

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M7_3.4 + Canvas text (two sentences: Whiteboard IFX sentence + spatial argument sentence). Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The key question for this composition is whether students are staging knowledge structures rather than a fight scene. If a student is placing IFX for “Mercutio’s sword” or “Romeo standing between them,” redirect: What does each person believe is true when they walk in? The spatial overlap between the two knowledge zones is the conceptual achievement of the assignment. The Whiteboard [IRONY] + [WHEREAS] sentence is the primary assessment item.
