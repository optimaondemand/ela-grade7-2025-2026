Romeo and Juliet -- Quickwrite 3.6

Quickwrite 3.6: What Dramatic Irony Does

5 points -- Canvas text box submission

Write 5-7 sentences responding to this prompt. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

Shakespeare tells us in the prologue that the lovers will die. Then he spends three acts making us hope they won't. In 5-7 sentences, explain: what does the dramatic irony of Act 3 -- the gap between what the audience knows and what the characters know -- do to the experience of watching Mercutio die and Romeo kill Tybalt? Your answer should explain what the dramatic irony adds to the scene that simply watching the events unfold could not.

Required Tags

[IRONY-D] -- before your analysis of dramatic irony in Act 3
[FOIL] -- name how Mercutio functions as Romeo's foil and what his death clarifies
[EQ] -- connect to the essential question: How does Shakespeare use the structure of language to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?

Grading

5 points for a complete response using all three tags with specific reference to Act 3 events. 0 points for missing or off-topic responses.
