VR 4.4: Stage the Tomb Scene

What each character finds vs. what is actually true — dramatic irony at its maximum

What You’re Doing

The tomb scene is the play’s dramatic irony at its absolute maximum: Paris comes to mourn someone he believes simply dead; Romeo comes to die beside someone he believes dead; Juliet wakes to find everyone she loves dead. Every character in the scene acts on wrong or incomplete information, and the audience watches all of it knowing more than any of them. Stage this scene not as an action sequence but as a map of misunderstanding: what each character believes they find, and what is actually true.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M7-W4 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Tomb / Crypt or Dark Interior setting
  • Bring: Your Act 5 text; your [IRONY] + [WHEREAS] sentences from Grammar 4.3 Exercise 1
What to Do
1

Stage the space of misunderstanding (6–8 IFX)

Place IFX for the four presences in the tomb scene: Romeo, Juliet, Paris, and what you might call the “space of misunderstanding” itself — the gap between what any character believes and what is actually true. For each character IFX, attach two sticky notes: one naming what the character believes when they enter the tomb, and one naming what is actually true. For the “space of misunderstanding” IFX, write one phrase that names what the audience knows that no character fully knows. Consider: how does the Tomb / Crypt location’s darkness and enclosure reinforce the argument you are staging?

2

Sticky notes: [IRONY] + [WHEREAS] on each major IFX

For each of the three character IFX (Romeo, Juliet, Paris), place an additional sticky note with a phrase using [IRONY] and [WHEREAS]: “[IRONY][WHEREAS] Whereas Romeo believes Juliet is dead, the audience knows she sleeps” is a starting template. Write your own version for each character, making the contrast specific to what that character believes vs. what is true. Push beyond the template to say what the gap argues — not just what the character doesn’t know, but what the misunderstanding reveals about fate, love, or language.

3

Submit: snapshot + Canvas text

Take a snapshot named YourLastName_M7_4.4. In the Canvas text box, type your three [IRONY] + [WHEREAS] sticky note sentences (one per character) and add one more sentence: what does your spatial arrangement of the tomb argue that the sticky note sentences don’t capture?

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M7_4.4 + Canvas text (three [IRONY][WHEREAS] sentences + one spatial argument sentence). Estimated time: 20–25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The key analytical move is the “space of misunderstanding” IFX — the fourth presence in the scene. Students who only stage three character IFX are staging a tableau; students who stage the gap itself are staging an argument. Push students to articulate what that fourth IFX represents: not a character but a condition of knowledge that the play has constructed. The three sticky note sentences are the primary assessment items; they should make claims, not just note differences.
