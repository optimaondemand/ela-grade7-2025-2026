VR 4.4: Stage the Tomb Scene

Act 5 Scene 3 -- the ending as argument

What You're Doing

Stage the tomb scene as the convergence of the play's three major formal choices: the light/dark extended metaphor, dramatic irony, and the iambic pentameter that carries both. Your composition argues that the ending is not just sad but formally complete -- the metaphors, irony, and meter all arrive at the same conclusion together. This is not staging a scene; it is staging an argument about form.

What You'll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M7-W4 -- Persistent Session
  • Location: Crypt/Tomb interior or Stone Cathedral setting
  • Bring: Act 5 text; your revised [THESIS] from Grammar 4.3
What to Do
1

Three-zone composition: metaphor, irony, meter (6-8 IFX)

Place IFX in three zones: one zone for the light/dark metaphor as it appears in the tomb (Romeo's final speech to Juliet's "crimson lips"); one zone for the dramatic irony (what the audience knows vs. what Romeo knows as he drinks the poison); one zone for the formal verse structure (how the meter carries the final speeches). Label each zone with a sticky note naming what formal choice it represents and one phrase of your analytical claim about it.

2

Whiteboard IFX: [THESIS] + your essay focus

Write your revised [THESIS] from Grammar 4.3. Then write which essay focus option (A, B, C, or D) you are writing and one sentence explaining why the tomb scene is crucial evidence for your argument.

3

Submit: snapshot + Canvas text

Snapshot YourLastName_M7_4.4. In the Canvas text box, type your [THESIS] and add one sentence: what does your three-zone spatial composition argue about how the play's formal choices converge in the tomb?

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M7_4.4 + Canvas text (thesis + one sentence). Estimated time: 20-25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The three-zone composition requires students to have read Act 5 carefully. Check that students can identify: (1) the specific light/color imagery in Romeo's final speech; (2) the specific knowledge gap that produces dramatic irony in the poison scene; (3) at least one moment where the iambic pentameter is doing something particular. Students who can't answer these before entering VR need to re-read before composing. The thesis on the Whiteboard IFX is the primary assessment item.
