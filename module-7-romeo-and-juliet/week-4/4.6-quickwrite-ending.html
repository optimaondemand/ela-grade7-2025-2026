Romeo and Juliet -- Quickwrite 4.6

Quickwrite 4.6: What the Ending Argues

5 points -- Canvas text box submission

Write 5-7 sentences responding to this prompt. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

At the end of Romeo and Juliet, the Montague and Capulet families reconcile and agree to build golden statues of the dead lovers. The Prince says: "For never was a story of more woe / Than this of Juliet and her Romeo." In 5-7 sentences, explain: what does the ending argue about the relationship between language, love, and the social world? Is the ending a tragedy because the lovers died -- or because the reconciliation came too late and cost too much? What has Shakespeare argued, by the time the final couplet closes, about what language can and cannot do?

Required Tags

[IRONY-S] -- use when naming the situational irony of the reconciliation
[METER] -- say something about what the Prince's rhymed couplet does formally
[EQ] -- connect to the essential question: How does Shakespeare use the structure of language to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?

Grading

5 points for a complete response using all three tags with specific reference to the ending. 0 points for missing or off-topic responses.
