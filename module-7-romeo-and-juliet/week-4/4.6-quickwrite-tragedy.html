7th Grade ELA — Module 7 — Week 4
Romeo and Juliet — Quickwrite 4.6

Quickwrite 4.6: What the Ending Argues

5 points — Canvas text box submission

Write 5–7 sentences responding to this prompt. This is not a formal essay — it should sound like clear, intelligent thinking on the page, not a rough draft and not a polished essay. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

The Prince ends Romeo and Juliet with a rhyming couplet: “For never was a story of more woe / Than this of Juliet and her Romeo.” He does not give a speech about justice or the feud or what ought to be done. He names it as a story. This is a meta-theatrical move: the play, in its final moment, calls itself a story — aware that it is being told, aware that it will be retold. In 5–7 sentences, explain: what does this move argue about language, memory, and what tragedy does for the living? When we tell the story of suffering, what do we do to it — and for whom? Is the Prince’s final couplet elegiac (honoring the dead through language), ironic (insufficient to contain the grief), or something else? What does Shakespeare argue, in the play’s final two lines, about why we tell stories about loss?

Required Tags

[IRONY or METAPHOR] — use one of these to analyze the formal choice in the Prince’s couplet or the play’s conclusion: is the rhyming form ironic? Does the play’s light/dark metaphor reach its endpoint here?
[FOIL] — use the foil structure to illuminate one final contrast: what does the Prince’s act of naming (turning event into story) reveal by contrast with the silence of the characters who have died?
[EQ] — end your quickwrite by connecting to the essential question: how does the Prince’s final couplet — and the act of telling this tragedy at all — argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words, but can be transformed by form (by tragedy, by verse, by story) into something the living can hold?

Grading

5 points for a complete response that uses all three tags and engages seriously with the question of what it means that the play ends by naming itself as a story. 0 points for a missing or off-topic response. Graded for genuine engagement and use of required structures, not for polish.
