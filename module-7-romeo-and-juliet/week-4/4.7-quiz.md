**Question 10** (5 pts)
The essential question asks how Shakespeare uses the structure of language to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words. How does the tomb scene answer this question?

A. The tomb scene shows that Shakespeare preferred poetic language because it was more beautiful than prose.
B. The tomb scene is primarily about plot rather than language, so it does not directly address the essential question.
C. By bringing the light metaphor, the dramatic irony, and the iambic pentameter all to the same chamber at the same moment, Shakespeare argues that the experience of tragedy -- watching something irreversible happen in the formal language of verse -- is something that a summary, a newspaper account, or a plain narrative could not produce.
