Grammar: Module 7 Quick Reference and Essay Tags

All four Module 7 structures -- what they are, how to tag them, when to use them

All Four M7 Structures at a Glance

Structure Tag(s) What it does in an essay Best for
Iambic pentameter / shared line / sonnet [METER] / [METER-SHARED] Argues that a specific rhythmic or formal pattern performs something the content alone cannot Options A, B (tomb scene)
Extended metaphor [METAPHOR] Tracks a figurative pattern across multiple passages and argues what the vehicle adds to the tenor Option B; useful in all options
Dramatic / verbal / situational irony [IRONY-D] / [IRONY-V] / [IRONY-S] Names the gap between what is known and what is said/expected, and argues what that gap does to the audience Option C; useful in all options
Foil / soliloquy [FOIL] / [SOLO] Names a character contrast or private speech and argues what it reveals about the play's central conflict Option D; useful in all options

Model Paragraph with All Four M7 Structures Labeled

"[IRONY-D] When Romeo enters the Capulet tomb believing Juliet dead, [APP] the last moment in the play before the tragedy becomes irreversible, the audience carries a knowledge that makes every line Romeo speaks cost twice what it appears to cost: they know Juliet is alive; they know the letter failed; they know. [METAPHOR] When Romeo declares that 'Beauty's ensign yet / Is crimson in thy lips and in thy cheeks,' the light/color metaphor returns one final time -- not to praise, but to make death visible in the vocabulary of love. [METER] The iambic pentameter that has carried the play's most luminous declarations now carries the poison Romeo drinks, and the form does not change -- which is Shakespeare's argument: the love and the death are the same shape."

[IRONY-D] + [APP] -- dramatic irony with appositive
[METAPHOR] -- extended metaphor return in tomb
[METER] -- iambic pentameter carries the death
[APP] -- appositive from Module 6, still active

Exercise 1 -- Identify All Four in a Paragraph

Read this paragraph about Mercutio's death and identify which four M7 structures appear. Label each one with its tag in brackets and write one sentence for each explaining what it adds analytically.

"Act 3 Scene 1 is the turning point of the play -- the moment the comic movement ends and the tragic movement begins. Romeo, the Montague son who married Juliet that morning, steps between Mercutio and Tybalt in a gesture of peace that becomes the mechanism of Mercutio's death. The audience, armed with the prologue's knowledge, watches this not as a surprise but as a recognition: the feud has more force than the marriage, and the iambic pentameter that carries Mercutio's dying curse -- 'A plague on both your houses!' -- does not break; it holds, even as the social order shatters. Whereas Mercutio is loyal to the world as it is -- to honor, to friendship, to the obligations of his class -- Romeo has abandoned all of this for a love he cannot name in public, and Mercutio pays the price of a choice he was not consulted about."
