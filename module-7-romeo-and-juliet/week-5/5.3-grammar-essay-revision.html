Romeo and Juliet -- Grammar 5.3

Grammar: Essay Self-Revision Checklist

Four questions -- read before you submit

The Four Revision Questions

[R1] Does my thesis name a specific formal choice AND argue what it does?

Check: Can you name the specific formal choice (iambic pentameter, extended metaphor, dramatic irony, foil)? Does your thesis argue what that choice does -- not just that it exists? "Shakespeare uses dramatic irony" fails. "By sustaining dramatic irony from the prologue through Act 5, Shakespeare argues that tragedy is recognition, not surprise" passes.

[R2] Does my evidence actually demonstrate my thesis, or does it just relate to the topic?

Check: Does each quotation show the formal choice operating -- not just a scene that is relevant? A quotation about love is not evidence for a thesis about dramatic irony unless you show how the dramatic irony operates in that specific quotation.

[R3] Does my explanation sentence name something the quotation does not say?

Check: Read your explanation sentence. Could it have been written without the quotation? If yes, it is restating rather than interpreting. The explanation must ADD something -- a claim about what the formal choice DOES that is not visible in the quotation alone.

[R4] Does my conclusion extend the thesis rather than restate it?

Check: Does your conclusion say something you couldn't have said in the introduction? Does it connect to the essential question with knowledge the essay has built? "In conclusion, Shakespeare uses dramatic irony to make the audience sad" restates. "Reading Romeo and Juliet teaches you that tragedy does not happen to you -- you participate in it; the dramatic irony makes the audience's knowledge into a form of complicity" extends.

Self-Revision Exercise

Apply all four revision questions to your current draft. For each question, write: (a) your label [R1]/[R2]/[R3]/[R4]; (b) the specific sentence from your essay you are evaluating; (c) a one-sentence revision or confirmation.

Format:

[R1] My thesis: "..." Revision: "..." OR Confirmation: "This passes R1 because..."

Required Essay Tags Checklist

[THESIS] -- one-sentence thesis in introduction
[METER], [METAPHOR], [IRONY-D/V/S], or [FOIL/SOLO] -- at least two M7 structures in body
[Q][SIGNAL] -- before each quotation
[APP] -- at least one appositive phrase (from M6)
[WHEREAS] or [ALTHOUGH] -- contrast clause in Body 2 (from M6)
[CON] -- concession and counter in Body 2
