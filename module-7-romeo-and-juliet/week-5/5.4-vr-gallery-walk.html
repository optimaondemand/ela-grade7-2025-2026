VR 5.4: Gallery Walk

Study two classmates' essay compositions and respond analytically

What You're Doing

Visit two classmates' VR sessions from Week 4 (their essay staging compositions) and respond analytically to each. You are looking for: what argument does their composition make? What does their spatial arrangement argue about the formal choices they are analyzing? Where do you see their argument most and least clearly?

What You'll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session IDs: Two classmates' ELA7-M7-W4 Persistent Sessions
  • Bring: Your own essay focus option and thesis, so you can compare arguments
What to Do
1

Visit and study (two sessions)

For each classmate's composition: study the IFX arrangement and Whiteboard IFX. What essay focus option are they working with? What is their thesis? What does their composition argue spatially?

2

Leave a sticky note in each session

For each session, place a sticky note with your name and: [OBS] -- one specific observation about what their composition argues; [Q] -- one genuine question about their thesis or evidence; [WHEREAS] or [ALTHOUGH] -- one contrast clause noting how their approach differs from yours.

3

Submit: screenshots + Canvas text

Take a screenshot in each session showing the composition and your sticky note. Submit as YourLastName_M7_5.4a and YourLastName_M7_5.4b. In the Canvas text box, type your [OBS][Q][WHEREAS/ALTHOUGH] for each classmate -- two sets total.

Submission

Upload screenshots YourLastName_M7_5.4a and YourLastName_M7_5.4b + Canvas text (two sets of three sentences). Estimated time: 20-25 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

Students need access to two classmates' W4 session IDs before entering VR. Coordinate sharing of session IDs either through Canvas or in class. The [Q] is the most important piece: it should be a genuine analytical question about the classmate's argument, not a compliment or a vague prompt. "Why did you choose that IFX to represent dramatic irony?" is a good question. "Nice composition!" is not. The Canvas text box submission is the primary assessment item.
