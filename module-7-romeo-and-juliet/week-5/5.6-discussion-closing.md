## Discussion Prompt

We began Module 7 with a question: *How does Shakespeare use the structure of language — meter, iambic pentameter, metaphor, and dramatic irony — to argue that some experiences cannot be expressed in ordinary words?*

You have now read the full play and worked with all four of its formal choices. This is your chance to answer that question in your own words, with the specific evidence you have gathered.

**Your initial post must do four things:**

1. **State a specific claim** about what *Romeo and Juliet* argues through its formal choices — not what the play is about, but what the play's language *does* that the plot cannot do alone. Your claim should be precise enough that a careful reader could disagree with it.

2. **Name one formal choice** — shared meter [METER-SHARED], the light/dark extended metaphor [METAPHOR], dramatic irony [IRONY-D], or the foil structure [FOIL] — and explain in two or three sentences how that choice performs your claim. Cite at least one specific line or moment from the play.

3. **Use at least one required M7 tag** from the list: [METAPHOR], [IRONY-D], [FOIL], [METER-SHARED], [SOLO], [APP], [WHEREAS].

4. **End with a genuine open question** for your classmates — something you are still uncertain about, something you want to think further, or something the play left unresolved for you. This question should come from your reading, not from the prompt.

**Then reply to one classmate's post.** Your reply must do two things: (1) engage directly with their claim — either extend their argument with new evidence, or name one place where you read the same moment differently; (2) address their open question, even if your answer is "I don't know, and here is why I think that is the right response."
