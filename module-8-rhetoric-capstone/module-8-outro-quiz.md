## Survey Questions

**Q1 — Essential Question (revised answer)**
Type: essay_question | Points: 0

At the start of this module you answered: *What makes language powerful enough to move people to action?*

Now that you have analyzed Patrick Henry's speech and your own chosen text, write your revised answer. You do not have to change what you said — but your answer should be more specific and grounded in textual evidence than your first response.

Write 4–6 sentences. Use at least one M8 tag.

**Q2 — Essay Confidence**
Type: multiple_choice_question | Points: 0 (Likert)

How confident do you feel that your Module 8 essay accurately identifies and explains the rhetorical choices in your chosen text?

- 1 — Not confident — I am not sure my analysis is correct
- 2 — Slightly confident — I think some parts are right but others feel uncertain
- 3 — Somewhat confident — I can defend most of my claims
- 4 — Confident — I believe my thesis and analysis are well-supported
- 5 — Very confident — I would stand behind every claim in my essay

*(Teachers: sort by this column in SpeedGrader to identify students who may need a follow-up conversation before the grade is finalized.)*

**Q3 — What Remains Unclear**
Type: essay_question | Points: 0

What is one thing about rhetorical analysis — any concept, any technique, any aspect of the essay — that still feels uncertain or confusing to you after this module? Be specific: name the concept, not just "the essay" or "the analysis."

**Q4 — Grammar Self-Assessment**
Type: essay_question | Points: 0

List all six M8 rhetorical structures you studied this module — by tag name and a brief definition in your own words. Then name which one you feel most confident analyzing in writing and which one you found most difficult.

*(Example format: [ANAPHORA] — repeating a word or phrase at the start of successive clauses; this one I find easy to spot but hard to explain in terms of what it does to the audience.)*

**Q5 — Virtue Reflection**
Type: essay_question | Points: 5 (completion)

The Optima virtues include **perseverance**, **honesty**, and **self-government**. Which of these did Module 8 require of you most — and where specifically did you feel it? Name a moment in the module (a lesson, a VR session, a draft revision, a quiz) where you had to practice that virtue, and describe what practicing it looked like for you.
