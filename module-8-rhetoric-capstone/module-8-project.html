Module 8 Project: Rhetorical Analysis Essay
100 points · Written Essay + VR Snapshot + Spatial Recording
What This Project Is

Over five weeks you have built the tools of rhetorical analysis: the three appeals, sentence architecture, figures of repetition, and figures of address. You have applied each one to Patrick Henry's speech and to a text of your own choosing. This project asks you to bring everything together in a five-paragraph rhetorical analysis essay arguing how your chosen text uses language to move its audience toward action or belief.

Your project has three components: (1) the written essay, (2) a snapshot of your VR capstone composition from 5.5, and (3) the spatial recording from 5.5 in which you present your argument aloud. All three are submitted together.

Your Chosen Text

You selected your text in Week 1 with teacher approval. Your text must be one of the following types:

  • A speech (historical or contemporary, at least 300 words)
  • An editorial or opinion essay (published, at least 400 words)
  • A poem that makes an argument (at least 14 lines)
  • Song lyrics that make an argument (at least 20 lines)

If you have not yet confirmed your text with your teacher, do so before beginning your final draft.

Essay Structure — Five Paragraphs
Paragraph 1 — Introduction

Introduce your text, its speaker or author, and the occasion for which it was written. Name the audience the text was addressing and what it was trying to persuade them to do or believe. End with your [THESIS]: an arguable claim that names the text, the specific formal choices you will analyze, and their effect on the audience.

Required tags: [THESIS]

Paragraph 2 — First Body Argument

Analyze the first formal choice you identified in your thesis. Introduce your evidence with a [SIGNAL] phrase. Quote exactly. Label the M8 structure with its tag. Write an [APP] sentence explaining what that formal choice does to the audience — not just what it is, but how it works on the listener or reader.

Required tags: [EV1] [SIGNAL] + at least one of [ETHOS/PATHOS/LOGOS] + at least one of [PERIODIC/CUMUL/ANAPHORA/PARALLEL/RQ/APOS] [APP]

Paragraph 3 — Second Body Argument

Analyze a second formal choice — different from the first. Follow the same structure: introduce with a [SIGNAL] phrase, quote exactly, label the structure, write an [APP] sentence. If possible, show how this second choice works together with the first, rather than simply repeating the same kind of analysis in a new paragraph.

Required tags: [EV2] [SIGNAL] + at least one additional M8 structure tag [APP]

Paragraph 4 — Concession and Response

Acknowledge the strongest version of a reading that differs from yours. Use a [WHEREAS] clause to introduce the concession. Then explain why your reading is still stronger, or what the concession reveals that actually supports your thesis rather than undermining it.

Required tags: [WHEREAS]

Paragraph 5 — Conclusion

Do not simply restate your thesis. Your [CON] paragraph should extend the argument — explain what your analysis reveals about how rhetoric works on audiences in general, or why this text's methods matter beyond this single occasion. What does a reader who understands this text understand about language itself?

Required tags: [CON]

Required Tags — Complete List

Your essay must contain all of the following tags, correctly used. Missing tags will reduce your score on the Structure criterion.

[THESIS] [ETHOS] or [PATHOS] or [LOGOS] [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL] [ANAPHORA] or [PARALLEL] [RQ] or [APOS] [EV1] [EV2] [SIGNAL] [APP] [WHEREAS] [CON]
