Grammar 2.2: [PERIODIC] and [CUMUL] as Analytical Tags

Identifying sentence architecture and arguing what it does

The Tags

Tag Structure Effect Weak analytical use Strong analytical use
[PERIODIC] Main clause withheld to the end; conditions and qualifications precede it Suspense; inevitability; premises accepted before claim arrives “[PERIODIC] This is a periodic sentence. It delays the main point.” “[PERIODIC] By stacking three ‘if we wish’ clauses before ‘we must fight,’ Henry forces the audience to accept the premises before they can reject the conclusion — the periodic structure makes the claim feel necessary rather than arguable.”
[CUMUL] Main clause stated first; extensions and qualifications added after Momentum; accumulation; claim made larger and heavier by what follows “[CUMUL] Henry uses a cumulative sentence here. It lists many examples.” “[CUMUL] Henry’s enumeration of failed petitions — ‘we have petitioned, remonstrated, supplicated’ — builds the logos appeal through accumulation: each addition extends the proof, so that by the end the audience has absorbed not one piece of evidence but a sequence that argues by sheer weight.”

Exercise 1: Identify and Argue the Sentence Type

For each passage from “Give Me Liberty or Give Me Death,” (1) identify whether it is [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL], and (2) write one sentence arguing what the sentence structure accomplishes for Henry’s argument.

Passage A

“If we wish to be free — if we mean to preserve inviolate those inestimable privileges for which we have been so long contending — if we mean not basely to abandon the noble struggle in which we have been so long engaged and which we have pledged ourselves never to abandon until the glorious object of our contest shall be obtained — we must fight!”

Is this [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL]? Write one sentence arguing what the structure accomplishes.

Passage B

“They tell us, sir, that we are weak — unable to cope with so formidable an adversary. But when shall we be stronger? Will it be the next week, or the next year? Will it be when we are totally disarmed, and when a British guard shall be stationed in every house?”

Is this [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL]? Write one sentence arguing what the structure accomplishes.

Passage C (from your own chosen text)

Find one sentence in your chosen text that is [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL]. Copy it here. Tag it and write one sentence arguing what the sentence structure does for the text’s argument.

Exercise 2: Write with Deliberate Sentence Architecture

Using the same position from Grammar 1.2 Exercise 2 (the argument you actually believe), write two sentences: one [PERIODIC] and one [CUMUL]. Both should make your argument. Label each. Then write one sentence explaining which sentence better serves your argument and why.

Model:

[PERIODIC] If we accept that students learn better when they have some say in their schedule, if we accept that research consistently shows sleep deprivation impairs memory formation, if we accept that the purpose of school is learning rather than compliance, then starting classes before 8 a.m. is not a scheduling preference but an educational harm. [CUMUL] Schools that start before 8 a.m. harm student learning, creating sleep deficits that impair memory consolidation, reducing the cognitive capacity students bring to every class, and producing chronic stress responses that make the very work of learning more difficult.

Write your two sentences, each labeled. Then your one sentence of explanation.
