Quickwrite 2.6: What Sentence Architecture Argues

5 points — Canvas text box submission

Write 5–7 sentences responding to this prompt. Use the required tags below.

Prompt

In 5–7 sentences, explain: choose one sentence from your chosen text that uses a [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL] structure. Quote it, name the structure, and argue what that sentence architecture does for the dominant appeal ([ETHOS], [PATHOS], or [LOGOS]). Then connect your answer to the essential question: How does a writer use the structure of language to make an argument?

Required Tags

[PERIODIC] or [CUMUL] — before your analysis of the sentence structure
[ETHOS], [PATHOS], or [LOGOS] — naming the appeal the structure serves
[APP] — use an appositive to identify the author’s or text’s rhetorical function
[EQ] — connect your answer to the essential question

Grading

5 points for a complete response that quotes a sentence from your chosen text, correctly identifies its structure with the right tag, argues what the structure does for the dominant appeal, and connects to the essential question. 0 points for a missing or off-topic response.
