VR 3.5: Your Text — Figures in the Argument

Stage the figures of repetition in your chosen text; refine your thesis and body paragraph

What You’re Doing

Stage the anaphora and parallelism of your chosen text spatially, then use what you see to refine your body paragraph and thesis. By the end of this session, you should have a body paragraph that uses all four M8 analytical structures and a thesis that is ready for the capstone essay.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M8-W3 — Persistent Session (same as VR 3.4)
  • Location: Colonial Assembly Hall or Council Chamber setting
  • Bring: Your chosen text with [ANAPHORA] and [PARALLEL] marked; your revised [THESIS] and body paragraph from Grammar 3.3
What to Do
1

Figures composition for your text (4–6 IFX)

In a new area of the Location, build a two-zone composition for the figures in your text: anaphora zone and parallel zone. Label each with a sticky note quoting the passage and naming what the figure does for the dominant appeal.

2

Whiteboard IFX: [THESIS] + complete body paragraph

Write your revised [THESIS] at the top. Then write the complete body paragraph from Grammar 3.3 Exercise 1, with all tags visible: [ETHOS/PATHOS/LOGOS], [ANAPHORA] or [PARALLEL], [PERIODIC] or [CUMUL], [APP], [WHEREAS]. This is your essay in miniature.

3

Record your argument (60–90 seconds) and submit

Name your text. State your thesis. Explain how the figures you staged serve the dominant appeal and connect to your thesis. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M8_3.5 + recording link + thesis sentence + [FIX] sentence.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M8_3.5 + recording link + thesis sentence + [FIX] sentence to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The Whiteboard IFX body paragraph is the primary assessment. It should show all four M8 structural tags working together in sequence. A paragraph that has all the tags but in no logical order needs the question: “Which structure does the first work of the argument? Which one closes it?”
