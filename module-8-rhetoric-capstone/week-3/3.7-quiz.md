**Question 1** (5 pts)
Anaphora is defined as:

A. The repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses — the mirror image of anaphora, which appears at the beginning.
B. The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences, creating an accumulative or insistent effect.
C. The use of identical grammatical structures across related elements to argue their equivalence.
D. A rhetorical question that does not expect a direct answer from the audience.

**Correct: B**

**Question 2** (5 pts)

B. It argues that only one person — Henry — has been responsible for the colonial petitions, which is why he has the authority to call for war.

**Question 3** (5 pts)
Parallelism is best defined as:

A. The repetition of a phrase at the start of successive clauses, building an accumulative argument.
B. The use of the same grammatical structure across related elements, arguing that those elements are equivalent in kind, weight, or importance.
C. A sentence structure that delays the main clause to the end, creating suspense.
D. The use of contrasting ideas in the same sentence, such as "give me liberty, or give me death."
