Rhetoric Capstone — Week 4 — VR 4.4

VR 4.4: Your Text — Full Rhetorical Map

Build the complete spatial argument for your chosen text across all six M8 structures

What You’re Doing

Build the complete rhetorical map of your chosen text as a spatial composition. All six M8 analytical structures should be represented in the spatial arrangement: the three appeals, the two sentence architecture types, and the four figures. The composition is the visual argument of your essay; the Whiteboard IFX is the written argument. Together they should tell the same story.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M8-W4 — Persistent Session
  • Location: Colonial Assembly Hall or student choice (teacher-approved)
  • Bring: Your fully annotated text; your revised [THESIS] and introduction from Grammar 4.3; your body paragraph from Grammar 4.2 Exercise 2
What to Do
1

Full rhetorical map composition (8–12 IFX)

Create a spatial map of your text’s full rhetorical argument. Organize IFX into clusters representing each structure you are analyzing. Each cluster should have a sticky note with: the tag name, the quotation it represents, and one word describing what it does (e.g., “builds logos,” “closes exit,” “gives presence,” “makes audience supply conclusion”). Arrange the clusters so they tell a story — the composition should argue which structures do the foundational work and which ones complete it.

2

Whiteboard IFX: [THESIS] + introduction + body paragraph

Write your [THESIS] at the top. Below it, write your essay introduction (from Grammar 4.3) and your complete body paragraph (from Grammar 4.2 Exercise 2) with all tags visible. This Whiteboard IFX is your essay draft.

3

Record your argument (60–90 seconds) and submit

Name your text. State your thesis. Walk the rhetorical map and explain what the composition argues about how the structures work together. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M8_4.4 + recording link + thesis sentence.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M8_4.4 + recording link + thesis sentence to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–30 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

The Whiteboard IFX draft is the primary assessment. Students whose body paragraphs have all the tags but no logical sequence need the question: “If you were going to read this paragraph aloud, which sentence comes first? Why?” The Whiteboard at this stage is essentially a first essay draft — students should leave this session with something they can revise into their final submission.
