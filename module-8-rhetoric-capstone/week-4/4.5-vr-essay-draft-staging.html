VR 4.5: Essay Draft Staging

Stage the full essay argument spatially; record your presentation draft

What You’re Doing

Using your VR 4.4 composition as the foundation, build the spatial staging for your complete capstone essay argument — introduction, two body paragraphs (evidence 1 and evidence 2), and conclusion. Then record a 2–3 minute presentation of your full argument. This recording is your presentation draft and will become the foundation for the Week 5 capstone presentation.

What You’ll Need
  • Device: VR headset
  • Session ID: ELA7-M8-W4 — Persistent Session (same as VR 4.4)
  • Location: Same Location as VR 4.4
  • Bring: Your complete essay draft (introduction + both body paragraphs + conclusion outline); your VR 4.4 composition
What to Do
1

Extend the VR 4.4 composition: add evidence zones for both body paragraphs

Add two new IFX clusters to your existing composition: [EV1] for body paragraph 1 and [EV2] for body paragraph 2. Each cluster should have a sticky note with the quotation, the tags that apply, and one sentence of analytical explanation. Add a [CON] zone representing your conclusion’s argument.

2

Update the Whiteboard IFX: [THESIS] + [EV1] + [EV2] + [CON]

Update your Whiteboard IFX from VR 4.4 with the second body paragraph and a conclusion sentence. The Whiteboard should now contain the four structural components of your full essay in miniature.

3

Record your presentation draft (2–3 minutes) and submit

Record a 2–3 minute spatial recording of your full argument: state your thesis, present both pieces of evidence with their analytical tags, explain the connection between them, and deliver your conclusion. This is your first full run of the capstone presentation. Submit snapshot YourLastName_M8_4.5 + recording link + [FIX] sentence identifying the weakest moment in your argument.

Submission

Upload snapshot YourLastName_M8_4.5 + recording link + [FIX] sentence to Canvas. Estimated time: 25–35 minutes in headset.

Facilitator Note

This session produces the recording that will become the Week 5 capstone presentation. The [FIX] sentence is the most valuable assessment item here — a student who can accurately name the weakest moment in their argument is ready to revise. A student who says “everything is fine” needs a more specific question: “Which of your two body paragraphs makes the stronger argument? Why is the other one weaker?”
