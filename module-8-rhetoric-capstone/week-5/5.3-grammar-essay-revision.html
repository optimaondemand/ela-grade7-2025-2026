7th Grade ELA · Module 8 · Week 5
5.3 — Grammar: Final Essay Revision
Grammar & Writing · ~30 minutes

This is your final revision session before submission. Work through the four questions and the tag checklist in order. Make every fix in your actual draft as you go — this is not a planning exercise.

The Four Revision Questions
[R1] — Thesis

Read your thesis aloud. Is it arguable — could a reasonable person disagree? Does it name the text, the speaker's technique, and the effect on the audience in one sentence?

Fix: If your thesis only describes what the speaker does without arguing why it works or what it achieves, add the "and this achieves _________ because _________" clause.

[R2] — Evidence

Every piece of evidence must be quoted exactly and cited. Is your evidence introduced with a [SIGNAL] phrase? Is each quotation followed by analysis — not just restatement?

Fix: Find the sentence after your quote. If it begins "This shows that..." rewrite it to name what the formal choice is doing, not what the content says.

[R3] — Concession and Complexity

Does your essay acknowledge what the other side gets right? Your [WHEREAS] clause should name a genuine complication — not a strawman — before you resolve it.

Fix: If your concession sentence begins "Some may say..." followed by a weak position you immediately dismiss, replace it with the strongest version of the opposing reading.

[R4] — Conclusion

Your [CON] paragraph must not simply restate the thesis in different words. It should zoom out: why does your argument matter beyond this one text or this one speech? What does it reveal about how rhetoric works on audiences in general?

Fix: Add one sentence that begins "The larger implication is..." or "What this reveals about rhetoric is..." — then make sure that sentence earns its claim.

Required Tag Checklist

Go through your draft and confirm that each tag below appears at least once, correctly placed, with a sentence that actually demonstrates what the tag names. Check each one off as you verify it.

Tag What it must do in your essay
[THESIS]Arguable claim naming text, technique, and audience effect
[ETHOS/PATHOS/LOGOS]Identify which appeal and explain its function on the audience
[PERIODIC] or [CUMUL]Name the sentence architecture and explain what the structure achieves
[ANAPHORA] or [PARALLEL]Identify the figure of repetition with exact quotation; explain its effect
[RQ] or [APOS]Identify the figure of address; explain why this form is stronger than a direct statement
[EV1] [EV2]Two quoted pieces of evidence, each followed by analysis
[SIGNAL]At least one evidence introduction phrase ("Henry argues that...", "As the speaker insists...")
[APP]Explanation of what the formal choice does to the audience, not just what it is
[WHEREAS]Genuine concession to the strongest opposing reading
[CON]Conclusion that extends beyond thesis restatement — broader implication
[FIX] — Final Polish

After completing the checklist, read your entire essay aloud from beginning to end. Mark any sentence where you stumble or slow down — those sentences need to be revised. Reading aloud catches problems that silent reading hides.
