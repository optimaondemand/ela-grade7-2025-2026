7th Grade ELA · Module 8 · Week 5
5.4 — VR Gallery Walk: Classmates' Rhetorical Maps
Solo VR Assignment · ~25 minutes · Snapshots YourLastName_M8_5.4a and 5.4b
What You're Doing

In Weeks 3 and 4 your classmates built spatial compositions of their chosen texts inside the Colonial Assembly Hall Location. This week you will visit two of those compositions, read the IFX arrangements and Whiteboard annotations left behind, and leave a sticky note observation on each one. You are not grading your classmates — you are reading their work as a critic reads an essay, noticing what the spatial argument says and asking one genuine question it raises for you.

What You'll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required — sticky notes and spatial navigation are VR-only)
  • Session type: Persistent
  • Session ID: ELA7-M8-W4 (your classmates' compositions are in this Session)
  • Location: Colonial Assembly Hall
  • Spawn point: Main entrance — your classmates' work is arranged around the hall
How to Join
  1. Open ENGAGE on your headset
  2. Select Join Session and enter Session ID ELA7-M8-W4
  3. Choose Colonial Assembly Hall as the Location
  4. Spawn at the main entrance — you should see IFX compositions already placed around the hall
What to Do — In Order
  1. Walk the hall and find two compositions that are not yours. Look for compositions from different students — marked by their signed sticky notes.
  2. At the first composition: Spend at least 4–5 minutes reading the IFX arrangement and Whiteboard annotations. Ask yourself: What is this student arguing about their text? Which rhetorical structure does the spatial layout make most visible? What does the composition make you notice that you might not have found in words alone?
  3. Place a Sticky Note IFX next to the first composition. Your sticky note must include: (a) one specific observation — name what you see the composition doing, using at least one M8 tag; (b) one genuine question the composition raises for you. Sign your sticky note with your last name. Example: The three-zone layout makes the [ANAPHORA] cluster feel heavier than the [LOGOS] zone — is that because the physical space gives repetition more room, or because the speaker's text actually weights it that way? — Smith
  4. Take a snapshot of the first composition with your sticky note visible. Name it YourLastName_M8_5.4a.
  5. Move to the second composition and repeat: read, observe, place a sticky note (one specific observation + one genuine question + your name), take a snapshot named YourLastName_M8_5.4b.
  6. Log out of the Session.
What to Submit
  • YourLastName_M8_5.4a — snapshot of the first composition with your sticky note visible
  • YourLastName_M8_5.4b — snapshot of the second composition with your sticky note visible

Upload both snapshots to this Canvas assignment. No written reflection needed — your sticky notes are the record of your thinking.

Estimated Time

25 minutes total — 10–12 minutes per composition, including reading, sticky note writing, and snapshot.

Facilitator Note

Students are in the persistent W4 Session, reading IFX work left by classmates. The most common sticking point is writing sticky notes that only say "good job" — encourage students to name a specific structure and ask a real question. A student who asks a genuine question has done the thinking.
