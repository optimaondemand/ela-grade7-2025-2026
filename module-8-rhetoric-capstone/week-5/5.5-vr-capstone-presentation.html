7th Grade ELA · Module 8 · Week 5
5.5 — VR Capstone: Final Essay Staging and Presentation
Solo VR Assignment · ~35 minutes · Snapshot YourLastName_M8_5.5 + 3–5 min recording
What You're Doing

This is the final VR session of Module 8. You will stage your revised essay argument spatially — placing IFX and Whiteboard annotations that represent your thesis, your two body arguments, and your conclusion — and then record a 3–5 minute spatial recording in which you present your argument as if your classmates are in the room with you. The recording and the snapshot are submitted alongside your written essay as the VR component of the Module 8 Project.

What You'll Need
  • Device: VR headset (required)
  • Session type: Persistent
  • Session ID: ELA7-M8-W5
  • Location: Colonial Assembly Hall
  • Before you enter: Your revised essay draft (5.3 complete); a clear sense of your two main arguments and your [THESIS]
How to Join
  1. Open ENGAGE on your headset
  2. Select Join Session and enter Session ID ELA7-M8-W5
  3. Choose Colonial Assembly Hall as the Location
  4. Spawn at the main entrance — you will build your capstone composition in the central area of the hall
What to Do — In Order
  1. Build your capstone composition (10–15 minutes). Place IFX from the ENGAGE library to represent the overall argument of your chosen text — this should feel like a refined version of your W4 work, organized around your thesis rather than just the text's content. You need at least 6 IFX placed deliberately, representing the argument's arc.
  2. Place a Whiteboard IFX in the center of your composition. Write your [THESIS] at the top. Below it, write one sentence naming each body argument and the M8 structure it focuses on (e.g., "[EV1]: the anaphora in line 7 — [ANAPHORA]+[PATHOS]"; "[EV2]: the periodic sentence in the closing stanza — [PERIODIC]+[LOGOS]").
  3. Place a sticky note at each of the two main IFX zones naming the M8 structure that zone represents and quoting the exact words from your text. Sign each sticky note.
  4. Take a snapshot of the full composition — step back far enough to show the Whiteboard, both IFX zones, and the sticky notes. Name it YourLastName_M8_5.5.
  5. Start a spatial recording (3–5 minutes). Walk through your composition and present your argument aloud as if your classmates are in the hall with you. You must cover:
    • Your [THESIS] — say it clearly at the start
    • Your first body argument: quote the evidence, name the M8 structure, explain its effect on the audience
    • Your second body argument: same — quote, name, explain
    • Your [CON]: why does your argument matter beyond this single text?
  6. Save the spatial recording from inside ENGAGE (use Save from the recording menu).
  7. Log out of the Session.
What to Submit (as part of Module 8 Project)
  • YourLastName_M8_5.5 — snapshot of the full capstone composition
  • Spatial recording — the 3–5 minute recording of your oral presentation (exported from ENGAGE or uploaded directly)
  • YourLastName_M8_Essay.docx — your revised written essay

All three items are submitted together to the Module 8 Project assignment in Canvas. Upload the snapshot and recording as additional files alongside your Word document.

Estimated Time

35 minutes — 10–15 min composing, 5 min Whiteboard + sticky notes, 5 min snapshot, 5 min recording, 5 min save and exit.

Facilitator Note

This is the module's capstone VR session. The most common difficulty is students who are reluctant to speak their argument aloud — they write fluently but go quiet in the recording. Encourage them before they enter: the recording is a first presentation draft, not a performance. If they can say their thesis out loud once, the rest will follow. Also check that they know how to save a spatial recording from the ENGAGE menu before they start.
