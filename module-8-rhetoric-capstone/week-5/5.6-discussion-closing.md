# Week 5 Discussion — Closing: The Essential Question
**Canvas Discussion | 15 points | Rubric: 15/8/0**

---

## Prompt

At the start of this module you answered the essential question for the first time: **What makes language powerful enough to move people to action?**

You have now spent five weeks studying the answer — not in theory, but through close attention to a text that actually did it. This discussion is your final answer.

**Initial post requirements (4 required elements):**

1. **Answer the essential question directly**, using the specific text you chose for your capstone essay. Your answer must name at least one M8 structure (use its tag) and explain precisely how that structure contributed to the language's power over its audience.

2. **Make an arguable claim** about what the most powerful single moment in your chosen text is and why. Quote the exact words. Name the M8 structure operating there and explain what it does to the audience.

3. **Use at least one M8 tag** — [ETHOS], [PATHOS], [LOGOS], [PERIODIC], [CUMUL], [ANAPHORA], [PARALLEL], [RQ], or [APOS] — correctly placed in your analysis.

4. **Close with a genuine open question** — something this module leaves unresolved for you. Not a summary question ("What did everyone think?") but a real question you do not know how to answer yet. What do you still wonder about how rhetoric works?

**Reply requirement:**

Reply to one classmate. Your reply must: (a) engage with the specific claim they made — do you agree with their reading of that moment? (b) address the open question they ended with — either offer a partial answer, sharpen the question, or explain why you find it genuinely difficult too. Replies that only say "great post" or restate what the classmate said will not receive credit.
